# Chapter 19a: Contract Negotiation (Witness Role)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH19, STATION LAYOVER]**
> *Location: Guild Station private meeting room*
> *Present: Avyanna (observing), Vesper, potential client, Cinnamon (recording)*
> *Teaching: Contract boundaries, saying no, client vetting*

---

[The meeting room is small. Soundproofed. One table, four chairs, a secure terminal, and the particular weight of money about to maybe change hands.]

[Vesper sits across from a potential client-human, middle-aged, wearing clothes that say "I have resources but I'm pretending I don't." The client's hands are folded on the table. Professional. Controlled. Hiding something.]

[Avyanna sits beside Vesper, one chair back. Observer. Witness. Learning.]

[Cinnamon manifests on the table's holo-projector-a small crystalline form, rotating slowly. Recording everything.]

---

Client: [measured] I need evidence retrieved from a corporate data vault. Financial records proving contractual violations. The contract in question is between my family's estate and Helios Extraction. They've been siphoning funds for three years. I need proof.

Vesper: [stylus poised over her datapad] Helios Extraction. Corporate entity. That means enhanced security, legal complications if caught, and potential retaliation.

Vesper: [meeting the client's eyes] Our fee for corporate targets starts at fifty thousand credits. Non-negotiable. Half up front, half on delivery.

Client: [not flinching] Acceptable.

---

Vesper: [continuing] Evidence retrieval only. We don't plant false data. We don't delete files. We don't alter records. We pull what exists and verify authenticity. Understood?

Client: [nodding] Understood.

Vesper: Timeline. How urgent is this?

Client: [hesitating] Ideally within two weeks. The estate litigation is scheduled for three weeks from now. I need the evidence before then.

Vesper: [noting it] Tight timeline. Increases risk. Adds ten thousand to the fee.

Client: [tense] That's-

Vesper: [interrupting, flat] Non-negotiable. Rush jobs mean less preparation time. Less preparation means higher risk. Higher risk means higher cost. You want it done right, or you want it done cheap?

---

[The client's jaw tightens. But they nod.]

Client: [forced] Fine. Sixty thousand total.

Vesper: [precise] Sixty thousand. Half up front, transferred to Guild escrow. We retrieve the evidence, verify it, deliver it to you. You confirm receipt, payment releases from escrow. Standard terms.

Vesper: [tapping her datapad] Now. Scope limitations. We will not retrieve evidence that incriminates *you*. We will not retrieve evidence that could be used to harm uninvolved third parties. We will not participate in corporate espionage beyond what you've specified.

Vesper: If we find evidence outside your stated scope, we notify you. You decide whether to accept it or have it deleted. Your choice. Our execution.

Client: [frowning] What if the evidence I need is... intertwined with other information?

Vesper: [measured] Define intertwined.

---

Client: [carefully] The financial records I need are in a database that also contains... personnel files. Medical records. Things that aren't relevant to my case but might be in the same data structure.

Vesper: [leaning back] Then we pull the financial records only. If the database structure makes selective extraction impossible, we pull everything, isolate your specified evidence, and delete the rest before delivery.

Vesper: [firm] We don't keep copies. We don't sell data to third parties. We don't leverage what we find for secondary profit. You're hiring us for retrieval, not for exploitation.

Client: [nodding slowly] And if I wanted... additional services?

[Vesper's expression doesn't change. But something in her posture sharpens.]

Vesper: [precise] Define additional services.

---

Client: [hesitant] If, in the process of retrieving my evidence, you found... other useful information. Information about Helios's other clients. Their operations. Vulnerabilities.

Client: [leaning forward slightly] I have contacts who would pay well for that kind of intelligence.

[Silence.]

[Avyanna watches Vesper's face. Neutral. Unreadable. But her stylus has stopped moving.]

Vesper: [after a long moment, very calm] No.

Client: [surprised] No?

Vesper: [flat] No. That's espionage. That's data brokering. That's not our service. If you want corporate intelligence, hire a different contractor.

---

Client: [trying again] I'm offering to pay-

Vesper: [interrupting] I don't care what you're offering. The answer is no. We retrieve evidence per contract. We don't steal unrelated data. We don't broker information. We don't participate in intelligence trafficking.

Vesper: [meeting their eyes] If that's what you need, this meeting is over.

[The client sits back. Recalculating.]

Client: [measured] I apologize. I misunderstood your services.

Vesper: [cold] You understood perfectly. You were testing boundaries. Now you know where they are.

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: client attempted to expand contract scope to include unauthorized intelligence gathering | VESPER DECLINED | RATIONALE: maintains Guild standing and legal protections | RECORDING: preserved for crew records>

[Vesper taps her datapad.]

Vesper: [businesslike] Let's return to the original contract. Financial records proving contractual violations by Helios Extraction. Sixty thousand credits. Two-week timeline. Evidence retrieval only, no alteration, no secondary use.

Vesper: [precise] Do you accept these terms as stated, with no additional requests?

Client: [after a pause] Yes. I accept.

Vesper: [pulling up a contract template] Then we proceed.

---

[She drafts the contract in real-time. Every term they discussed, formalized in plain language. No jargon. No loopholes. Just clear statements of what will be done, what won't be done, and what happens if either party deviates.]

[She turns the datapad toward the client.]

Vesper: Read it. Every word. If anything is unclear, ask. If you disagree with any term, say so now. Once you sign, this is binding.

[The client reads. Takes their time. Avyanna watches their eyes move across the screen, parsing legal language, looking for traps.]

[There are no traps. Just boundaries.]

---

Client: [looking up] This clause here. "Contractor reserves right to terminate contract if client requests actions outside stated scope." That's standard?

Vesper: [nodding] Yes. If you ask us to do something we didn't agree to, we can walk away. You get your deposit back minus expenses incurred. We get to not participate in things we don't ethically endorse.

Client: [frowning] What counts as "outside stated scope"?

Vesper: [precise] Anything not in the contract. You want us to plant evidence? Outside scope. You want us to harm someone? Outside scope. You want us to steal unrelated data for resale? Outside scope.

Vesper: [meeting their eyes] This clause protects both of us. You from being accused of requesting illegal actions you didn't request. Us from being coerced into things we didn't agree to.

---

[The client nods slowly. Continues reading.]

[After two minutes, they look up.]

Client: [calm] I'm satisfied. Where do I sign?

Vesper: [tapping the screen] Here. Biometric signature. Timestamp. Guild-certified binding contract.

[The client signs. The contract seals. Cinnamon chimes softly-recording complete.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// CONTRACT REGISTERED: 7851-K | CLIENT: [encrypted per Guild privacy protocols] | TERMS: evidence retrieval, Helios Extraction financial records, 60k credits, 14-day timeline | STATUS: active>

---

Vesper: [standing] We'll begin reconnaissance within 48 hours. You'll receive progress updates every three days via encrypted Guild channels. If complications arise, we notify you immediately and renegotiate terms if necessary.

Client: [standing] Understood. And if I need to contact you outside the scheduled updates?

Vesper: [handing them a data chip] Encrypted contact protocol. Use this channel only. Don't contact us through public Guild boards or station comms. Operational security.

Client: [taking the chip] Understood.

[They leave. The door hisses closed behind them.]

---

[Vesper sits back down. Lets out a slow breath.]

Vesper: [to Avyanna] What did you observe?

Avyanna: [careful] They tried to expand the contract. You said no.

Vesper: [nodding] What else?

Avyanna: [thinking] They tested you. To see if you'd compromise. If money would change your boundaries.

Vesper: [approving] Yes. That's client vetting. Some clients are honest. Some are testing. Some are actively trying to implicate you in illegal activity so they have leverage later.

Vesper: [tapping the table] That's why we use contracts. Why we document everything. Why we say no to requests that feel wrong.

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// ADDENDUM: client behavior flagged for monitoring | IF CLIENT ATTEMPTS FURTHER SCOPE EXPANSION, CONTRACT TERMINATION RECOMMENDED | VESPER'S BOUNDARY-SETTING: APPROPRIATE AND DOCUMENTED>

Avyanna: [hesitant] What if they'd offered more money? A lot more?

Vesper: [flat] Doesn't matter. I'm not for sale. My boundaries aren't negotiable. The moment I compromise once, every future client knows I can be bought. My reputation becomes "will bend rules for the right price." That's not a reputation worth having.

Vesper: [meeting her eyes] Integrity is the only currency that matters in this work. Money comes and goes. Reputation is permanent.

---

[Avyanna processes that. Boundaries as professional asset. Saying no as strength.]

Avyanna: [quiet] The mine never let us say no. To anything.

Vesper: [nodding] I know. That's why I'm teaching you now. Saying no is a skill. You practice it in low-stakes contexts until it becomes reflex.

Vesper: [standing] Today you watched me say no to sixty thousand credits plus unknown additional fees. That's not low-stakes. But it's necessary.

Vesper: [meeting her eyes] You'll be in negotiations eventually. Not today. Not next month. But eventually. And when that happens, I need you to know that saying no is always an option. Always.

---

[Avyanna stands. Her legs are steadier than they were when they entered the meeting.]

Avyanna: [careful] Thank you. For letting me watch.

Vesper: [slight smile] You're crew. You get to see how contracts work. The good ones, the questionable ones, and the ones we walk away from.

[They leave the meeting room. In the corridor, station life continues-people moving, dealing, negotiating, living in the spaces between extraction and autonomy.]

[Avyanna walks beside Vesper, thinking about boundaries. About saying no. About the client who tried to buy more than they'd contracted for, and the contractor who refused to sell.]

---

[Back on the ship, Avyanna finds Bubbles in the cargo bay.]

Avyanna: [to Bubbles] I watched a contract negotiation today.

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: noted | QUERY: what did you learn>

Avyanna: [sitting on a crate] That saying no is... powerful. That boundaries are worth more than money. That contracts protect both sides, not just one.

<BUBBLES// ASSESSMENT: correct observations | ADDENDUM: vesper is excellent at boundary-setting | YOU ARE LEARNING FROM GOOD MODELS | THIS IS IMPORTANT>

---

[Avyanna sits with Bubbles in the quiet cargo bay, processing.]

(Vesper said no. Lost money. Kept integrity. And the client accepted it.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(The warmth at the base of her skull steadies into a wordless impression: boundary, floor, power.)

[She stands. Tomorrow, there will be reconnaissance for the new contract. She'll watch that too. Learn more. Build her understanding of how contracts work, how boundaries hold, how saying no keeps you safer than saying yes to the wrong things.]

[And slowly, incrementally, she's learning that autonomy has a price, and that price is worth paying.]
