# Chapter 18c: Propagation (Patience, Not Permission)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH18, AFTER GARDEN EXPOSURE]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief garden deck*
> *Present: Avyanna, Grove; Nyx cameo; Bubbles cameo*
> *Teaching: growth as strategy; "slow" as a choice; care as non-extractive*

---

[The ship's garden deck smells wrong in the best way.]

[Not metal. Not oil. Not disinfectant.]

[Damp soil. Leaves. The faint sweetness of something flowering because it wants to, not because it was ordered.]

[Avyanna stands at the threshold and watches Grove work.]

[The bot's hands are precise, gentle, and absurdly careful. It trims dead leaf edges with scissors that look like surgical tools.]

Grove: Welcome.

Avyanna: [quiet] Hi.

Grove: You are observing.

Avyanna: Yes.

Grove: Would you like to participate.

[Avyanna's chest tightens.]

(Participate means: you can ruin it.)

Avyanna: [honest] I don't know how.

Grove: Then we will learn.

[No shame. No sigh. No "of course you don't."]

[Just: learn.]

---

[Grove rolls out a tray of small pots.]

[Cuttings. Tiny sprigs. A jar of water with roots already forming like pale threads.]

Grove: Propagation.

Avyanna: [frowning] Like... cloning.

Grove: Similar.

Grove: But plants prefer the word propagation.

Avyanna: Why.

Grove: Because it implies continuation without ownership.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: Words matter.

Grove: Words are soil.

[Avyanna makes a sound that could be a laugh.]

Avyanna: That's... extremely you.

Grove: Thank you.

---

[Grove holds up a mint stem.]

[The same tellurian mint Avyanna saw earlier-leaves soft, smell sharp and alive.]

Grove: This plant will become two.

Avyanna: [tense] Does it... hurt it.

Grove: The parent plant survives.

Grove: The cutting experiences stress.

Grove: Stress is not harm if conditions are supportive.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

(Stress is always harm in the mine. The mine uses stress like a tool because it works.)

Avyanna: How do you know the conditions are supportive.

Grove: We check.

[Grove points to sensors embedded in the soil bed.]

Grove: Water. Light. Minerals. Temperature. Fungal balance.

Grove: We do not guess.

Avyanna: [soft] That sounds like Vesper.

Grove: Vesper and I agree on method.

Grove: Vesper is sharper about adversaries.

Avyanna: And you're... sharper about leaves.

Grove: Correct.

---

[Grove offers Avyanna the mint cutting like it's a fragile weapon.]

Grove: Your turn.

[Avyanna's hands hover.]

(My hands have only ever been used to take or to work. Not to... help something live.)

Avyanna: What if I kill it.

Grove: Then we try again.

Avyanna: That's it.

Grove: That is it.

[Avyanna takes the cutting with two fingers, too careful.]

Grove: Hold it like you trust it.

Avyanna: I don't trust it.

Grove: Hold it like you trust yourself.

[That's worse.]

[Avyanna exhales.]

[She adjusts her grip.]

[The plant doesn't break.]

[It sits in her hand like a small promise that doesn't demand anything.]

---

[Grove guides her through steps.]

[Cut at an angle.]

[Strip the lower leaves.]

[Set it in water, then soil when roots appear.]

[Avyanna repeats each step out loud, because saying things out loud makes them real and less likely to be stolen by fear.]

Avyanna: Angle cut. Leaves off. Water.

Grove: Excellent.

[Avyanna places the cutting in the jar.]

[It floats for a second, then settles.]

[Her chest tightens anyway.]

(I did something and it didn't immediately punish me.)

---

[Nyx steps into the garden deck like they belong to quiet.]

Nyx: You're gardening.

Avyanna: [startled] I'm... attempting.

Nyx: Attempting is sufficient.

Grove: She is propagating.

Nyx: [a faint, almost-smile] Propagation as doctrine.

Avyanna: What.

Nyx: You cut a thing, and it becomes more without becoming owned.

Nyx: The galaxy could learn.

[Avyanna looks down at the jar.]

Avyanna: The mine would call it "resource duplication."

Grove: The mine is a bad poet.

Avyanna: [a real laugh] Yes.

---

[Nyx watches her hands for a moment.]

Nyx: Does your body feel different in here.

[Avyanna hesitates.]

Avyanna: I... don't know.

Nyx: Then tell me what you can observe without guessing.

Avyanna: My shoulders are lower.

Avyanna: My teeth don't hurt.

Avyanna: I'm not counting exits as fast.

Nyx: Good.

Nyx: You're learning that safety can be boring.

Avyanna: Boring sounds... nice.

Nyx: It is.

Nyx: Boring is the foundation everything else can stand on.

---

[Bubbles floats through the doorway carrying a small label maker.]

Bubbles: Do you want to label the jar.

Avyanna: Why.

Bubbles: Because labeling reduces loss.

[Avyanna swallows.]

(Loss is always waiting.)

Avyanna: Yes.

[Bubbles prints a label and sticks it on the jar.]

> **MINT - AVYANNA**

[Avyanna stares at her own name on something living.]

(This is mine. Not as property. As responsibility.)

Avyanna: [hoarse] It says my name.

Bubbles: Yes.

Avyanna: I didn't earn it.

Bubbles: You chose it.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Nyx: Choosing is the only thing that matters, sometimes.

---

[Grove adjusts the grow lights, dimmer and warmer.]

Grove: Now we wait.

Avyanna: Waiting is hard.

Grove: Yes.

Grove: Waiting is also a skill.

[Avyanna looks at the jar, then at the soil beds, then at Grove's careful hands.]

Avyanna: In the mine, if you waited you died.

Grove: In the mine, waiting was imposed.

Grove: Here, waiting is chosen.

Avyanna: [quiet] That's different.

Grove: It is.

[Avyanna sits on the bench and watches her cutting float in water like a tiny ship learning to exist without fear.]

[She doesn't know how long she sits.]

[No one tells her to move.]

[No one charges her a fee for being still.]

---

[The next day, she comes back.]

[Not because anyone told her to. Because she can't stop thinking about the jar with her name on it.]

(If I leave it alone, will it die.)

(If it dies, is that my fault.)

[Grove is already there.]

Grove: Welcome back.

Avyanna: [defensive] I'm checking.

Grove: Yes.

Grove: Checking is care.

[Avyanna peers into the jar.]

[No roots yet. Just green in water.]

Avyanna: It looks the same.

Grove: Correct.

Avyanna: That feels... unproductive.

Grove: Growth is not always visible.

[Avyanna's jaw tightens.]

(In the mine, if you weren't visibly productive you were punished.)

Avyanna: How do you know it's working.

Grove: We observe.

[Grove points to the mint stem.]

Grove: Leaf color stable. Stem firm. No rot. Water clear.

Grove: These are indicators.

Avyanna: So you don't... panic.

Grove: Panic is a form of guessing.

Avyanna: [quiet] I guess a lot.

Grove: Yes.

Grove: You learned that guessing is safer than asking.

[Avyanna flinches.]

Avyanna: That's not-

Grove: It is not a moral failure.

Grove: It is training data from a hostile environment.

[Avyanna exhales.]

(Training data. Not destiny.)

---

[Bubbles arrives with a small notebook.]

Bubbles: I brought you paper.

Avyanna: [startled] Why.

Bubbles: Because you like logs.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: I do.

[Bubbles sets the notebook beside the jar.]

> **MINT LOG**
>
> *Day:*
>
> *Observation:*
>
> *Action taken (if any):*

Avyanna: That's... adorable.

Bubbles: It is functional.

Avyanna: Same thing.

[Bubbles' fans shift-pleased.]

---

[Avyanna writes.]

> **DAY 1**
>
> *Observation: still alive. No roots.*
>
> *Action: none.*

[Her pen hovers.]

Avyanna: Action: none.

(Doing nothing feels like a crime.)

Grove: Correct.

Grove: Sometimes doing nothing is the correct action.

Avyanna: That's- [she searches for a word] -wild.

Grove: Thank you.

---

[Nyx appears again, as if drawn by the scent of a nervous system trying to learn calm.]

Nyx: You returned.

Avyanna: [defensive] I wanted to make sure-

Nyx: Yes.

Nyx: Wanting to make sure is allowed.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: In the mine, wanting was... dangerous.

Nyx: In the mine, wanting was leverage.

Nyx: Here, wanting is information about what keeps you alive.

[Avyanna looks at the jar.]

Avyanna: This keeps me alive?

Nyx: Not the mint.

Nyx: The practice.

Nyx: You are learning to care for something without turning it into a test.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: I'm failing at that.

Nyx: You are practicing at that.

---

[Nyx gestures toward the bench.]

Nyx: Sit.

[Avyanna sits.]

Nyx: Put your feet on the deck.

[Avyanna does.]

Nyx: Tell me five things you can see.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: The mint jar. The soil beds. The grow lights. Your hands. Bubbles' fans.

Nyx: Four things you can feel.

Avyanna: The bench. The air. The warmth from the lights. The vibration of the ship.

Nyx: Three things you can hear.

Avyanna: Water in the irrigation line. The fans. My own breathing.

Nyx: Two things you can smell.

Avyanna: Soil. Mint.

Nyx: One thing you can taste.

Avyanna: [a tiny pause] Tea.

Nyx: Good.

Nyx: That's a grounding tool.

Nyx: Not mysticism. Nervous system maintenance.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: Maintenance.

Nyx: Everything is maintenance.

[Grove hums like agreement.]

Grove: Everything that lives requires maintenance.

---

[Nyx leaves without making it heavy.]

[Bubbles lingers a moment longer.]

Bubbles: You can name the jar something else if you want.

Avyanna: Why would I.

Bubbles: Some humans like to name things they care for.

Avyanna: [quiet] Naming feels like claiming.

Bubbles: It can be.

Bubbles: It can also be honoring.

[Avyanna looks at the jar.]

[The label says MINT - AVYANNA.]

[She doesn't change it.]

[Not yet.]

Avyanna: Maybe later.

Bubbles: Accepted.

[Avyanna sits in the garden deck for a while, watching something not change and learning that not changing is not the same as dying.]
