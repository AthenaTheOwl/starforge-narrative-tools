# Chapter 16b: Emergency Drill

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH16, ROUTINE TRAINING]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief, full ship*
> *Present: All crew*
> *Purpose: Ship safety protocols, crew coordination under pressure, Avyanna's first drill*

---

[The alarm is loud. Too loud. A sound designed to cut through sleep, conversation, any layer of distraction. It fills the ship with urgency.]

[Avyanna bolts upright in her bunk. Her heart is already racing. Alarm = danger = threat = run.]

[But she's on the ship. Not in the mine. Different alarm. Different meaning.]

[Probably.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// ATTENTION ALL CREW: emergency drill initiated | SCENARIO: fire in cargo bay | THIS IS A DRILL | REPEAT: THIS IS A DRILL | REPORT TO DESIGNATED STATIONS PER PROTOCOL>

[A drill. Not real. But her body doesn't care. Her hands are shaking as she pulls on clothes and stumbles into the corridor.]

---

[The corridor is full of crew moving fast but not panicking. Rho heads toward engineering. Elia toward the cargo bay. Vesper toward the bridge. Everyone knows where they're supposed to be.]

[Avyanna doesn't.]

[She stands in the corridor, frozen. Where is she supposed to go? What is her station? No one told her-or they told her and she forgot, or she wasn't supposed to know yet because she's too new-]

<CINNAMON.EXE// AVYANNA LAGRANGE: report to galley | ROLE: muster point, head count, emergency supplies verification | NYRELLA SYRE IS YOUR DRILL SUPERVISOR>

[Galley. She can find the galley. She's been there a dozen times.]

[She runs.]

---

[The galley is organized chaos. Nyx is there, pulling emergency supply crates from storage. Waffle hovers near the door, tracking crew arrivals.]

Nyx: [calm, seeing Avyanna] Good. You're here. Help me verify the emergency med supplies. Count as I call them out.

[Nyx rattles off a list. Bandages. Coagulants. Painkillers. Antiseptics. Avyanna counts with shaking hands. Tries to focus. Tries to not think about the alarm still blaring overhead.]

Nyx: [watching her] Breathe. This is a drill. No one is hurt. We're just practicing.

Avyanna: [shaking] What if it was real?

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] Then we'd do exactly what we're doing now. But calmer, because we've practiced.

---

[They finish the supply count. Everything's accounted for. Waffle confirms all crew locations.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// STATUS UPDATE: all crew at designated stations | FIRE RESPONSE TEAM (elia, rho): cargo bay | EVACUATION COORDINATOR (vesper): bridge | MEDICAL RESPONSE (nyx): galley | TRAINEE (avyanna): galley with medical | NAVIGATION (jalen): maintaining engine readiness>

[The alarm cuts off. The silence is almost worse.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// DRILL SCENARIO UPDATE: fire has not been contained | STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY COMPROMISED IN CARGO BAY SECTION | CREW MUST MAKE DECISION: fight fire or evacuate ship>

Vesper: [over ship comms] All crew, report status.

---

[One by one, crew reports in.]

Elia: [over comms, slightly breathless] Fire response team assessing. Blaze is too large for onboard suppression. Recommend evacuation.

Jalen: [over comms] Engines ready for emergency undock if needed. Escape pods prepped.

Nyx: [over comms] Medical standing by. Emergency supplies verified. Ready to support evacuation.

Vesper: [over comms, decisive] Evacuation protocol initiated. All crew to escape pod stations. Avyanna, you're with Nyx in pod two. Move.

---

[Nyx grabs the emergency med kit. Avyanna follows them to the escape pod bay. The pod is small. Cramped. Designed for survival, not comfort.]

[They strap in. Waffle secures itself in a charging bracket. Around them: the sounds of other crew doing the same in other pods.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// POD STATUS: pod one (vesper, rho), pod two (nyx, avyanna, waffle), pod three (jalen, elia) | ALL PODS READY FOR LAUNCH | AWAITING FINAL EVACUATION COMMAND>

Vesper: [over comms] Final count. Everyone accounted for?

[Crew confirms. Everyone's in a pod. Everyone's ready to abandon ship if necessary.]

Vesper: [over comms] Good. On my mark, launch sequence would initiate. Mark.

---

[Nothing happens. Because this is a drill. In a real emergency, they'd launch. The ship would be lost. The crew would survive in pods until rescue or until they ran out of air, whichever came first.]

[But this is a drill. So they wait.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// DRILL COMPLETE | ALL CREW AT EVACUATION STATIONS IN: 4 minutes 17 seconds | TARGET TIME: under 5 minutes | ASSESSMENT: acceptable response time | CREW MAY DISEMBARK PODS>

[The all-clear sounds. Different tone. Less urgent. The sound of "you can stop running now."]

---

[Nyx unbuckles. Avyanna's hands are shaking too hard to manage the straps. Nyx reaches over, releases them gently.]

Nyx: [quiet] First drill?

Avyanna: [barely audible] Yes.

Nyx: [nodding] They're always disorienting. Your body doesn't know the difference between drill and real emergency until it's over.

[They exit the pod. Other crew is emerging too. Rho looks annoyed. Elia looks professionally satisfied. Vesper looks like she's already cataloguing performance metrics.]

---

[Vesper gathers everyone in the common room.]

Vesper: [addressing the group] Drill debrief. Response time: four minutes seventeen seconds. That's within acceptable range but not optimal. We need to hit under four minutes consistently.

Vesper: [pointing at a schematic on her datapad] Bottleneck was in corridor C. Rho and Elia both used it simultaneously. Slowed movement by eight seconds. Next drill, Rho uses corridor B.

Rho: [nodding] Noted.

Vesper: [to Avyanna] You responded appropriately for a first drill. Got to your station, followed instructions, evacuated without freezing. That's exactly what we need.

Avyanna: [shaking] I didn't know where to go at first.

Vesper: [firm] That's why Cinnamon directed you. You heard the direction and followed it. That's the correct response.

---

Elia: [to the group] Fire response assessment. If that had been real, we'd have lost the cargo bay and possibly the adjacent sections. Onboard suppression couldn't handle a blaze that size. Evacuation was correct call.

Jalen: [adding] Engines were ready in ninety seconds. If we'd needed emergency undock, we could have done it safely.

Nyx: [reporting] Medical supplies verified. Emergency kit is fully stocked. No issues.

Vesper: [satisfied] Good. Next drill is scheduled for two weeks from now. Scenario will be different. We'll iterate until response time is optimal and everyone knows their role without hesitation.

---

[The crew disperses. Avyanna sits on the couch, still shaking. Rho sits beside her.]

Rho: [gentle] First drill is always the worst. You don't know what's happening. You don't know if it's real. Your body just screams RUN and your brain has to catch up.

Avyanna: [quiet] I thought... for a second, I thought the ship was actually on fire.

Rho: [nodding] Yeah. That's the point. Drills are supposed to feel real. That's how we know we'll respond appropriately when it is real.

Avyanna: [looking at him] Has it ever been real?

Rho: [somber] Twice. Once we had a coolant leak. Once we had a power surge that started small fires in two sections. Both times, we evacuated. Both times, everyone survived because we'd drilled the procedures until they were reflex.

---

[Elia joins them, sitting on the armrest.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] You did well. Didn't freeze. Didn't panic past the point of function. Made it to your station. That's all we need from you right now.

Avyanna: [frustrated] I felt useless. Everyone else had important jobs. I just... counted bandages.

Elia: [flat] Counting bandages is important. If Nyx doesn't know what supplies are available, they can't treat injuries effectively. You verified critical information. That's not useless.

Nyx: [from across the room] Also, you were calm enough to count accurately. Under stress, people miscount. You didn't. That's reliability.

---

[Avyanna absorbs that. Her contribution mattered. Even if it felt small.]

Vesper: [addressing Avyanna specifically] Next drill, you'll have a different role. We rotate assignments so everyone knows multiple positions. Eventually, you'll be able to fill any role in an emergency. But for now, you learn one piece at a time.

Avyanna: [nodding] Understood.

Vesper: [standing] Rest of the day is light duty. Everyone's adrenaline is going to crash in the next hour. Take it easy. We resume normal operations tomorrow.

---

[Crew disperses again. Avyanna returns to her quarters. Lies on her bunk. Her heart is still pounding. The alarm is still echoing in her head.]

[But she didn't freeze. She got to her station. She did her job. She evacuated to the pod. She survived the drill.]

(This is how the crew survives real emergencies. By drilling until panic becomes procedure. By practicing until everyone knows exactly what to do when chaos happens.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(A warm pulse at the base of her skull-drill, practice, ready-like her body is learning a new language.)

[She closes her eyes. Tries to slow her heartbeat. Tomorrow, there will be normal operations. No alarms. No drills. Just ship life. Boring, safe ship life.]

[She's never been so grateful for boring.]

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// DRILL ASSESSMENT: crew response time adequate but requires improvement | SPECIFIC NOTES: avyanna (trainee) performed appropriately for first drill | RECOMMENDATION: additional drill cycles to improve familiarity and reduce response time | NEXT DRILL: scheduled 14 days>

<RHO PERSONAL LOG: "First drill is always brutal. She was shaking for an hour after. But she didn't freeze. She got to her station. She did what we needed. That's courage. That's what matters.">

<VESPER PERSONAL LOG: "Four minutes seventeen seconds. Acceptable for a crew with a new trainee. Next drill we'll hit four minutes flat. After that, three forty-five. We iterate until perfection, because perfection is what keeps everyone alive when the emergency is real.">

<ELIA PERSONAL LOG: "She asked if she was useless. I told her medical supply verification is critical. Because it is. She needs to know that every role matters. That there are no small jobs in an emergency. Everything is life or death. Everything counts.">

---

*End Scene*
