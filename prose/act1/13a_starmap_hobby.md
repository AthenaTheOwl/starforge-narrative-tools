# Chapter 13a: Starmap Hobby

> **[MICRO-SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH13, LIKELY SHIP'S EVENING]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief navigation alcove*
> *Present: Jalen, Avyanna*
> *Teaching: Constellation naming, pattern recognition, early shard-sense hints*

---

[Evening cycle. The Lumen Thief's navigation alcove is dim except for the star display-holographic projections overlaid on real-time sensor feeds. Jalen sits at the console, updating route calculations. Avyanna hovers near the doorway, uncertain whether she's intruding.]

Jalen: [not looking up] You can come in.

Avyanna: [stepping forward] I didn't want to interrupt.

Jalen: Navigation doesn't mind witnesses. Sit.

[She settles onto the jump seat beside the console. The star display rotates slowly-hundreds of labeled points, trade routes marked in faint lines, hazard zones shaded in warning colors.]

[The shard at the base of her skull pulses. Gentle. Curious.]

---

Avyanna: Earlier, when we were calibrating sensors... I said I didn't know the constellation names.

Jalen: [glancing at her] Most people don't. Not the real ones, anyway.

Avyanna: Real ones?

Jalen: [gesturing at the display] Every culture names the stars. Compact names, Guild names, local names. Sometimes the same cluster has six different designations depending on who's claiming it.

[They tap a control. The labels shift-same stars, different text.]

Jalen: See? One set is pure sector code. One set is corridor shorthand. One set is whatever the locals decided was worth remembering.

Avyanna: [leaning forward] Why so many names?

Jalen: Because names aren't just labels. They're claims. "I named this, therefore I know it, therefore it's mine."

---

[Avyanna studies the display. The shard pulses again-still gentle, but insistent. Like it's trying to show her something.]

Avyanna: [hesitant] Can you teach me? Not the politics. Just... I want handles. Ways to remember where things are.

Jalen: [considering her] Most people learn navigation for work. You asking because you're curious?

Avyanna: [quiet] In the mine, I never saw the stars. Didn't know there were this many. Didn't know they had names, or that names could be... complicated.

[She touches the edge of the display. Her fingers pass through a projected cluster.]

Avyanna: I want to understand the map. Even if the map lies sometimes.

Jalen: [after a beat] That's a good reason.

---

[They adjust the display. Zoom out. The galactic arm spreads before them-a sweep of light and void.]

Jalen: Start with navigation-practical handles. The ones that matter for orientation, not ownership.

[They point to a bright cluster near the center of the display.]

Jalen: Four stars in a rough square. No matter where you are in this arm, if you can find that shape, you can get your bearings: coreward, rimward, up, down.

Avyanna: [committing it to memory] Square. Coreward.

Jalen: Good. Now this one-

[They highlight another formation. Three stars in a tight triangle, with a dimmer fourth trailing behind.]

Jalen: Triangle and a tail. It points relative to the plane. Helps you orient vertical when sensors are compromised.

---

[They work through the practical constellations. Jalen is patient, methodical. Avyanna listens, focused, absorbing the patterns.]

[The shard continues to pulse. And then, as Jalen highlights a particular cluster near the edge of the display, something shifts.]

[The stars feel different. Not visually-she's still seeing the same holographic projection. But underneath the image, there's a... texture. A sense of presence. The cluster feels warm, somehow. Active. Like something is paying attention to it.]

Avyanna: [blinking] That one feels-

[She stops. Tries to articulate what she just perceived. Fails.]

Avyanna: [frustrated] Never mind. It's nothing.

Jalen: [sharp] Don't do that. If you noticed something, say it. Navigation is pattern recognition. Weird patterns are data.

---

[Avyanna hesitates. Then tries again.]

Avyanna: That cluster. The one you just highlighted. It feels... different. Not bad different. Just-

[She touches the base of her skull, where the shard sits.]

Avyanna: It feels like something's there. Not a ship. Not a station. Something else.

Jalen: [leaning back, studying her] Different how? Temperature? Pressure? Visual?

Avyanna: [struggling] None of those. It's like... like the difference between a room with people in it and a room that's empty. You can't see them, but you know.

[Jalen pulls up sensor data for that cluster. Scans it. Frowns.]

Jalen: Nothing on sensors. No anomalies. Standard stellar activity.

[They look at Avyanna. Thoughtful.]

Jalen: But you're not looking at sensors, are you?

Avyanna: [uncertain] I don't know what I'm looking at.

---

Jalen: [making a note on their tablet] Tell me when it happens again. If you feel something the sensors don't show, I want to know.

Avyanna: [defensive] I'm not making it up-

Jalen: [cutting her off] I know. But perception is subjective, and subjective data is still data. If you're sensing something I can't, that's worth tracking.

[They adjust the display again. Rotate through different star fields.]

Jalen: Look at these. Tell me if any of them feel "different."

---

[Avyanna focuses. The shard hums, cooperating now. It's interested in this game.]

[Most of the stars feel neutral. Background. Ordinary.]

[But one cluster-a dense formation near a nebula-has that same presence. That same sense of occupied attention.]

Avyanna: [pointing] That one.

[Jalen checks the coordinates. Makes another note.]

Jalen: Heavy Primordial signature. Low overlay clutter.

[They look at Avyanna with something like approval.]

Jalen: You're sensing Lattice topology. The shard's giving you perceptual texture that standard sensors don't pick up.

Avyanna: [slow] Is that... normal? For shard carriers?

Jalen: [dry] I don't know. You're the first one I've met.

---

[They continue the lesson. Jalen teaches her the navigation-practical constellations. Avyanna learns the names, commits the patterns to memory.]

[But underneath the lesson, she's learning something else: the shard isn't just communicating with her. It's changing how she perceives reality. Giving her information that doesn't fit into visual, auditory, or tactile categories.]

[The galaxy has texture now. Presence. Topology.]

[And she doesn't know yet if that's a gift or a warning.]

---

[Later, when Avyanna leaves the alcove, Jalen writes a note in their personal log:]

> *Trainee reports perceptual anomalies correlated with heavy Primordial signatures. Consistent with Nyx's stated shard characteristics, but timeline unclear. Worth monitoring. Flagging for Nyx.*

[And then, in smaller text beneath:]

> *She asked good questions. Wanted handles, not propaganda. Noticed when names were claims.*
>
> *Reminds me of someone. In a good way.*

---

*End Micro-Scene*
