# Chapter 09a: Medical Autonomy

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH09, FIRST WEEK ABOARD]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief medical bay*
> *Present: Avyanna, Nyx*
> *Teaching: Consent vocabulary, body autonomy, medical agency*

---

[The medical bay smells like antiseptic and something gentler underneath-herbs, maybe, or the faint residue of someone's careful attention.]

[Avyanna sits on the exam table with her hands folded in her lap. The paper under her crinkles when she shifts. Every muscle is tensed for inspection.]

[Nyx stands near the counter, not looming, organizing supplies with deliberate slowness. Giving Avyanna time to acclimate.]

Nyx: This is a routine check. Post-rescue protocol. I need to look at your general health, document any injuries, make sure nothing needs immediate treatment.

[Avyanna nods automatically. The motion is reflex.]

Nyx: [turning to face her] That wasn't a question. I'm about to explain what I need to do, and you're going to tell me if it's acceptable.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: I... what?

Nyx: [measured] Medical exams require consent. Not just once, at the beginning. Continuously. I'm going to walk you through what I need to do, and at every step, you can say yes, no, or "I need more information."

---

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: [careful] In the mine, medical was... mandatory.

Nyx: I know.

Nyx: Here, it's not.

[They pull a stool closer, sit so they're below her eye level. Less threatening. More human.]

Nyx: Here's what I need to do today. I need to check your reflexes, listen to your heart and lungs, look at any visible injuries or scars, and take a blood sample for baseline health markers.

Nyx: None of that requires you to be naked. None of it requires you to be unconscious. All of it can be paused or stopped at any time.

[Avyanna's hands tighten in her lap.]

Avyanna: And if I say no?

Nyx: Then we don't do it.

Avyanna: Even if it's... medically necessary?

Nyx: [precise] Medical necessity doesn't override autonomy. If you refuse, I document the refusal, I explain the risks, and I respect your choice.

---

[The words land strange. Like tools she doesn't know how to hold yet.]

Avyanna: [slow] What if I don't know if I want something?

Nyx: Then you say: "I need more information." Or: "Can you explain that again?" Or: "I want to think about it."

[Nyx pulls a datapad closer, shows Avyanna a form.]

Nyx: See this? Consent documentation. Before I do anything, I note what I'm planning to do, and whether you agreed. If you change your mind halfway through, I stop and update the record.

[Avyanna reads the form. It's written in plain language, not medical jargon. Each procedure has a checkbox: CONSENT GIVEN / CONSENT REFUSED / DEFERRED.]

Avyanna: [quiet] You're asking permission. Every time.

Nyx: Yes.

Nyx: Because your body belongs to you. Not to me. Not to medical protocol. Not to the ship's operational needs.

Nyx: You.

---

[Avyanna's vision blurs for a second. She blinks it away.]

Avyanna: [barely audible] Okay.

Nyx: [gentle] Okay you understand, or okay you consent to the exam?

Avyanna: [swallowing] Both. I think. Can you... can you tell me again what you're going to do?

Nyx: [nodding] Yes.

---

[Nyx walks through it again. Slower this time.]

Nyx: First, I'm going to check your reflexes. That means tapping your knees with this small hammer, and watching your legs respond. It doesn't hurt. It tells me if your nervous system is functioning normally.

Nyx: I'll need to touch your knees. Is that acceptable?

[Avyanna stares at the hammer. It's small. Harmless. The question feels enormous anyway.]

Avyanna: [nodding] Yes.

Nyx: Say it out loud. Practice the words.

Avyanna: [hesitant] Yes. You can... touch my knees. To check reflexes.

Nyx: [approving] Good. If at any point you need me to stop, you say "stop" or "wait" or "I need a minute." I will stop immediately. No questions, no judgment, no consequences.

---

[Nyx performs the reflex test. Quick, professional, exactly as described. Avyanna's legs jerk in response. The motion is automatic, biological, outside her control. It should feel violating. Instead, it feels... informative.]

Nyx: [making notes] Normal response. Next: heart and lungs. I need to listen with a stethoscope. It'll be cold at first. I'll place it on your chest, over your shirt, and ask you to breathe deeply.

Nyx: I'll need to touch your upper back for the lung check. Is that acceptable?

[Avyanna's breath catches. Back = vulnerable. Back = can't see what's happening.]

Avyanna: [tight] Can I... can I see what you're doing? If you're behind me?

Nyx: [immediately] Yes. We can do this in front of a mirror, or I can narrate every move before I make it, or we can skip the back exam and just do the front.

---

[Avyanna processes this. Options. She has options.]

Avyanna: Narrate. Tell me before you move.

Nyx: [standing, moving slowly] I'm standing up now. Walking to your left side. I'm going to place my hand on your shoulder-your left shoulder-to steady myself. Is that acceptable?

Avyanna: [voice shaking] Yes.

[Nyx's hand settles on her shoulder. Warm. Steady. Not gripping. Just resting.]

Nyx: I'm lifting the stethoscope now. I'm going to place it on your back, between your shoulder blades. You'll feel cold metal, then warmth as it adjusts to your body temperature. Ready?

Avyanna: [barely] Yes.

[The stethoscope touches her back. Cold, as promised. Then warming. Nyx's other hand stays on her shoulder, grounding.]

Nyx: Breathe in deeply. Hold. Release.

[Avyanna obeys. Her lungs expand, filling with recycled ship air that somehow feels cleaner than anything in the Kennel.]

Nyx: Good. Again. In. Hold. Out.

---

[They move through the exam. Every step narrated. Every touch announced. Avyanna's heart rate stays elevated-fear, not danger-but she doesn't flee. Doesn't freeze.]

[Nyx finishes the lung check, steps back into view.]

Nyx: That's done. Your lungs sound clear. No scarring I can detect, though I'd want imaging to be certain. We can schedule that later if you want.

Avyanna: [exhaling] Later. Not today.

Nyx: [noting it] Understood. Next is the blood draw. This one is more invasive. I need to insert a needle into your arm, draw a small vial of blood, then bandage the site.

Nyx: It will hurt-not much, but it will hurt. It will take about thirty seconds. You can look away, or watch, or ask me to count down.

---

[Avyanna's hands clench.]

Avyanna: [quiet] In the mine, they didn't... they didn't ask.

Nyx: [steady] I know.

Avyanna: They just... held you down if you fought.

Nyx: [after a beat] That is not medicine. That is violence with a medical aesthetic.

[The sentence lands like a hammer.]

Nyx: You do not have to consent to this. We can defer it. We can do it another day, or never. Your health will not catastrophically suffer from skipping a baseline blood panel.

[Avyanna sits with that. The needle is terrifying. But the choice-the choice is hers.]

Avyanna: [slow] I want to do it. But I need... I need you to tell me when. Count down. So I know when it's coming.

Nyx: [nodding] I can do that.

---

[Nyx preps the site. Alcohol swab, cold and clinical. Tourniquet around Avyanna's upper arm. The needle, sterile and waiting.]

Nyx: I'm going to count from three. On one, the needle goes in. Ready?

Avyanna: [gripping the edge of the table] Ready.

Nyx: Three.

Nyx: Two.

Nyx: One.

[The needle slides in. Sharp. Brief. Avyanna gasps but doesn't pull away.]

Nyx: [calm] Needle's in. Ten seconds. You're doing well.

[Avyanna watches the vial fill. Dark red. Her blood. Her body giving information, not being taken from.]

Nyx: Five seconds. Almost done.

[The vial fills. Nyx withdraws the needle smoothly, presses gauze to the site.]

Nyx: Done. Pressure here for thirty seconds, then I'll bandage it.

---

[Avyanna presses the gauze. Her hands are shaking. Her eyes are wet. But she's upright. Present. Not dissociated.]

Avyanna: [unsteady] I did it.

Nyx: [quiet] You did.

Avyanna: I said yes. And I could have said no.

Nyx: [applying the bandage] Yes. That's the point.

[They step back, give Avyanna space.]

Nyx: That's the exam done. You consented to everything. You stayed present. That's significant progress.

---

[Avyanna looks at the bandage on her arm. Small. Ordinary. Proof of something she chose.]

Avyanna: [careful] What happens next time? If there's a next time?

Nyx: Same process. I explain, you consent or refuse, we proceed or defer. Every time.

Avyanna: Even in an emergency?

Nyx: [precise] In an emergency, I'll explain as fast as I can. If you're unconscious, I'll do what's necessary to keep you alive, then explain after. But if you're conscious and capable of communication, I ask first.

Nyx: Even in an emergency, autonomy matters.

---

[Avyanna nods. The motion is less reflex, more genuine agreement.]

[Nyx pulls up a chair, sits at Avyanna's level again.]

Nyx: Now I'm going to teach you the vocabulary. The words you use to maintain consent in medical contexts and beyond.

[They pull up a document on the datapad. A list. Simple. Powerful.]

---

**CONSENT VOCABULARY**

*Phrases for maintaining autonomy:*

- **"I need more information."** Use this when you don't understand what's being proposed.
- **"Can you explain that again?"** Use this when you need clarity.
- **"I need a minute to think."** Use this when you need time to process.
- **"I consent to X, but not Y."** Use this when you're comfortable with part of a proposal, but not all of it.
- **"I want to stop."** Use this when you need to end an interaction immediately.
- **"I'm not comfortable with that."** Use this when something feels wrong, even if you can't articulate why.
- **"Can we do this differently?"** Use this when you want to negotiate method or approach.
- **"Not today. Ask me again later."** Use this when you're not ready now, but might be later.

---

[Avyanna reads the list twice.]

Avyanna: [quiet] You're giving me... scripts.

Nyx: Yes.

Nyx: In the mine, you had no language for refusal. They took that from you deliberately. Stripped you of the words that create boundaries.

Nyx: This is me giving them back.

---

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: What if I use them and people get angry?

Nyx: [flat] Then they're not safe people.

Nyx: Anger at your boundaries is a tell. It means they were counting on your compliance, not inviting your participation.

[They lean forward slightly.]

Nyx: Here, on this ship, if you use any of those phrases, the response will be: "Understood" or "Thank you for telling me" or "How can I help?"

Nyx: If anyone responds with anger, you tell me. Or tell Vesper. Or tell Elisira. Or tell any of the AI citizens. And we handle it.

---

[Avyanna stares at the list. Memorizing. Internalizing.]

Avyanna: [slow] Can we... practice? The phrases?

Nyx: [approving] Yes. That's exactly what we should do.

[Nyx stands, creates a scenario.]

Nyx: I'm going to ask you to do something. You're going to practice refusing. Ready?

Avyanna: [nervous] Okay.

Nyx: [neutral tone] Avyanna, I need you to take off your shirt so I can examine your ribs.

---

[Avyanna's breath catches. The scenario is hypothetical. Her panic is real.]

Avyanna: [struggling] I... I don't...

Nyx: [gentle] Try one of the phrases. Any of them.

Avyanna: [forcing it out] I'm not comfortable with that.

Nyx: [immediately, warmly] Understood. We can do the rib exam over your shirt, or we can defer it entirely. What would you prefer?

[Avyanna blinks. The response is... safe. Respectful. No punishment.]

Avyanna: [shakily] Over my shirt.

Nyx: [nodding] Good. Let's try another scenario.

---

[They practice. Ten scenarios. Each one designed to give Avyanna the muscle memory of refusal without consequence.]

**Scenario: Medication**
- Nyx: I'm prescribing a painkiller. You'll take two pills, twice a day.
- Avyanna: [using the script] Can you explain what it does?
- Nyx: It reduces inflammation and blocks pain signals. Side effects include drowsiness and nausea.
- Avyanna: I don't want to be drowsy. Can we do something else?
- Nyx: Yes. We can try a lower dose, or a different medication, or non-pharmaceutical pain management.

**Scenario: Physical Contact**
- Nyx: I need to check your shoulder mobility. I'm going to lift your arm.
- Avyanna: [using the script] I need you to tell me before you touch me.
- Nyx: I'm placing my hand on your shoulder now. I'm going to lift slowly. Tell me if it hurts.

**Scenario: Deferral**
- Nyx: I'd like to schedule a follow-up exam for next week.
- Avyanna: [using the script] Not next week. Ask me again in two weeks.
- Nyx: Understood. I'll send a reminder in two weeks, and you can confirm or defer again.

---

[After ten rounds, Avyanna is exhausted. But something in her posture has shifted. Less collapsed. More present.]

Nyx: [standing] That's enough for today. You did well.

Avyanna: [quiet] I said no. Ten times. And you didn't... punish me.

Nyx: [steady] I never will.

Nyx: Consent isn't a favor I grant you. It's a right you already have. I'm just teaching you the language to exercise it.

---

[Avyanna slides off the exam table. Her legs are shaky. Her mind is clearer than it's been in weeks.]

Avyanna: [turning at the door] Nyx?

Nyx: [looking up] Yes?

Avyanna: [careful] Thank you. For asking.

Nyx: [quietly] You're welcome. And Avyanna?

[Avyanna waits.]

Nyx: I had to relearn this too. After the Sutured Houses. It took time. Be patient with yourself.

---

[Avyanna nods. Leaves. The door closes behind her with a soft hiss.]

[In the corridor, she leans against the bulkhead and breathes.]

(I said no. Ten times. And nothing bad happened.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm. Not warning. Just... witnessing.]

(At the base of her skull, warmth gathers-an impression she can only translate after: boundary, floor, yours.)

[She pushes off the wall and walks back to her quarters. Her arm aches where the needle went in. The bandage is bright against her skin.]

[Proof. Choice. Hers.]
