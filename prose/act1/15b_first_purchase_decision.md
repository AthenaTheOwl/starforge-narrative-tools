# Chapter 15b: First Purchase Decision (Earned Money)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH15b after first contract payment]**
> *Location: Mid-tier station market*
> *Present: Avyanna, Bubbles*
> *Purpose: Learning to spend money autonomously, distinguishing wants from needs*

---

[Avyanna has two hundred credits in her account. Money she earned. Not given. Not stolen. Earned through work.]

[The concept is still strange. The mine took everything and gave nothing. Here, she worked one contract and has enough money to buy... things. Whatever things she wants. Or needs. Or both.]

[That's the problem. She doesn't know the difference yet.]

[She stands in the market district of Station Kael-7 with Bubbles hovering beside her. She's supposed to be buying things. Vesper said: "You have money. You need supplies. Go buy them. It's good practice."]

[But what does she need? And what does she want? And how does she tell them apart?]

---

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee has been stationary for 4.7 minutes | QUERY: do you require assistance>

Avyanna: [uncertain] I don't know what to buy.

<BUBBLES// CLARIFICATION REQUIRED: you don't know what to buy because you lack information about available goods, or because you are uncertain of your preferences>

Avyanna: [frustrated] Both. Neither. I just... I have money and I don't know what to do with it.

<BUBBLES// ACKNOWLEDGED: first autonomous purchase decision is difficult | SUGGESTION: start with needs, then proceed to wants | NEEDS = items required for survival/function | WANTS = items that increase comfort/pleasure but are not strictly necessary>

---

[Avyanna processes that. Needs first. She can do needs. Needs are objective.]

Avyanna: [to Bubbles] What do I need?

<BUBBLES// ASSESSMENT: basic hygiene supplies running low | CLOTHING: adequate but minimal variety | NUTRITION: ship provides meals but personal snack stores are recommended for autonomy | COMFORT ITEMS: worry stone acquired, other grounding tools may be beneficial>

[Bubbles projects a small list in the air.]

<BUBBLES// RECOMMENDED PURCHASES:
- Hygiene supplies (soap, cleaning cloths, personal care items): ~15 credits
- Additional clothing (second set for rotation): ~30 credits
- Personal food storage (non-perishable snacks): ~20 credits
- Grounding tools (if desired): ~10 credits
TOTAL: ~75 credits | REMAINING BUDGET: 125 credits for discretionary use>

---

[Avyanna reads the list. Seventy-five credits for needs. One hundred twenty-five credits left over. For wants.]

[The concept of having money left over after needs feels impossible.]

Avyanna: [quiet] What do I do with the rest?

<BUBBLES// OPTIONS:
1. Save for future needs
2. Purchase comfort/pleasure items
3. Combination of both
ALL OPTIONS ARE VALID | YOUR CHOICE>

[Her choice. Everything here is her choice.]

[She starts with needs. Easier to think about.]

---

**Hygiene Supplies**

[The vendor has twenty different kinds of soap. TWENTY. Avyanna stares at them like they're a threat.]

Vendor: [helpful] Looking for anything specific?

Avyanna: [overwhelmed] Just... soap.

Vendor: [nodding] Bar or liquid? Scented or unscented? Antibacterial or moisturizing?

[Avyanna's brain shorts out. Too many options. Too many variables.]

<BUBBLES// ASSISTANCE: recommend unscented, moisturizing, bar form | REASONING: less likely to trigger scent-based trauma memories, easy to store, station-standard quality>

Avyanna: [to vendor] That one. The unscented moisturizing bar.

Vendor: [handing it to her] Good choice. Anything else?

---

[She adds cleaning cloths. Toothpaste. Things that are universal. Things that don't require decisions beyond "do I need this? yes."]

[Total: fourteen credits. She pays. The transaction is simple. No haggling. No games. Just goods exchanged for money at the stated price.]

[One down. Three to go.]

**Clothing**

[The clothing stalls are worse. So many colors. So many styles. So many ways to be wrong.]

[She gravitates toward gray. Safe. Familiar. The mine color. But then she remembers-she doesn't have to wear gray anymore. Gray isn't mandatory. Gray is just habit.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee selecting gray garments | QUERY: is this preference or default trauma response>

Avyanna: [pausing] I don't know.

<BUBBLES// SUGGESTION: test alternative | SELECT ONE ITEM IN DIFFERENT COLOR | COMPARE EMOTIONAL RESPONSE | DETERMINE IF GRAY IS PREFERENCE OR SAFETY BEHAVIOR>

---

[Avyanna picks up a gray shirt. Then a blue one. The blue reminds her of Jalen's nav displays. Of the first coffee ritual. Of sky that isn't filtered through mine dust.]

[She holds both. Compares. The gray feels safe. The blue feels like... possibility?]

Vendor: [nearby] Try them on if you want. Changing room's open.

[Avyanna tries the blue shirt. It fits. The color is bright against her skin in a way that feels foreign but not wrong.]

[She buys both. Gray for safe days. Blue for days when she wants to remember that she's not in the mine anymore.]

[Cost: thirty-two credits. Within budget.]

---

**Personal Food Storage**

[The food vendors sell pre-packaged snacks. Protein bars. Dried fruit. Nut mixes. Things that keep without refrigeration. Things you can eat when ship meals don't align with hunger.]

[Avyanna picks items based on Bubbles' nutritional recommendations and her own tentative preferences. She's learning that she likes sweet things. That salt is sometimes too much. That texture matters as much as taste.]

[She fills a small bag with selections. Enough for two weeks if she rations. Or one week if she doesn't.]

[Cost: eighteen credits.]

---

**Grounding Tools**

[This is the hard one. This is wants, not needs. She already has the worry stone. She doesn't *need* more tools.]

[But Nyx gave her the weighted stone and it helped. Maybe other things would help too.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee hesitating at grounding tools vendor | REMINDER: comfort items are not frivolous | MENTAL HEALTH TOOLS ARE VALID PURCHASES | YOU ARE ALLOWED TO INVEST IN YOUR OWN WELL-BEING>

[Avyanna approaches the vendor. Looks at the options. Fidget rings. Textured squares. Small puzzles. Things designed to give restless hands something to do.]

[She picks up a smooth metal ring. It spins. The motion is satisfying. Mindless. Something to do when her thoughts get too loud.]

Vendor: [knowledgeable] That's a spinner ring. Good for anxiety. Fidget without being disruptive.

Avyanna: [testing it] How much?

Vendor: Eight credits.

[Eight credits. For something she doesn't strictly need. For comfort. For mental health. For herself.]

Avyanna: [decision made] I'll take it.

---

[She pays. The ring is hers. She immediately starts spinning it on her finger. The motion is soothing.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee purchased non-essential comfort item autonomously | ASSESSMENT: developing ability to identify and meet own comfort needs | THIS IS PROGRESS>

[Total spent on needs: sixty-four credits. One hundred thirty-six credits remaining.]

[Avyanna stands in the market with her purchases and more money than she expected to have left. The money is hers. She can save it. She can spend it. She can do whatever she wants with it.]

[The freedom is terrifying and exhilarating in equal measure.]

---

**Discretionary Purchase**

[She walks past vendors selling things she doesn't understand. Art. Music chips. Decorative objects. Things that exist for pleasure, not function.]

[She stops at a stall selling plants. Real plants. Tiny ones, meant for small spaces. Hardy varieties that can survive ship environments.]

[There's a small succulent. Dark green with purple edges. Sturdy. Low-maintenance. The vendor says it needs water once a week and tolerates low light.]

[Avyanna stares at it.]

<BUBBLES// QUERY: interest in plant acquisition?>

Avyanna: [quiet] I've never had a plant before.

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: plants provide psychological benefits | CARING FOR LIVING THING DEVELOPS NURTURING CAPACITY | ALSO: AESTHETIC BENEFIT | ALSO: PLANTS PRODUCE OXYGEN (MINIMAL BUT NON-ZERO)>

---

Vendor: [to Avyanna] First plant?

Avyanna: [nodding] Yes.

Vendor: [gentle] That's a good starter. Very hard to kill. Forgiving of mistakes. If you forget to water it, it'll recover. If you overwater it, just let it dry out. It wants to survive.

[The phrase hits different. "It wants to survive."]

[She buys the plant. Twenty credits. More than the spinner ring. More than the soap. But it's alive. It's something that exists with her, not just for her.]

[Cost: twenty credits.]

---

[She returns to the ship with Bubbles carrying her bags. The plant sits carefully in her hands. Fragile. Trusting. Dependent on her not to kill it through neglect or overcare.]

[In her quarters, she sets the plant on the small desk. Finds a spot where it gets indirect light from the corridor. Not too much. Not too little.]

[She sits on her bunk and looks at it. A living thing. In her space. Proof that she can care for something. That she has enough stability to commit to keeping another living thing alive.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee purchased needs (64 credits) and wants (20 credits) | SAVED: 116 credits | FINANCIAL AUTONOMY: demonstrated | EMOTIONAL REGULATION TOOLS: acquired | LIVING THING: acquired | ASSESSMENT: successful first autonomous purchase experience>

---

[Avyanna spins the ring on her finger. Looks at the plant. Smells the new soap. Sees the blue shirt hanging in her small closet next to the gray one.]

(I earned money. I bought things. I chose what to buy. I have money left over. I have a plant.)

[The concepts are still strange. But they're real. Documented. Hers.]

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(Warmth gathers at the base of her skull, like a stamp: earned, chose, kept.)

[She waters the plant. Just a little. The vendor said it wants to survive. So does she. Maybe they can figure it out together.]

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// PURCHASE LOG: trainee spent 84 of 200 credits | CATEGORIES: hygiene, clothing, food, comfort items, living plant | FINANCIAL DECISIONS: appropriate and balanced | REMAINING FUNDS: saved for future use | ASSESSMENT: trainee developing economic autonomy and purchasing confidence>

<BUBBLES// PERSONAL NOTE: she bought a plant | THIS IS SIGNIFICANT | PLANTS REQUIRE ONGOING CARE AND COMMITMENT | SHE IS BUILDING CAPACITY FOR SUSTAINED RESPONSIBILITY | ALSO THE PLANT IS VERY CUTE | I AM PLEASED>

---

*End Scene*
