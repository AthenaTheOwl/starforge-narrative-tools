# Chapter 17: Rule of Locality

> **LUMEN THIEF - LATTICE FUNDAMENTALS (RESTRICTED):**
> *Subject: Primordial Mechanics*
> *Instructor: Nyx Halden*
> *Trainee: Avyanna Lagrange*
> *Summary: Power is not the problem. Scale is the problem.*
> *Rule 1: You must be present, or have an established anchor.*
> *Rule 2: Throughput is limited. This is a feature, not a bug.*
> *Rule 3: The conservation law is not what they told you.*
> *Addendum: The shard in you isn't normal. Remember that.*

---

[The meditation chamber is the quietest room on the ship.]

[Avyanna has been here before-passing through, curious, never lingering. Now she sits cross-legged on a cushion that smells faintly of old incense, watching Nyx arrange candles in patterns that make her eyes want to slide away.]

[The shard at the base of her skull hums. Not pain-attention. Something is happening that it wants to witness.]

Nyx: [still arranging] The first thing you need to understand is that everything you've heard about magic is propaganda.

Avyanna: Everything?

Nyx: The parts that matter.

---

[Nyx finishes the candle arrangement. Sits across from Avyanna. Their pale eyes are focused, intent-the look of someone about to share something dangerous.]

Nyx: Tell me what you know.

Avyanna: [uncertain] Magic has costs. You pay with memory, or life, or... something. The more power you use, the more it takes from you.

Nyx: [nodding] That's the standard education. What else?

Avyanna: The Lattice is infrastructure. It connects systems. People who can use it are regulated because... because they're dangerous.

Nyx: Dangerous to whom?

[The question hangs. Avyanna doesn't have an answer.]

Nyx: The official version says magic is dangerous to *everyone*. That unchecked practitioners destabilize reality. That the Writbound exist to protect the galaxy from uncontrolled power.

[They lean forward.]

Nyx: The official version is correct about the danger. It's lying about the source.

---

[The candles flicker. Avyanna is acutely aware of the shard-warmer now, more present. Listening.]

Nyx: There are two systems of magic operating in this galaxy. Two Lattices, layered on top of each other.

Avyanna: Two?

Nyx: The Primordial Lattice is the original. It's the substrate-the way reality actually works. It's slow, local, sustainable. Bounded by presence and consent.

[They gesture at the candle arrangement.]

Nyx: The Synthetic Lattice is the overlay. It was built on top of the Primordial-designed to scale, to extract, to operate without the original constraints. It's what most people learn. It's what the Writbound enforce.

Avyanna: What's the difference?

Nyx: [quietly] The difference is who pays.

---

[Nyx picks up two of the candles. Holds them so Avyanna can see both flames-small, steady, ordinary.]

Nyx: Watch carefully.

[They focus on the left candle. The flame brightens, grows, pulses with new energy. But the right candle's flame dims at the same moment-flickering, struggling, like something is pulling its light away.]

Avyanna: [realizing] The second candle is feeding the first.

Nyx: [nodding] That's Synthetic extraction. One source pulls from another. The bright flame doesn't pay-it makes the dim flame pay instead.

[They set both candles down. The flames return to normal, equal again.]

Nyx: Now watch Primordial.

[They focus on a single candle. Nothing happens for a long moment. Then, slowly, the flame brightens-but only slightly. And nothing else dims to compensate.]

Avyanna: But it's slower. And smaller.

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] Yes. Because Primordial magic *asks*. The candle can say no. The flame can only grow as much as the local environment is willing to give. No theft. No externalization.

---

Nyx: Primordial magic is locally bounded. You must be *present*-physically, emotionally, intentionally. You can't fire it and forget. You can't scale it to industrial levels. You can't extract more than the local region can regenerate.

[They gesture at the candle arrangement.]

Nyx: Synthetic magic removed those constraints. It lets you act at distance. It lets you scale indefinitely. It lets you draw power from anywhere, as long as you're willing to pay the cost.

Avyanna: The costs you mentioned. Memory. Life.

Nyx: [shaking their head] That's where the lie lives.

---

[The shard flares. Not pain-something sharper. Interest. Recognition.]

Nyx: The costs are real. But they're not *natural*. The Synthetic overlay doesn't just use power-it *extracts* it. And the extraction has to come from somewhere.

Avyanna: From where?

Nyx: From people. From regions. From the Lattice itself. Every time someone uses Synthetic magic, the cost is externalized. Someone else pays-usually people without the power to refuse. Debtor labor. Extraction facilities. The "short lives" you saw in the mine.

[Their voice is steady, but something underneath trembles.]

Nyx: The conservation law isn't physics. It's theft. The energy has to come from somewhere, and the Synthetic overlay was designed to make sure it comes from people who can't say no.

---

[Avyanna's chest is tight. The shard hums-verification, maybe. Or just the weight of truth landing.]

Avyanna: The mine. The workers who aged too fast. That was...

Nyx: Extraction damage. Yes. The facility was drawing from everyone inside it. Every worker's life, compressed. Stolen. Fed into systems they never understood.

[The candle nearest Nyx gutters.]

Nyx: That's what "short lives" means. Not natural wear. Not hard work. Deliberate, systematic extraction of vitality to power things that didn't need to cost that much.

[Avyanna is silent for a long moment. The candles flicker. The meditation chamber feels smaller suddenly-the walls pressing in.]

Avyanna: Why doesn't everyone know this?

Nyx: Because the people who benefit from extraction also control the education. The Compact teaches the conservation law as physics. The Writbound enforce it as law. Anyone who suggests alternatives is labeled a heretic, a terrorist, a threat to stability.

[They gesture at the candles again.]

Nyx: The Primordial way works. It just doesn't scale the way powerful people want. You can't build empires on it. You can't extract wealth from populations. You can't create immortal elites who live forever while others die young.

Avyanna: [quiet] Immortal?

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] The extraction has to go somewhere. The life stolen from workers and regions and entire systems-it feeds something. Someone. We call them Debtors. They're real. They're old. They're the reason the Synthetic overlay exists.

---

[The word "Debtors" hangs in the air. The shard goes cold-not pain, but recognition. Like hearing a name it already knew.]

Avyanna: How old?

Nyx: Old. The records are incomplete-deliberately, I think. Someone wanted the history lost. What we know is: a group found a way to externalize their mortality. They built infrastructure to harvest life from everyone else.

[Their voice tightens.]

Nyx: We have names for the system. Not for the beneficiaries. Not yet.

Avyanna: The Writbound-

Nyx: Maintain the infrastructure. Whether the individual Auditors know what they're serving-that's harder to say. The licenses, the containment protocols, the "rogue" classifications-all of it keeps extraction running smoothly. Order isn't the point. Order is the excuse.

---

[Avyanna's hands are shaking. She presses them flat against her knees. The shard pulses-warm now, steadying.]

Avyanna: Why are you telling me this?

Nyx: [very quiet] Because the shard in you isn't Synthetic. It's Primordial. Old. Possibly older than the overlay itself.

[They lean forward.]

Nyx: Whatever is waking up inside you doesn't follow the extraction rules. It's not feeding on anyone. It's not drawing from a stolen pool. It's something else-something that existed before the theft began.

Avyanna: Is that... good?

Nyx: [a thin smile] That's the question, isn't it.

---

[They spend the next hour on practical exercises.]

[Nyx teaches Avyanna to feel the Lattice-not the Synthetic overlay, but what's underneath. The shard makes her sensitive; she can perceive patterns most practitioners train for years to detect.]

[It's uncomfortable. The Primordial Lattice feels different-slower, heavier, more *present*. Like the difference between reading about water and actually being submerged.]

Nyx: The first constraint is locality. You must be physically present to work with the Primordial Lattice. No remote effects. No acting at distance. If you can't be there, you can't affect it.

Avyanna: What about anchors? You mentioned-

Nyx: Anchors are established connections. Places you've been. People you're bonded to. They extend your presence, but they still require relationship. You can't anchor to something you've never touched.

---

[Avyanna tries to feel the ship around her. The shard helps-or she helps it, or they work together in ways she doesn't understand yet.]

[The Lumen Thief has a presence. Old, layered, full of the crew's accumulated attention. It's not quite alive, but it's not quite dead either. Something in between-a space that's been loved into coherence.]

Nyx: [watching her face] You feel it.

Avyanna: The ship. It's... it's like it knows we're here.

Nyx: [nodding] This ship has been worked with for decades. Maintained with care. Treated as crew instead of tool. That attention builds-it creates a kind of anchor. The Lumen Thief isn't just a vessel. It's a relationship.

---

[The second constraint is throughput.]

Nyx: Primordial magic is rate-limited. You can only move so much energy through a given channel in a given time. Push harder, and the channel degrades.

Avyanna: Like the CLI symptoms. Corridor Load Index.

Nyx: [surprised] You've been paying attention.

Avyanna: Jalen explained it. What happens when a route gets overloaded.

Nyx: Same principle. The Synthetic overlay ignores throughput limits-that's why it can scale. But the underlying Lattice still has them. When Synthetic practitioners run hot, they're drawing more than the local capacity can regenerate. The result is degradation. Static zones. Collapsed routes. Regions that die.

---

[The third constraint is consent.]

Nyx: Primordial magic requires participation. You can't do things *to* people-only *with* them. Effects that harm require the target's resistance; effects that help require their acceptance.

Avyanna: That sounds... limiting.

Nyx: [smiling] It is. Beautifully so.

[They gesture at the candle arrangement.]

Nyx: The Synthetic overlay removed consent requirements. That's what makes it useful for extraction-you can take from people who didn't agree. You can build systems that harvest without permission. You can treat populations as fuel.

[Their voice hardens.]

Nyx: The Primordial way can't do that. It's not a weapon for empire. It's a tool for relationship. That's why the Debtors suppressed it. That's why the education teaches everyone to ignore it.

---

[The lesson continues. Avyanna learns to modulate her perception-to feel the difference between the Synthetic overlay and the Primordial substrate. The shard helps, translating sensations she doesn't have words for.]

[By the end, she's exhausted. Not physically-something deeper. The kind of tired that comes from using muscles she didn't know she had.]

---

[As they clean up the candles, Avyanna watches Nyx's hands. Precise. Practiced. The movements of someone who's done this ritual many times before.]

Avyanna: How do you know all this? The Sutured stuff. The Primordial techniques.

[Nyx's hands pause. Just for a moment.]

Nyx: I was affiliated. Years ago. Before I understood what it would cost.

Avyanna: What did it cost?

[Silence. The candle smoke rises between them.]

Nyx: Someone I loved used Synthetic magic to save my life. The cost was memory. Theirs, not mine.

[They set down the last candle. Don't look at Avyanna.]

Nyx: They don't remember loving me anymore. They don't remember any of it-the years, the promises, anything that mattered. It was extracted to pay for my survival.

Avyanna: [quiet] That's... that's why you know the costs are real.

Nyx: [finally meeting her eyes] That's why I know the costs are *wrong*. The magic worked. I'm alive. But there should have been another way. A way that didn't require someone's love to be fuel.

[A beat.]

Nyx: The Sutured Houses teach the Primordial methods because they know the Synthetic costs fall on real people. I lapsed because I needed to do more than teach. But the knowledge stayed.

Avyanna: [hesitant] Why you? For the training, I mean. Elia sees the sparkles. She's the one who found me.

Nyx: [a short, dry sound that might be a laugh] Elia is unfairly good at almost everything. Sight, instinct, pattern-reading-she operates at a level most practitioners don't reach in a lifetime.

[Something fond and exasperated crosses Nyx's face.]

Nyx: She's terrible at teaching. She sees so clearly that she forgets other people are still learning to open their eyes. What's obvious to her is invisible to everyone else.

Avyanna: So you translate.

Nyx: Elisira and I translate. Vesper translates in her own way-less theory, more "watch how I do it, then try." Elia finds the sparks. The rest of us help them understand what they're looking at.

---

Nyx: [observing] You'll need rest. The shard is processing too.

Avyanna: Processing what?

Nyx: Everything you just learned. Whatever's inside you isn't passive-it's learning alongside you. Building context. Preparing for something.

[A beat.]

Nyx: When it fully manifests, remember what I taught you today. Locality. Throughput. Consent. Those constraints aren't limitations-they're what make the power sustainable. They're what kept the galaxy alive before the overlay was built.

---

Avyanna: [quiet] And the extraction facilities? The workers who died young?

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] Didn't have to happen. Any of it. The conservation law is a lie wrapped in physics language. The costs could have been distributed. Could have been voluntary. Could have been sustainable.

[Their voice breaks, just slightly.]

Nyx: They chose extraction because it was profitable. Because some lives were worth less than others. Because consent is slower than coercion, and the Debtors didn't want to wait.

---

[Avyanna stands. Her legs are unsteady. The shard is quiet-processing, like Nyx said. Integrating.]

Avyanna: Thank you.

Nyx: [surprised] For what?

Avyanna: For telling me the truth. The whole thing. Not just the parts that are safe.

[Nyx's face does something complicated-grief and pride and something else she can't name.]

Nyx: That's what we do here. Sparkle Doctrine. No secrets that don't need to be secrets.

Avyanna: Even when the truth is this big?

Nyx: [quietly] Especially then.

---

[Later, in her bunk, Avyanna stares at the ceiling and lets the lesson settle.]

[Two Lattices. Primordial and Synthetic. One bounded by locality and consent. One designed to extract without limits.]

[The mine wasn't an accident. The short lives weren't natural. The whole system is running on stolen time, and whoever built it is still out there, still taking.]

(I was inside the extraction. I was being harvested. Every day in the Kennel, something was being taken from me that I'll never get back.)

[The shard pulses. Warm. Steady. Present.]

(But the shard isn't Synthetic. It's Primordial. Whatever is waking up inside me follows different rules.)

[She doesn't know what that means yet. Doesn't know if it's good or bad. Doesn't know what "fully manifest" will look like or cost.]

[But she knows one thing now, with absolute certainty:]

(The conservation law is a lie. And I'm going to learn everything they tried to hide.)
