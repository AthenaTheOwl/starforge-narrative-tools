# Chapter 17d: Glossary of Lies

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH17c, SELF-DIRECTED STUDY]**
> *Location: Library / Study alcove*
> *Present: Avyanna; brief check-ins from Vesper and Cinnamon.exe*
> *Teaching: reader/world literacy via Avyanna's notes; "describe, then interrogate" as reflex*

---

[The library is small, which makes it feel safer.]

[Shelves. Terminals. A table with a lamp that throws warm light without being asked.]

[A stamp on the wall reads:]

> **TAKE WHAT YOU NEED**

[Avyanna reads it like it's a threat.]

(Need according to who. Enforced by whom. Paid how.)

[No one jumps out to correct her for touching a book.]

[She touches one anyway.]

[The cover is worn. The title is boring: *Civic Forms and Jurisdictional Practice (Guild Primer).*]

[She sits.]

[She opens her notebook.]

[On the first page she writes, in careful block letters:]

> **GLOSSARY OF LIES**

[Then, underneath, columns:]

> WORD
>
> WHAT THEY SAY IT MEANS
>
> WHO PROFITS
>
> WHO ENFORCES
>
> WHAT IT COSTS

(If I can name the shape of the trap, maybe it stops being invisible.)

---

[She starts with the words that keep showing up like insects in her teeth.]

**COMPLIANCE**

[She pauses.]

[In the mine, "compliance" meant: do what you're told or your body becomes a lesson.]

[She writes:]

> WHAT THEY SAY: safety / order / protocol
>
> WHO PROFITS: whoever issues the rule
>
> WHO ENFORCES: guards / forms / hunger
>
> COST: agency

[The word sits there, ugly and honest.]

[She turns the page.]

**WEATHER**

[She almost laughs.]

(Weather. Like the corridor is a sky and not a mechanism.)

> WHAT THEY SAY: natural / inevitable / no one's fault
>
> WHO PROFITS: insurance / people selling "protection"
>
> WHO ENFORCES: fear / delayed shipments / fees
>
> COST: questions

[She taps her pen.]

[Elia would write: "STAB IT."]

[Elisira would write: "FILE IT."]

[Vesper would write: "PROVE IT."]

[Avyanna writes:]

> NEXT: find pattern that shows it isn't weather

[It feels like building a knife one note at a time.]

---

[Cinnamon pings the terminal with a small notification.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// NOTE: good structure | SUGGESTION: add "what would disprove it" column>

[Avyanna stares at that.]

Avyanna: [muttering] Of course you want a disproof column.

<CINNAMON.EXE// AFFIRMATION: yes | COMMENTARY: adversaries exist>

[She adds a sixth column.]

> WHAT WOULD PROVE ME WRONG

[The notebook looks more dangerous immediately.]

---

[She keeps going.]

**VOLUNTARY DISCLOSURE**

> WHAT THEY SAY: transparency / cooperation
>
> WHO PROFITS: whoever wants your history
>
> WHO ENFORCES: social pressure / clerks with smiles
>
> COST: leverage (yours)
>
> DISPROOF: they cite a law with enforcement and consequence

[She underlines "cite a law."]

**PROCESSING FEE**

[Her pen hovers.]

(Mine word. Station word. Same shape.)

> WHAT THEY SAY: administrative necessity
>
> WHO PROFITS: middlemen
>
> WHO ENFORCES: delays / "lost paperwork"
>
> COST: time / dignity
>
> DISPROOF: fee correlates to actual labor

---

[A shadow falls over her page.]

[Vesper stands at the end of the table with a mug in hand, reading without touching.]

Vesper: Glossary of lies.

Avyanna: [bracing] It's... for me.

Vesper: Good.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: Good?

Vesper: Yes.

Vesper: This is how you stop being controlled by vocabulary.

[Vesper's gaze moves to the disproof column.]

Vesper: You added falsification tests.

Avyanna: Cinnamon told me to.

Vesper: [dry] Cinnamon is correct.

[Vesper taps the edge of the spreadsheet.]

Vesper: Add one more column.

Avyanna: Of course.

Vesper: "How would I explain this to someone else."

Avyanna: Why.

Vesper: Because if you can teach it, you own it.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

(Own it. Not be owned by it.)

Avyanna: Okay.

[She writes:]

> HOW TO TEACH IT

---

[Vesper sets the mug down beside her notebook.]

[Not a gift. Not a transaction. Just a fact: warm liquid exists, and you are allowed to drink it.]

Vesper: Keep going.

[Then she leaves.]

[Avyanna watches her go and feels, for a moment, like she is being trained to survive a world that wants her illiterate.]

[She looks back down at her notebook.]

[She writes a new entry.]

**FAMILY**

[Her hand pauses.]

(Not established. Not defined. Not safe. Not a trap. Maybe.)

[She writes in the first column anyway.]

> WHAT THEY SAY: nothing

[She exhales.]

[Then, carefully:]

> WHO ENFORCES: us

---

[She adds more entries until her hand cramps.]

**JURISDICTION**

> WHAT THEY SAY: neutral authority / procedure
>
> WHO PROFITS: whoever owns the court
>
> WHO ENFORCES: uniforms / locks / paperwork
>
> COST: options
>
> DISPROOF: enforcement is absent

**HUMANITARIAN**

[She stares at the word for a long time.]

(Humanitarian is what people call themselves when they want to feel clean.)

> WHAT THEY SAY: mercy
>
> WHO PROFITS: whoever controls the aid
>
> WHO ENFORCES: distribution offices / "eligibility"
>
> COST: dignity (if conditional)
>
> DISPROOF: aid is delivered without leverage

**SAFETY**

> WHAT THEY SAY: protection
>
> WHO PROFITS: whoever sells it
>
> WHO ENFORCES: fear
>
> COST: freedom
>
> DISPROOF: safety increases options instead of reducing them

[The last one makes her pause.]

(Safety that cages you is not safety.)

---

[A new book appears on the table beside her notebook.]

[She didn't hear the door.]

[Bubbles must have done it. Or Elisira. Or the ship itself, quietly feeding her curiosity.]

[*Rim Accord Civic Practices: A Field Guide.*]

[A sticky note is attached to the cover in neat handwriting.]

> **This one will help you avoid talking yourself into a trap. - E**

[E.]

[Elisira.]

[Avyanna touches the note like it might burn.]

(She's paying attention.)

[Avyanna opens the book.]

[It is full of arguments.]

[Not propaganda arguments. Real arguments: people disagreeing on paper because disagreement is allowed to exist in public.]

[Her stomach twists with something that isn't fear.]

(Noise. Choice. Floors.)

---

[Cinnamon pings again.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// QUESTION: do you want this indexed>

Avyanna: Indexed.

<CINNAMON.EXE// CLARIFICATION: do you want privacy on your notes>

[Avyanna's heart stutters.]

Avyanna: Privacy.

<CINNAMON.EXE// RESPONSE: default privacy enabled | NOTE: sharing is always opt-in>

[Avyanna's eyes sting. Again. Annoying. Habit-forming.]

---

[She writes one more entry, smaller than the others.]

**OPTIONAL**

> WHAT THEY SAY: choice
>
> WHO PROFITS: depends
>
> WHO ENFORCES: no one
>
> COST: fear (yours)
>
> DISPROOF: nothing happens when you decline

[She underlines the last line three times.]

[Then she closes her notebook.]

[She sits in the library until her body stops expecting someone to come take the table away.]
