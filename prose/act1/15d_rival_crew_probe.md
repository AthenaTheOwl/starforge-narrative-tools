# Chapter 15d: Rival Crew Probe

> **LUMEN THIEF - GUILD STATION NOTE (INTERNAL):**
> *Topic: curiosity is a form of targeting*
> *Rule: understate; let them remember their own assumptions*

---

[The Guild concourse is loud in a way the mine never was.]

[The mine's noise was work.]

[This noise is choice.]

[Vendors. Bounties. Arguments that end without blood.]

[People laughing like it doesn't cost them.]

[Avyanna keeps her back to a support column and watches the flow of crews.]

[Not because she's scared.]

[Because she learned that crowds are ledgers.]

[Everyone here has an invoice attached to their posture.]

---

[The Lumen Thief crew draws eyes even when they try not to.]

[Not because they're loud.]

[Because they don't look like they're hunting for permission.]

[Because Elia moves like she owns the air, and Elisira moves like the air asked her to.]

[Because Vesper's stillness reads like a warning sign to anyone with sense.]

[Because Rho carries a parts crate like it weighs nothing and his eyes like it weighs everything.]

[Because the AIs talk where everyone can hear them and nobody shushes them.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: external attention density elevated within 8m radius | INFERENCE: reputation gradient interacting with local rumor flow | CONFIDENCE: 0.74>

---

[Ten people approach in a loose wedge.]

[A full-sized crew.]

[Uniform jackets. Patches. Matching boots.]

[Roles visible even to Avyanna's untrained eye: lead in front, two heavies, a tech, a pilot, a medic, an admin with a slate, and extra bodies for redundancy.]

[They are built to win by occupying space.]

[Avyanna recognizes the technique.]

[The mine did it with supervisors.]

[This is the same math in cleaner fabric.]

---

Their Lead: [smiling] Lumen Thief.

[Not a greeting.]

[A label tossed like bait.]

Elia: [bright] Hi.

Their Admin: [pleasant, to Vesper] You're the fixer, right.

Vesper: [mild] Define "fixer."

Their Admin laughs like it's a joke because admitting it isn't would be rude.

Their Lead: We don't mean anything by it. People talk.

Elisira: [flat] People always talk.

Their Lead: They say you take jobs nobody else can.

Rho: [dry] People say the corridor weather is natural.

Their Lead: [smile tightening] So it's true.

Vesper: What's your office.

Their Lead: Excuse me.

Vesper: Your office. Your authority. The thing you represent when you say "we." Which jurisdiction are you trying to drag us into by talking to us like this.

[The lead's smile wavers.]

Their Admin: We're just contractors.

Elisira: That's a credential. Not an office.

[The admin glances down at their slate. Not nervous. Logging.]

[Avyanna watches the motion and feels her skin crawl.]

(A list.)

---

Their Lead: [recovering, to Avyanna] You're new.

[He says it like an advantage.]

[Avyanna keeps her face neutral.]

Avyanna: Define "new."

[Elia's mouth twitches.]

[Elisira doesn't look at Avyanna, but her posture shifts by a fraction: approval, filed.]

Their Lead: [laughing] Okay. Fair.

Their Lead: We heard you run a small crew.

Rho: We run a sufficient crew.

Their Lead: Seven bodies.

Their Pilot: [low] Plus their drones.

Their Medic: And the ship systems. You can't count them as bodies.

Elia: [cheerful] You can if they yell at you.

<WAFFLE.BAT// OBSERVATION: external crew attempting informal intimidation via headcount | COUNTERMEASURE: ignoring | CONFIDENCE: 0.92 | COMMENTARY: numbers are not competence>

Their Tech: [to Cinnamon's voice source] Is that your ship AI.

Cinnamon doesn't answer questions from strangers.

Vesper: She has a name.

Their Tech: What is it.

Vesper: Not your business.

---

[Their lead spreads their hands.]

Their Lead: We're not trying to steal anything.

Their Lead: We're offering to coordinate. Guild's getting crowded. Contracts are getting weird. You know how it is.

Elisira: [flat] We do.

Their Lead: We can share intel. Share lanes. Keep from stepping on each other.

Rho: [dry] Define "step."

Their Lead: [smile thinning again] You know. Compete.

Vesper: Then file a co-op agreement.

Their Lead: That's-

Vesper: That's how coordination works if you're not trying to hide your leverage.

[The lead's gaze slides to Avyanna again.]

Their Lead: How old are you.

[Silence drops like a tool hitting metal.]

[Not because the question is illegal.]

[Because it's diagnostic.]

Vesper: [cold] Leave.

Their Lead: I'm not-

Elia: [still bright] She is.

Elia: You're doing math in public.

Elia: We don't like your math.

Their heavy shifts their weight.

[Elia smiles wider.]

[It isn't friendly.]

It's delighted.

Elia: If you want to learn how old she is, go ask the Guild for her file and see what they charge you.

Their Medic: [murmuring] That's not fair.

Rho: [to the medic, almost kind] The galaxy isn't.

Rho: That's why we build floors anyway.

---

[Their admin finally looks up from the slate.]

Their Admin: We're not a threat.

Elisira: Then stop asking threat questions.

Their Admin: We were curious.

Elisira: Curiosity is not neutral.

Elisira: Curiosity is a direction you walk in.

---

[The other crew backs up a step.]

[Not fear.]

[Calculation.]

[Elia is smiling.]

[Vesper is not.]

[Elisira looks like a locked door.]

[And the AIs are listening like witnesses.]

Ten bodies doesn't feel like power anymore.

It feels like liability.

Their Lead: [hands up] Okay. Okay. Message received.

Their Lead: We'll keep our distance.

Vesper: Good.

Their Lead: People are going to keep talking.

Vesper: They were going to anyway.

Their Lead: You're not worried.

Rho: We're always worried.

Rho: We just don't let it run the ship.

---

[The other crew leaves.]

[Ten bodies peeling away into the concourse noise.]

[Avyanna watches their admin's slate as they go.]

[Not the writing. The habit.]

[The way they cataloged her like a salvage item.]

(Curiosity is targeting.)

[Elisira touches Avyanna's elbow once as they fall back into motion.]

[Not a drag.]

[A check-in disguised as navigation.]

Elisira: Green.

Avyanna: [quiet] Green.

Elia: [cheerful] See.

Elia: We can do public spaces without murder. Growth.

Vesper: [dry] Don't jinx it.

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee held boundary under social pressure | INFERENCE: integration improving | CONFIDENCE: 0.86 | NOTE: good work>

[Avyanna doesn't smile.]

[But she feels something settle in her chest.]

[Not safety.]

[A tool.]

[A script that isn't obedience.]

---

[As they pass a bounty board, Avyanna sees the reflection of the other crew's lead watching them from across the concourse.]

[Not approaching.]

[Just... measuring.]

[Avyanna keeps walking.]

[She doesn't look back.]

[She lets them keep their assumptions.]

[Because the ship taught her that being underestimated is sometimes the only affordable cloak.]
