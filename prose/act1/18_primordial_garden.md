# Chapter 18: Primordial Garden

> **SUTURED HOUSE VISITOR LOG - GARDEN-7 (RESTRICTED):**
> *Vessel: Lumen Thief, Registry 7742-KAEL*
> *Purpose: Training access (provisional)*
> *Duration: 36 hours standard*
> *Escort: Nyx Halden (House affiliate, lapsed)*
> *Note: The trainee carries something old. The garden will respond.*
> *Addendum: This is not a gift. This is a test.*

---

[The station doesn't look like much from the outside.]

[A converted asteroid, hollowed and pressurized, spinning slowly in a static-adjacent zone where most ships wouldn't dare to anchor. The hull is old-pre-Compact construction, maybe older. No visible weapons. No obvious defenses.]

[Avyanna stands at the Lumen Thief's viewport, watching the approach. The shard at the base of her skull is warm-not pain, not warning. Something else. Anticipation, maybe.]

Nyx: [beside her] They call it a Garden. One of the Sutured training grounds.

Avyanna: It doesn't look like a garden.

Nyx: The garden is inside.

---

[Docking is smooth. Jalen navigates the Thief through an approach corridor that feels wrong-angles that shouldn't work, distances that don't quite add up. The shard hums, translating something she can't consciously perceive.]

[The airlock opens onto a receiving bay. Clean. Quiet. No armed guards-but a stone pedestal near the door holds a visitor's log, real paper, and a stylus worn smooth from decades of use. A placard mounted above it reads: *Record your presence. Presence is consent.*]

[A single figure waits beside the log: an elderly person in simple robes, face weathered, eyes sharp. They gesture for Nyx to sign.]

[Nyx does, without complaint. Avyanna watches them write their name, the date, the ship registry-the same paperwork rituals she saw everywhere else, just rendered in older materials.]

Elder: [to Nyx] You're late. Still.

Nyx: [dry] I'm always late. You still haven't adjusted your expectations.

Elder: And you still haven't come home. Three years lapsed now, is it?

Nyx: [carefully neutral] Four. But who's counting.

[Something passes between them. Old familiarity. Old tension. Old fracture Avyanna can't read.]

Elder: [looking at Avyanna] And this is the carrier.

Avyanna: [stiffening] My name is Avyanna.

Elder: [after a beat] Yes. It is.

---

[The elder leads them through corridors that curve in directions they shouldn't. The station's geometry is wrong-not threatening, just different. Built according to rules Avyanna doesn't know yet.]

[And then they step through a final door, and Avyanna stops breathing.]

---

[The Garden is impossible.]

[A vast domed space, kilometers across, filled with growing things-trees, flowers, vines, organisms that aren't quite plants and aren't quite anything else. Light filters from above, soft and golden, from a sun that shouldn't exist inside an asteroid.]

[The air is thick with scent: loam, chlorophyll, something sweeter underneath. The shard flares-not pain, not warning. *Recognition*. Like coming home to a place she's never been.]

Avyanna: [barely a whisper] How is this...

Nyx: [quiet] The Primordial Lattice. This is what it can do when it's not being extracted from.

---

[They walk through growing things. The path winds between cultivation plots, meditation circles, small structures that might be homes or temples or something in between.]

[Other practitioners are here-dozens of them, working in the gardens, sitting in circles, moving through exercises that look like dance and feel like something else. They're all ages. All backgrounds. And they all look... healthy. Vital. None of the extraction damage Avyanna saw in the mine.]

Elder: [walking ahead] This garden has been maintained for three hundred years. Primordial methods only. No Synthetic extraction. No externalized costs.

Avyanna: [looking at the practitioners] They don't age like-

Elder: Like mine workers? No. They age like humans are supposed to age. Slowly. With dignity. Without having their lives stolen to power someone else's convenience.

[The words land like blows. Avyanna thinks of the woman in the mine who looked ancient at thirty-two. Thinks of the fifthieth birthday that was treated like a funeral joke.]

(This is what they took from us. This is what life is supposed to look like.)

---

[The training area is a clearing at the garden's center.]

[A circle of stones. A pool of still water. Trees whose branches form a natural dome overhead. At the entrance, a weathered sign reads: *DO NOT RUN HOT. Throughput limits protect everyone. Report discomfort immediately.* The warning looks decades old-safety placards, even here.]

[The shard is pulsing now-steady, rhythmic, almost excited.]

Nyx: [to the elder] The basic exercises. Grounding, perception, local boundary work.

Elder: [studying Avyanna] The shard will interfere. It's already trying to communicate.

Nyx: That's why we're here. To give it context. To give *her* context.

[The elder nods. Turns to Avyanna.]

Elder: What do you know about what you're carrying?

Avyanna: [careful] It's Primordial. It's old. It's... waking up.

Elder: Did Nyx tell you what "waking up" means?

Avyanna: [glancing at Nyx] Not exactly.

Elder: [a thin smile] Good. Some things should be discovered, not explained.

---

[The first exercise is simple: sit. Breathe. Feel the Lattice around her.]

[Avyanna settles onto one of the stones. The surface is warm, sun-heated, solid. She closes her eyes.]

[The shard responds immediately. Pressure behind her eyes. Geometric patterns at the edge of perception. Something trying to communicate in a language she doesn't speak yet.]

Nyx: [nearby] Don't fight it. Don't chase it. Just notice.

[She tries. The patterns shift-fractals, spirals, shapes that fold through dimensions she can't name. The shard is showing her something. Teaching her something. But the translation isn't working yet.]

[Pain. Sharp. Sudden. She gasps, opens her eyes.]

Elder: [unsurprised] It tried too much too fast. The channel isn't ready.

Nyx: [concerned] Avyanna-

Avyanna: [breathing hard] I'm okay. It's just... loud.

---

[They try again. Smaller this time. The elder guides her toward simple perception-not trying to use the Lattice, just feeling its presence.]

[The garden helps. Something about this place makes the shard calmer, more patient. The Primordial Lattice here is strong, stable, nurtured. It's not fighting extraction damage or Synthetic interference.]

[Avyanna feels it: a vast, slow presence underneath everything. The substrate that reality runs on. It's warm, patient, older than anything she has words for.]

Avyanna: [eyes still closed] It's... it's like being inside something alive.

Elder: [approvingly] Yes. The Primordial Lattice is the galaxy's nervous system. Not a tool to be used-a partner to be respected.

---

[The second exercise is boundary work.]

Elder: Feel where you end and the Lattice begins. The edge of your presence. The limits of your locality.

[Avyanna tries. It's harder than she expected. Her sense of self has always been... uncertain. In the mine, she learned to be invisible, to occupy as little space as possible. Now she's being asked to feel her own edges.]

[The shard helps, unexpectedly. It maps her boundaries for her-here is Avyanna, here is not-Avyanna, here is the edge where they meet. The geometry is strange but stable.]

Nyx: [watching] Good. Now extend it. Not far-just a hand's breadth. Feel the space around you as if it were part of you.

[She tries. The extension is clumsy, uncertain. But something happens-a shimmer at the edge of perception, a slight pressure in the air around her hands.]

Elder: [quiet] She's a natural. The shard chose well.

Nyx: [also quiet] Or she chose the shard. We still don't know which direction that went.

---

[They break for food. The garden provides: fruit from trees Avyanna doesn't recognize, bread baked in a communal oven, water that tastes like nothing she's ever tasted-clean, mineral, alive.]

[She sits with Nyx on a stone bench, watching other practitioners move through their routines.]

Avyanna: They're old.

Nyx: [glancing at her] Who?

Avyanna: The practitioners. Some of them. Old in a way I've never seen.

[She points to a figure across the clearing-a woman with silver hair, moving through a slow form with the grace of someone who's been doing it for decades. Her face is lined but not weathered. Her body is aged but not broken.]

Avyanna: In the mine, no one looked like that. "Fifty is a good run," they said. Like it was normal.

Nyx: [quietly] It's not normal. It's never been normal. The extraction makes people forget what human aging is supposed to look like.

---

Avyanna: How old is the oldest person here?

Nyx: [considering] In this garden? Perhaps ninety. Ninety-five.

Avyanna: [staring] Ninety-five?

Nyx: The Primordial way doesn't steal from practitioners. It works with them. Sustainable methods, sustainable lives.

[Nyx lets the number sit.]

Nyx: Someone is living longer than this. Much longer. We don't know exactly how old, or how many. But the extraction economy isn't running itself. It's feeding something.

Avyanna: And everyone else pays.

Nyx: Everyone else pays.

---

[They eat in silence for a few minutes.]

[Avyanna tries to make herself small on the bench. She fails. The garden makes smallness feel ridiculous-like crouching under a sky that wants you to stand.]

[Her fingers keep drifting back to the bread, to the fruit. Counting. Calculating.]

[She catches herself tucking an extra piece into her pocket. Habit. Scarcity math.]

[Nyx's gaze flicks-not accusation. Not permission either. Just noticing.]

Nyx: You can take food.

Avyanna: [stiff] I wasn't-

Nyx: I know.

[Nyx's voice stays careful. Neutral enough not to trip shame.]

Nyx: The Kennel taught you that eating is a negotiation.

Nyx: Here, it's maintenance.

Avyanna: Maintenance.

Nyx: Bodies are machines with feelings.

Nyx: If you run them on panic and caffeine and spite, they still run.

Nyx: For a while.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: In the mine, if you got tired, it meant you were weak.

Nyx: In the mine, if you got tired, it meant you were human.

[A beat.]

Nyx: They called humanity weakness because it made you easier to manage.

[Avyanna's jaw tightens. The sentence is a lever inside her ribcage.]

Avyanna: So... when someone says "that's just the way bodies work," I should ask-

Nyx: Who profits from you believing that.

Avyanna: [quiet, fierce] And what they're hiding.

[Nyx's mouth twitches. Not quite a smile. Something like approval.]

Nyx: Yes.

[Avyanna stares across the clearing at the older practitioners again. The ones who look... intact.]

Avyanna: If this is possible, why doesn't everyone-

Nyx: Because "possible" is not the same as "allowed."

Nyx: Because the people who benefit from extraction also control the education.

[Nyx taps the edge of Avyanna's pocket with two fingers-light, not touching her skin.]

Nyx: Eat when you're hungry.

Nyx: Rest when you're tired.

Nyx: Not as a reward.

Nyx: As a refusal.

[Avyanna sits with that. Rest as refusal. Food as refusal. Her body as a thing that belongs to her, not a schedule.]

[The afternoon brings harder exercises.]

[The elder has Avyanna attempt local influence-not magic in the Synthetic sense, but relationship with the Lattice. Asking, not demanding. Suggesting, not forcing.]

[She tries to move a pebble. Not with force-with invitation. Extending her presence, making space for the pebble to shift, offering a destination without insisting.]

[Nothing happens.]

[Nothing happens for a long time.]

Elder: [patient] You're still thinking like a Synthetic user. You're trying to make the Lattice do what you want.

Avyanna: [frustrated] How else would I-

Elder: Ask. Wait. Accept the answer, whatever it is.

---

[She tries again. And again. The shard pulses, patient, observing.]

[On the fifteenth attempt, something shifts. The pebble doesn't move-but she feels something. A response. A presence noticing her request and considering it.]

Avyanna: [eyes widening] It's... it's thinking about it?

Elder: [almost smiling] The Lattice isn't mechanical. It's relational. Every interaction is a conversation.

Nyx: [to the elder] The shard is translating for her. She shouldn't be perceiving that clearly yet.

Elder: [studying Avyanna] No. She shouldn't.

---

[By evening, Avyanna is exhausted.]

[The kind of tired that goes deeper than muscles-the fatigue of learning to use parts of herself she didn't know existed. The shard is quiet now, processing, integrating. It's worked as hard as she has.]

[Nyx finds her sitting by the pool, watching the water reflect the garden's false sunset.]

Nyx: How do you feel?

Avyanna: [honestly] Overwhelmed. Also... hopeful?

Nyx: [sitting beside her] Why hopeful?

Avyanna: Because this exists. All of this.

[She gestures at the garden. The practitioners. The impossible, sustainable, beautiful proof that another way is possible.]

Avyanna: In the mine, I thought short lives were just... how it worked. Physics. Economics. The way reality was structured. But this-

[She stops. Tries again.]

Avyanna: This means they *chose* to make it worse. The extraction. The theft. All of it. They chose it when they could have chosen this.

---

Nyx: [quietly] Yes.

Avyanna: That's... that's worse, isn't it? Knowing it was a choice.

Nyx: Is it? I find it clarifying.

[Avyanna looks at them. Waiting.]

Nyx: If it were physics-if extraction were truly the only way-we'd have nothing to fight against. Just entropy. Just the way things are.

[They gesture at the garden.]

Nyx: But this proves it's not physics. It's policy. And policies can be changed.

---

[That night, Avyanna sleeps in a guest chamber that feels more like a cave-carved stone, living moss, air that smells of growing things. The shard settles into something like contentment.]

[She dreams of gardens. Of time that isn't stolen. Of bodies that age with dignity and lives that extend into decades she never imagined.]

[And underneath the dreams, a voice that isn't quite a voice. A presence that isn't quite present yet. Something old, patient, learning alongside her.]

[The shape of waiting. The geometry of almost-ready.]

---

[Morning brings departure.]

[The elder walks them back to the docking bay. The path seems shorter than before-the station adjusting itself, maybe, or Avyanna perceiving it differently.]

Elder: [to Avyanna] The shard in you is preparing for something. When it fully manifests, remember what you learned here.

Avyanna: Locality. Throughput. Consent.

Elder: [nodding] And one more thing.

[They stop at the airlock. Turn to face Avyanna directly.]

Elder: The conservation law is a lie. But the truth underneath it is dangerous. Don't burn hot. Don't try to scale. The Primordial way is slow because slowness protects everyone-including you.

Avyanna: I understand.

Elder: [studying her] Perhaps. We'll see.

---

[Back on the Lumen Thief, Avyanna stands at the viewport and watches the Garden station recede.]

[The shard hums. Not pain. Not warning. Something like promise.]

(There's another way. The extraction isn't the only path. The costs can be sustainable.)

[She thinks of the practitioners moving through their routines. The woman with the graceful form who might be ninety-five. The garden that had been maintained for three centuries without destroying anyone.]

(Someone built a machine to steal life from everyone else. But this place proves the machine isn't the only option.)

[The thought is too big. She puts it aside. There will be time later to think about galaxy-scale problems.]

[For now, she has exercises to practice. Boundaries to feel. A shard to work with instead of against.]

[For now, that's enough.]
