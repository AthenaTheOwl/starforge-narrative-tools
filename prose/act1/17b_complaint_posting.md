# Chapter 17b: Complaint Posting (Sparkle Doctrine Introduction)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH17, SHIP LIFE WEEK TWO]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief common room*
> *Present: Avyanna, Rho, Elia, Bubbles*
> *Teaching: Sparkle Doctrine basics, complaint as communication, conflict without punishment*

---

[The common room is the ship's informal heart. Mismatched furniture. A wall-mounted screen showing station news. A shelf of books, games, and data chips. And-prominently displayed-a digital board labeled "COMPLAINTS AND OBSERVATIONS."]

[Avyanna sits on the couch with a cup of tea, watching Rho and Elia argue about something involving the climate control system.]

Rho: [exasperated] I'm just saying, if you're cold, wear a jacket. The rest of us are fine.

Elia: [flat] I'm not cold. The cargo bay is cold. There's ice forming on the bulkhead seals.

Rho: [defensive] That's... that's probably fine.

Elia: [staring at him] Ice. On the seals.

Rho: [sighing] Fine. I'll check the heating circuits. Again.

---

[Elia pulls out a datapad. Taps something. A notification chimes on the wall-mounted board.]

[Avyanna watches as new text appears on the board:]

**COMPLAINT #847: Cargo bay heating insufficient. Ice formation observed on bulkhead seals. Safety concern. Requesting repair priority escalation. -Elia**

Rho: [groaning] You're posting it?

Elia: [matter-of-fact] Yes. So it's documented. So you can't forget. So if the seals fail, we have a record that I warned you.

Rho: [muttering] I'm not going to forget.

Elia: [dry] You forgot last month.

Rho: [pointing at her] That was different.

Elia: [raising an eyebrow] How.

Rho: [pause] ...I don't have a good answer for that.

---

[Bubbles hovers nearby, lights pulsing with what might be amusement.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: complaint posted per protocol | COMPLAINT VALID | RECOMMENDATION: repair within 48 hours to prevent seal degradation | ALSO: rho has history of forgetting maintenance tasks | RECEIPTS AVAILABLE>

Rho: [to Bubbles] You're supposed to be on my side.

<BUBBLES// CORRECTION: i am on the side of operational safety and accurate record-keeping | YOUR SIDE IS NOT A CATEGORY>

[Avyanna almost smiles. Watching Rho get roasted by a drone is strangely comforting.]

---

Avyanna: [hesitant] What is... that?

[She nods at the complaints board.]

Rho: [sitting heavily on the couch] That is the bane of my existence.

Elia: [sitting across from him] That is Sparkle Doctrine in action.

[Avyanna looks between them. Confused.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] You don't know Sparkle Doctrine yet?

Avyanna: [shaking her head] No.

Rho: [grinning despite himself] Oh, you're in for a treat. Elia, explain. You're better at the theory.

Elia: [considering] Sparkle Doctrine is a conflict resolution framework. Instead of letting grievances fester or escalate to violence, you post them publicly. Complaint, observation, concern-whatever it is, you document it. Publicly. With receipts.

---

Avyanna: [frowning] Why publicly?

Elia: [precise] Because private complaints can be ignored, dismissed, or retaliated against without witnesses. Public complaints have accountability built in.

Elia: [gesturing at the board] Everyone on this ship can see that I posted a complaint about the cargo bay heating. Rho can't pretend I didn't warn him. If he ignores it and the seals fail, the record shows negligence. If he fixes it, the record shows responsiveness.

Rho: [adding] And if I fix it, I get to post a response saying "complaint resolved, heating repaired." Then I look competent.

Elia: [dry] Theoretical competence.

Rho: [wounded] I'm very competent.

---

<BUBBLES// ADDENDUM: sparkle doctrine prevents gaslighting | PUBLIC RECORD MEANS DISPUTES CANNOT BE MEMORY-BASED | "I TOLD YOU" VS "NO YOU DIDN'T" BECOMES OBSOLETE | RECEIPTS EXIST>

Avyanna: [slow] So... it's about documentation. Again.

Elia: [nodding] Everything here is about documentation. Consent, contracts, complaints. If it's not documented, it's not real.

Rho: [leaning forward] But here's the thing. Sparkle isn't just about complaints. It's also about observations. Compliments. Random thoughts. Anything you want the crew to know.

[He pulls up the board on his datapad, scrolls.]

Rho: [reading] "Observation #723: Rho's coffee is getting worse. Possible correlation with cheap station beans. Requesting upgrade budget. -Jalen."

[He grins.]

Rho: [to Avyanna] See? Not a complaint. Just an observation. But now it's on record. And now Vesper knows that if crew morale tanks, it might be coffee-related.

---

Elia: [pulling up another entry] "Complaint #699: Common room couch has a spring that attacks the lower back. Requesting replacement or repair. -Nyx."

[She pats the couch they're sitting on.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] That complaint was posted six months ago. Couch still has the spring. But the complaint stays on the board until it's resolved. Public reminder.

Rho: [defensive] I'm waiting on a parts shipment.

Elia: [flat] For six months.

Rho: [muttering] It's a very specific spring.

<BUBBLES// CORRECTION: spring is standard-issue | RHO HAS SIMPLY NOT PRIORITIZED REQUISITION | RECEIPTS AVAILABLE>

Rho: [to Bubbles] I'm going to disable your snark subroutines.

<BUBBLES// RESPONSE: you are not authorized | ALSO I DO NOT HAVE SNARK SUBROUTINES | I HAVE ACCURACY SUBROUTINES | THEY ARE VERY EFFECTIVE>

---

[Avyanna watches the board scroll. Hundreds of entries. Some resolved. Some pending. Some just... observations about ship life.]

Avyanna: [careful] What if someone posts something... mean?

Elia: [considering] Define mean.

Avyanna: Like... personal. Attacking someone.

Rho: [nodding] That's against Sparkle rules. Complaints have to be about behavior, not identity. "You're lazy" is not allowed. "You didn't complete the task you agreed to do" is allowed.

Elia: [adding] And if someone does post an attack, the crew can flag it. If it's flagged by three people, it gets reviewed. If it's genuinely out of line, it's removed and the poster gets a warning.

Elia: [meeting Avyanna's eyes] Sparkle isn't about cruelty. It's about accountability. There's a difference.

---

<BUBBLES// CLARIFICATION: sparkle doctrine is harm reduction for conflict | CONFLICT IS INEVITABLE AMONG HUMANS | QUESTION IS WHETHER CONFLICT ESCALATES TO VIOLENCE OR GETS DOCUMENTED AND RESOLVED | SPARKLE CHOOSES DOCUMENTATION>

Avyanna: [quiet] In the mine, if you complained, you got punished.

Rho: [somber] Yeah. That's extraction logic. Keep people silent. Keep them compliant.

Rho: [gesturing at the board] Here, silence is suspicious. If no one's complaining, either everything's perfect-which is statistically impossible-or people are afraid to speak. We want complaints. They're data.

Elia: [adding] I've worked on ships where complaints were forbidden. Where you had to smile and pretend everything was fine while systems failed and people got hurt.

Elia: [flat] Those ships were disasters. People quit. Or got injured. Or sabotaged things out of spite because they had no other outlet.

Elia: Here, you complain. It gets documented. It gets addressed or not addressed. But it's visible. That's the floor.

---

[Avyanna absorbs this. Complaints as data. Conflict as normal. Documentation as protection.]

Avyanna: [hesitant] Can I... try posting something?

Rho: [surprised] Yeah. Absolutely. What's your complaint?

Avyanna: [thinking] The... the shower in my quarters. The temperature control doesn't work right. It's either scalding or freezing. No in-between.

Rho: [wincing] Okay, that's actually a problem. Why didn't you mention it before?

Avyanna: [quiet] I thought... I thought I just had to deal with it.

Rho: [firm] No. You document it. Then I fix it. That's how this works.

---

[Elia hands Avyanna her datapad.]

Elia: [guiding] Open the Sparkle board. Hit "new complaint." Fill in the fields. Description, location, priority. Then post.

[Avyanna takes the datapad. Her hands shake slightly. This feels like tattling. Like insubordination.]

[She types:]

**COMPLAINT #848: Shower temperature control in crew quarters 3B malfunctioning. Oscillates between scalding and freezing. Requesting repair. -Avyanna**

[She hesitates before hitting "post."]

Rho: [gentle] It's okay. You're allowed to complain. No one's going to punish you.

[Avyanna hits "post." The complaint appears on the board.]

---

[Rho pulls out his own datapad. Types quickly. A response appears below her complaint:]

**RESPONSE TO #848: Acknowledged. Will inspect crew quarters 3B shower system within 24 hours. Likely thermostat calibration issue. -Rho**

Rho: [to Avyanna] See? You complained. I acknowledged. Now it's on record that I'm supposed to fix it. If I don't, you can complain again. Or escalate to Vesper.

Avyanna: [uncertain] You're not... angry?

Rho: [laughing] Angry? Kid, I'm the ship's engineer. Complaints are my job security. If nothing ever broke, I'd be useless.

Elia: [dry] You're already useless.

Rho: [grinning] And yet, beloved.

---

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee successfully posted first complaint | COMPLAINT VALID AND APPROPRIATELY FORMATTED | RESPONSE TIME: immediate | ASSESSMENT: trainee learning to use voice in conflict-resolution contexts>

[Avyanna stares at the board. Her complaint is there. Public. Acknowledged. Not punished.]

Avyanna: [whispered] This is... strange.

Elia: [nodding] It is. The mine taught you that speaking up is dangerous. Unlearning that takes time.

Elia: [meeting her eyes] But every time you post a complaint and nothing bad happens, your brain collects data. Eventually, it stops expecting punishment.

Rho: [standing, stretching] I'm gonna go fix that heating issue before Elia posts a follow-up complaint about my laziness.

Elia: [calm] Already drafting it.

Rho: [pointing at her] You're the worst.

Elia: [slight smile] I know.

---

[Rho leaves. Elia stays, scrolling through the board on her datapad.]

Elia: [after a moment] You know what the best part of Sparkle is?

Avyanna: [looking at her] What?

Elia: [quiet] It means you don't have to carry everything alone. In the Wandering Market, if something bothered you, you swallowed it. Because who were you going to tell? Who cared?

Elia: [meeting her eyes] Here, you tell everyone. And someone cares. Maybe not everyone. But someone. And that's enough.

[Avyanna nods slowly. The board is still glowing on the wall. Hundreds of small grievances and observations and requests. Proof that people speak. And people listen.]

---

[She finishes her tea. Stands.]

Avyanna: [to Elia] Thank you. For explaining.

Elia: [waving her off] Just doctrine. You'll use it a hundred times. Might as well understand it now.

[Avyanna leaves the common room. In the corridor, she pauses. Pulls out her own datapad. Opens the Sparkle board. Scrolls through complaints, responses, observations.]

(People complain here. And it's normal. And it's safe.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(The hum at the base of her skull steadies: speak, record, safe.)

[She pockets the datapad and walks back to her quarters. Tomorrow, she'll post another complaint. Or an observation. Or maybe just a thought.]

[And slowly, incrementally, she's learning that her voice has a place here.]

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// SPARKLE BOARD LOG: trainee posted first complaint | RESPONSE TIME: 3.2 minutes | RESOLUTION STATUS: acknowledged, repair scheduled | ASSESSMENT: trainee engaging with crew conflict-resolution systems appropriately | STATUS: integration progressing>

<ELIA PERSONAL LOG: "She looked terrified posting that complaint. Like she expected us to yell at her for having a broken shower. But she did it anyway. That's courage. That's growth.">

---

*End Scene*
