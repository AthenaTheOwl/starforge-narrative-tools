# Chapter 11b: Senna's Small Good Thing

> **[INTERSTITIAL - OCCURS POST-CH11]**
> *Trigger: quiet that doesn't punish*
> *Purpose: Senna Cross's first rule, reclaimed as Avyanna's*

---

[Night cycle on the Lumen Thief is not dark.]

[It is dimmed to a livable softness.]

[Avyanna sits on the edge of her bunk and waits for the moment when the ship remembers to be cruel.]

[The moment doesn't come.]

(This is when they collect the debt.)

[She listens instead: ventilation, distant footsteps, the subtle hum of systems doing their jobs without demanding gratitude.]

[Her hands fidget with nothing because she doesn't own anything yet that feels safe to touch.]

[The quiet makes room for a memory she hasn't let herself pick up in years.]

---

## Intercut - AE Site K-9 (Senna's Rule)

[The Kennel is never quiet.]

[Even when the machines stop, the people keep making noise-coughs, sobs swallowed into sleeves, the wet scrape of boots through glitter-dust.]

[Avyanna is small enough that the world is mostly legs and walls.]

[She knows the rules by the way the air changes when a supervisor walks past.]

[She knows how to hold her breath so she doesn't taste the metal, because tasting it makes her want to cry and crying makes you visible.]

[Senna Cross is visible anyway.]

[Not loud.]

[Not defiant.]

[Just... present, in the way a stone is present in a stream.]

[Senna kneels beside a broken ventilation grate and pries out something that shouldn't exist down here.]

[A leaf.]

[Not a whole plant. Not a miracle. A single stubborn leaf, pale but alive, grown from mold and accident and refusal.]

[Senna cups it in her palm like she's holding contraband.]

Senna: [quiet] Look.

[Avyanna looks because looking is safer than asking why.]

Avyanna: It's... green.

[The word comes out like she's never said it before.]

Senna: Yes.

Senna: One small good thing.

[Avyanna frowns.]

Avyanna: That's not a thing.

Senna: It is.

Senna: The mine wants you to believe there is nothing good, so you stop looking.

Senna: Stop looking, and you stop noticing exits.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: Exits don't exist.

Senna: [gentle, stubborn] Exits always exist.

Senna: Sometimes they're not doors.

Senna: Sometimes they're timing.

Senna: Sometimes they're people.

[Senna folds the leaf into a scrap of cloth and presses it into Avyanna's palm.]

[Avyanna flinches like she's being handed a weapon.]

Avyanna: I can't keep it.

Senna: You can.

Avyanna: They'll take it.

Senna: Then you'll find another one tomorrow.

[Senna's eyes meet hers. Dark. Steady.]

Senna: Rule.

Senna: Every day, you find one small good thing.

Senna: You don't tell them about it.

Senna: You don't trade it.

Senna: You keep it because it proves you are still a person.

[Avyanna stares at the wrapped leaf.]

(A person.)

[The word hurts like a bruise you didn't know you had.]

Avyanna: Why are you telling me.

Senna: Because you're still looking.

Senna: That means you're not gone yet.

---

## Return - Ship Quiet

[Avyanna's hands unclench around air.]

[There is no leaf in her palm.]

[There is no glitter dust in her teeth.]

[There is only the ship, and the quiet, and the ache of having been taught one small rule that the mine couldn't invoice.]

[She stands and crosses to the little desk bolted to the wall.]

[She finds a blank page in a cheap paper notebook Elia tossed at her earlier like it was nothing.]

[Avyanna writes:]

One small good thing:

[She stares at the words until they stop looking like a lie.]

[Then she writes:]

Quiet that doesn't punish.

[She closes the notebook.]

[The ship hums like it approves of being noticed.]

*End Chapter 11b*
