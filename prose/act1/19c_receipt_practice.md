# Chapter 19c: Receipt Practice

> **[SCENE - OCCURS BETWEEN CH19 AND CH20, PRE-HEARING WORK]**
> *Location: Vesper's office*
> *Present: Avyanna, Vesper*
> *Teaching: "claim -> evidence -> inference" as a safe spine; trauma handled as procedure*

---

[Vesper's office is too orderly to be natural.]

[Shelves. Binders. A desk that looks like it has never been used for anything except war.]

[Avyanna sits across from Vesper with a stack of printouts and a pen that feels heavier than it should.]

Vesper: Tell me what you saw.

[Avyanna's jaw tightens on instinct.]

(If I tell the truth wrong, it becomes a weapon aimed at me.)

Avyanna: Which part.

Vesper: The part you are scared to put into words.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: The station clerk tried to make me sign "voluntary disclosure."

Vesper: Good. That's a claim.

Avyanna: [baffled] That's not-

Vesper: It is. "Clerk attempted coercion." Claim.

[Vesper taps the printouts.]

Vesper: Evidence.

Avyanna: The slip. The camera footage request Elisira filed. The time stamp on the terminal log.

Vesper: Good.

Vesper: Inference.

Avyanna: They were fishing. For identity. For contacts.

Vesper: Next step.

Avyanna: Report it. Archive it. Don't sign.

[Vesper's mouth twitches.]

Vesper: You're learning.

[Avyanna's chest tightens.]

Avyanna: I'm trying.

Vesper: Trying is a verb. Keep using it.

---

[Vesper slides a new printout toward her.]

[Aurum-coded header. Not the mine's logo, exactly-cleaner, but the same colors.]

[Avyanna's vision tunnels.]

[The room tilts.]

(No.)

[Her hands go cold.]

Vesper: Avyanna. Eyes on me.

[Avyanna forces her gaze up.]

Avyanna: [thin] That looks like-

Vesper: Aurum.

Avyanna: [breathing wrong] They're here.

Vesper: No.

Vesper: Not physically.

Vesper: It's a record artifact. A data style. A mimic.

[Vesper's voice stays calm on purpose.]

Vesper: Check.

Avyanna: [after a beat] Yellow.

Vesper: Good. Stay with yellow.

[Vesper does not touch her.]

[She doesn't have to.]

[She moves the printout away, flips it face-down.]

Vesper: We don't process trauma by pretending it isn't there.

Vesper: We process it by turning it into something that can be handled.

Avyanna: That sounds like you.

Vesper: It is me.

---

[Vesper opens a binder and pulls out a template.]

> **WITNESS STATEMENT (DRAFT)**
>
> *Claim*
>
> *Evidence*
>
> *Inference*
>
> *Confounders*
>
> *What would prove me wrong*

Avyanna: Cinnamon would like that.

Vesper: Cinnamon does like that.

Vesper: Now.

Vesper: We're going to practice on something that won't kill you if you get it wrong.

Avyanna: [dry] That's generous.

Vesper: It's responsible.

[Avyanna picks up her pen.]

[Her hand still shakes, but she can write through it.]

Avyanna: Claim: the mine's medical bay neglected workers on purpose.

Vesper: Evidence.

[Avyanna's voice goes quieter.]

Avyanna: The logs. The ration schedule. The number of collapsed people per shift. The way they stamped "noncompliant" on anyone who asked for painkillers.

Vesper: Inference.

Avyanna: The neglect was policy. Not failure.

Vesper: Confounders.

[Avyanna freezes.]

Vesper: This is where you stop being a victim of your own certainty.

Avyanna: [working] Confounders... shortages. Staffing. Flicker day malfunctions.

Vesper: Good.

Vesper: What would prove you wrong.

Avyanna: A supply manifest showing adequate medicine. A record of workers treated promptly.

Vesper: And if those exist.

Avyanna: Then I'm wrong.

[The words taste strange in her mouth.]

(Wrong means you die.)

[Here, wrong means: adjust.]

Vesper: If you can be proven wrong, you can be trusted.

Avyanna: That's... backwards.

Vesper: It's adversarial.

Vesper: The people who can't be wrong are trying to sell you something.

---

[A pause.]

[Avyanna stares at the blank space on her template labeled WITNESS STATEMENT and feels something in her chest shift.]

Avyanna: This is... how you fight.

Vesper: This is one way.

Vesper: Another way is Elia.

Avyanna: [a small, real laugh] Yes.

[Vesper stands, goes to the shelf, and takes down a thin folder.]

Vesper: Tomorrow we go to arbitration.

Vesper: Today we build the spine.

[She sets the folder in front of Avyanna.]

Vesper: No heroics.

Avyanna: [soft] Just receipts.

Vesper: Just receipts.

[Avyanna opens the folder.]

[This time, the paper doesn't feel like a trap.]

[It feels like a handle.]

---

[Vesper doesn't let the moment turn sentimental.]

[She flips to a new section in the binder and slides it across.]

> **CROSS-EXAMINATION PREP**
>
> *If they say you're lying, what do you point to?*
>
> *If they say you misremembered, what do you point to?*
>
> *If they say it's "standard," what do you ask?*

Avyanna: [reading] What do you ask.

Vesper: "Under which jurisdiction. Enforced by whom. Consequence for refusal."

Avyanna: [quiet] That's... the spine.

Vesper: Yes.

Vesper: When someone is trying to drown you in language, you don't swim.

Vesper: You stand on three questions.

[Avyanna's pen hovers.]

Avyanna: In the mine, questions got you noticed.

Vesper: Here, questions keep you alive.

[A beat.]

Vesper: Not just physically.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: This feels like fighting without... touching anyone.

Vesper: It is.

Vesper: It's slower than Elia. But it leaves bodies intact.

Avyanna: [dry] Sometimes.

[Vesper's mouth twitches.]

Vesper: Sometimes.

---

[Vesper pulls a small index card from a box and slides it toward Avyanna.]

> **YOUR JOB IN THE ROOM**
>
> - say what you saw
> - don't guess
> - if you don't know, say you don't know
> - if you need a minute, ask for it

Avyanna: [soft] I can ask for a minute.

Vesper: Yes.

Vesper: If anyone tries to deny you that, I will make it expensive.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

(Expensive. Protective. Not ownership.)

---

[Vesper taps the face-down Aurum-coded printout with one knuckle.]

Vesper: This.

Avyanna: [tense] Yes.

Vesper: You did not break when you saw it.

Avyanna: I almost did.

Vesper: "Almost" is data.

Avyanna: That's... also you.

Vesper: [without apology] Yes.

[She pauses.]

Vesper: When you're in the room tomorrow and something tries to pull you back into the mine-

Avyanna: Like a smell. Like a word.

Vesper: -you do not have to white-knuckle it.

Vesper: You can say: "I need to pause."

Avyanna: [quiet] And nobody will-

Vesper: No penalty.

[The phrase lands like a seal on paper.]

---

[Avyanna writes it down.]

> **NO PENALTY**

[Not in Cinnamon's font. In her own.]

[She looks up at Vesper.]

Avyanna: Thank you.

Vesper: [measured] You're welcome.

[Vesper straightens Avyanna's collar without asking. Then catches herself.]

Vesper: Go drink water.

Avyanna: [blinking] That's an order.

Vesper: It's medical advice.

Avyanna: [a small smile] Who enforces it.

Vesper: Nyx.

[Avyanna laughs.]

Vesper: And also me.

[The second part is said so quietly it almost doesn't count.]

[Almost.]

[Avyanna gathers the folder and the index card like they're equipment.]

[Because they are.]

[She leaves Vesper's office with her spine built from paper and the strange sensation of being allowed to carry it.]
