# Chapter 14c: Bridge Transit Ritual

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH14, FIRST REAL TRANSIT]**
> *Location: Bridge / corridor approach*
> *Present: Full crew (bridge rotation), AI citizens via telemetry*
> *Teaching: travel is work; fear is handled as procedure; usefulness is a job, not a personality*

---

[The bridge smells like coffee and ozone and focus.]

[Jalen sits in the pilot cradle, spine straight, hands light on the controls like anything heavier would insult the ship.]

[Elisira stands behind them with a slate, posture relaxed in the way that means she is not relaxed.]

[Vesper has one hand on a rail and the other on a datapad full of forms that never stop wanting signatures.]

[Rho leans in the doorway with a mug and the expression of someone prepared to fight the universe with caffeine.]

[Elia is sprawled near weapons control, boots on a brace, looking bored on purpose.]

[Avyanna takes the last open station and tries not to look like she is begging to be allowed to exist there.]

(Bridge means responsibility. Responsibility means they can be angry at you.)

Jalen: [without looking back] Strap in.

[Avyanna straps in immediately.]

[Then stops herself.]

[Elia's drill rises like a reflex with teeth.]

Avyanna: [careful] Why.

[Jalen's mouth twitches. Not quite a smile.]

Jalen: Because this corridor is spiteful.

Elia: [bright] Because the map lies.

Elisira: Because the lane has been shifting.

Vesper: Because insurance pretends it's weather.

Rho: Because if you hit a seam wrong, you bite your tongue off.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: That's... all the same answer.

Elia: Correct. Welcome to the galaxy.

---

[Jalen taps the console. A route path blooms on the holo-blue line through blackness, annotated with numbers and warnings.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: corridor stability rating "marginal" | NOTE: recommended speed reduction 12% | INFERENCE: reduced peak shear | CONFIDENCE: 0.84>

<WAFFLE.BAT// COMMENTARY: marginal is my favorite kind of safe>

<BUBBLES// REMINDER: keep limbs inside existence at all times>

Elia: [to the air] Thank you, Bubbles. Very inspiring.

Bubbles: [over comm] You are welcome.

[The ship shifts. A subtle pressure change, like the universe leaning in to listen.]

[The stars on the forward display smear for a heartbeat.]

[Avyanna's stomach drops.]

(Mine collapse.)

[The memory tries to overlay reality: rock groaning, dust filling lungs, the moment the ceiling decides to become ground.]

[Her hands clench on the armrests.]

[She counts exits without thinking.]

[There are none. It's a bridge. It's a box. It's a trap.]

Vesper: [quiet, not looking at her] Check.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: [too fast] Green.

[A beat.]

Avyanna: [correcting] Yellow.

[Vesper's hand shifts-doesn't touch her, just moves closer to the rail between them, a visible choice not to invade.]

Vesper: Thank you.

(Thank you for telling the truth. Not thank you for being okay.)

[Jalen's voice stays calm, which is its own kind of weapon.]

Jalen: Drift left two degrees. Hold. Hold.

[The ship responds like it likes being spoken to with respect.]

[Pressure eases.]

[Avyanna's lungs remember they are allowed to take air.]

Rho: [sipping] See. Fine.

<CINNAMON.EXE// RESPONSE: "fine" is not a metric>

Rho: [without missing a beat] "Operationally adequate."

<CINNAMON.EXE// ACCEPTABLE>

---

[A small shudder runs through the deck plates.]

[Not violent. Just... a seam.]

Elisira: [to Jalen] That was early.

Jalen: Yeah.

Jalen: [flat] The corridor is lying today.

Elia: [cheerful] I love it when the universe has personality.

Vesper: It's not personality. It's incentives.

Elia: [glancing over] Everything is incentives with you.

Vesper: [dry] Everything is incentives with everyone. Most people just don't admit it.

[Avyanna watches them argue like it's a ritual. No one is afraid of disagreement here.]

(In the mine, arguing meant punishment. Here it means... mapping.)

[Jalen flicks a switch. The display shifts-blue line, but now with a second overlay: a thin gray shadow of possible alternatives.]

Avyanna: What's that.

Jalen: Official route.

Jalen: And what the ship thinks the route is.

Avyanna: [quiet] Those aren't the same.

Jalen: [looking at her for the first time] Now you're learning.

---

[They ride the corridor like a river that dislikes boats.]

[Avyanna's job is small.]

[Read out sensor warnings when Cinnamon flags them.]

[Repeat Elisira's directions back so no one mishears.]

[Keep a log of time-stamps the way Vesper taught her: claim, source, when.]

[Useful. Quiet. Not invisible.]

[At one point, the ship lurches harder.]

[Avyanna's vision narrows.]

[The mine tries to come back.]

[Rho's voice cuts in, rough and real.]

Rho: Avy. Breathe.

[Avyanna inhales.]

[Jalen exhales at the same time-slow on purpose.]

[It pulls her rhythm with it, like gravity.]

[She holds on.]

[The corridor releases them with a final shiver, grudgingly.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: stability improving | INFERENCE: seam passed | CONFIDENCE: 0.88>

Elia: [stretching] That was disappointingly non-lethal.

Elisira: [without looking at her] We can arrange lethal if you're bored.

Elia: [delighted] She loves me.

[Avyanna's mouth twitches.]

[It's almost a smile.]

[She files that too.]

---

[Afterward, nobody disperses like they're fleeing the smell of fear.]

[Jalen eases the ship down into a quiet stretch of corridor-space-stable enough to breathe, unstable enough to keep respect.]

Elisira: [to Avyanna] Debrief.

[Avyanna's shoulders go rigid.]

(Debrief means you explain why you failed.)

Avyanna: I-

[Elisira waits, patient in the way that doesn't let you hide behind panic.]

Avyanna: [working] We hit a seam early. The deck shuddered before the display predicted it. The pressure change came before the warning ping.

Elisira: Good.

Avyanna: Jalen adjusted two degrees left and reduced speed. Cinnamon flagged "marginal" stability, but it improved after the seam.

Elisira: What did your body do.

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: My body-

Vesper: [quiet] This is evidence too.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: My hands clenched. I went cold. I... counted exits.

[Elia makes a small sound, not quite a joke.]

Elia: Same.

Avyanna: [startled] You count exits.

Elia: [bright] I count fun. Different category.

Elisira: [flat] Elia.

Elia: [innocent] I was validating.

[Avyanna's mouth twitches again.]

[Rho drinks his coffee like it's a shield and watches her carefully, as if counting how long it takes her to come back.]

Rho: You breathed when I told you.

Avyanna: Yes.

Rho: Good.

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: trainee returned to baseline within 46 seconds | INFERENCE: recovery improving | CONFIDENCE: 0.81>

[Avyanna looks at the telemetry and feels, unexpectedly, protected by being measured without being judged.]

Vesper: Log it.

Avyanna: [automatic] Yes.

[She catches herself.]

Avyanna: Why.

Vesper: Because next time your body tells you a story, we compare it to the data.

Vesper: If the story is wrong, we correct it. If the story is right, we prepare.

Avyanna: [quiet] Memory lies under stress.

Vesper: [a small nod] Exactly.

---

[Later, in the corridor outside the bridge, Avyanna stops beside a wall panel and opens her notebook.]

[She writes in block letters, the way Vesper taught her to write claims:]

> **CLAIM:** corridor seam occurred early
>
> **OBSERVATION:** deck shudder preceded warning ping
>
> **RESPONSE:** speed reduction + lateral correction
>
> **BODY:** cold, exit-counting, then recovery

[She stares at the last line.]

(Body: witness. Not enemy.)

[A shadow falls over the page.]

[Jalen stands there, hands in pockets, pretending this isn't care.]

Jalen: You did fine.

Avyanna: [startled] Fine is not a metric.

[Jalen's mouth twitches.]

Jalen: Operationally adequate.

Avyanna: [quiet] Better.

Jalen: Don't make it a habit.

[They walk away before Avyanna can turn gratitude into a performance.]
