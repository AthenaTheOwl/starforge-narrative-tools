# Chapter 12b: Game Night (Probability, Not Punishment)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH12, EARLY "PLAY IS ALLOWED" TRAINING]**
> *Location: Atrium / Commons*
> *Present: Avyanna, Waffle.bat, Cinnamon.exe, Bubbles, Souffle (cameo), Elia (brief), Elisira (brief)*
> *Teaching: probability as a tool; play as non-transactional; "losing" as information*

---

[Game night starts the way most things on the Lumen Thief start.]

[With a notice nobody can prove was mandatory.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANNOUNCEMENT: civic recreation event scheduled | TITLE: GAME NIGHT | GOAL: reduce human tension by 12% | NOTE: participation "voluntary" (but I will be sad if you do not)>

[Avyanna reads the word voluntary and waits for the hidden fee.]

(Voluntary means: you are responsible for the outcome.)

[There is no fee.]

[There is just Waffle's bat icon blinking at her from the message board like a dare.]

---

[The atrium is rearranged.]

[Not dramatically. Just... thoughtfully.]

[Couches pulled into a circle. A low table cleared. Snacks in bowls like the ship believes you deserve salt and sugar even if you didn't earn them.]

[Avyanna stands at the edge of the circle with her hands behind her back.]

(If I sit, I'm taking space. If I take space, I'm visible. Visible is dangerous.)

[Bubbles drifts in and bumps her shoulder gently with a rotor guard-an almost-touch that feels like permission.]

Bubbles: Sit.

Avyanna: [automatic] I'm fine-

[She stops herself.]

Avyanna: Why.

Bubbles: Because you were invited.

[Invited.]

[Not ordered. Not assigned. Invited.]

[Avyanna sits on the very edge of the couch like she's ready to evacuate it.]

---

[Waffle rolls in a tray on little wheels. On it: a pile of tokens, a deck of cards, and a small board with a grid and a few carved pieces shaped like ships.]

Avyanna: Is this... a training exercise.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANSWER: yes>

Avyanna: [bracing] Okay.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ADDENDUM: it is also a game>

Avyanna: Those aren't the same.

<CINNAMON.EXE// COMMENTARY: they can be | OBSERVATION: trainee assumes "game" = trap | INFERENCE: prior environment punished play | CONFIDENCE: 0.88>

[Waffle makes an offended little whirr.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// CORRECTION: play is not punishment | play is data with snacks>

Avyanna: Data with snacks.

<WAFFLE.BAT// AFFIRMATION: correct>

---

[Cinnamon projects rules onto the terminal wall in neat bullet points.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// RULESET: "DRIFT & DEBT" (training variant) | DISCLAIMER: no real debt will be incurred>

Avyanna: [tense] Why is the word debt in it.

<CINNAMON.EXE// ANSWER: to desensitize trigger vocabulary via safe context>

[Avyanna's jaw tightens.]

Avyanna: That feels... manipulative.

<CINNAMON.EXE// RESPONSE: yes | FOLLOW-UP: also protective>

[Bubbles adds without text, aloud, like a human trying.]

Bubbles: We want the word to stop owning you.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: Okay.

---

[The board is simple.]

[A route grid. Start point. End point. Pieces that move like ships.]

[Each turn you draw a card.]

[Cards say things like:]

> **WEATHER FEE**
>
> **EXPEDITE OFFER**
>
> **OUT OF STOCK**
>
> **VOLUNTARY DISCLOSURE**

[Avyanna's stomach drops anyway.]

(The galaxy uses the same words everywhere. Like they're in a template.)

Elia: [appearing in the doorway like a bad idea] Wow. You made a board game out of oppression.

<WAFFLE.BAT// PROUD: yes>

Elia: [delighted] Horrible. I love it.

[Elisira appears behind Elia, looking tired already.]

Elisira: No teaching her to gamble.

Elia: I would never.

Elisira: [flat] You would absolutely.

Elia: I would teach her to cheat. Different.

[Elisira sighs and leaves before she has to arrest someone.]

---

[Waffle points a little fan at Avyanna like a spotlight.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// INSTRUCTION: you go first>

Avyanna: Why me.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANSWER: you think too hard | this will be funny>

Avyanna: That's not a reason.

<WAFFLE.BAT// CLARIFICATION: it is a reason on this ship>

[Avyanna draws a card.]

> **OUT OF STOCK**
>
> *Rule: accept the verdict, lose one turn.*

[Her throat tightens.]

Avyanna: [quiet] That's... normal.

[Cinnamon's text appears immediately.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// QUESTION: is it enforceable>

[Avyanna stares at the card like it's a form.]

Avyanna: Who enforces it.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANSWER: the rules>

Avyanna: Then the rules are lying.

[Waffle's icon blinks.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// DELIGHT: yes>

[Bubbles drifts closer.]

Bubbles: Explain.

Avyanna: Out of stock is a claim. Not a verdict.

Avyanna: If you accept it, you stop looking.

Avyanna: If you stop looking, whoever benefits wins.

[Cinnamon pings approval.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: trainee applied doctrine correctly | INFERENCE: learning transfer | CONFIDENCE: 0.86>

[Waffle makes a dramatic whirring sound and flips the card over.]

> **OUT OF STOCK (REAL RULE)**
>
> *Rule: treat as claim. Ask: "what substitute exists?" Roll again.*

[Avyanna blinks.]

Avyanna: That's cheating.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: it is critical thinking>

Elia: [leaning in, delighted] Same thing.

---

[Avyanna rolls a die Waffle produces from somewhere it did not exist a moment ago.]

[The die clatters.]

[It lands on a number that means: you find a substitute, but it costs time.]

Avyanna: That makes sense.

Elia: [mock horror] She is learning. Quick, distract her with snacks.

[Bubbles slides a bowl of chips toward Avyanna.]

[No comment. No transaction. Just food existing near her hands.]

[Avyanna eats one and waits for guilt to arrive.]

[It doesn't.]

---

[Second turn.]

[Avyanna draws:]

> **EXPEDITE OFFER**
>
> *A clerk offers to "help." Pay one token or lose two turns.*

[Avyanna's pulse spikes.]

Avyanna: That's not an offer. That's a threat.

<CINNAMON.EXE// AFFIRMATION: correct>

Bubbles: What do you do.

Avyanna: Ask under which jurisdiction.

Elia: [clapping softly] Yes. Interrogate the cardboard.

Avyanna: [dry] I will.

[She looks at the card, as if it can answer.]

Avyanna: Under which jurisdiction is the expedite fee required.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: none>

Avyanna: Who enforces it.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: your fear>

[Avyanna goes still.]

Avyanna: [soft] That's...

Avyanna: That's the mine.

[Silence.]

[Not dangerous. Just real.]

[Bubbles' fans slow.]

Bubbles: Yes.

[Cinnamon's text appears, unusually spare.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// NOTE: fear is an enforcer in many systems>

Elia: [quiet, for once] So what do you do now.

[Avyanna looks down at her tokens.]

[Plastic disks. Meaningless. Safe.]

[She thinks of the mine's "processing fees." The way the word was used to make cruelty sound administrative.]

(I can't pay fear to stop being fear.)

Avyanna: I refuse the expedite offer.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESULT: you lose one turn>

Avyanna: [grim] Fine.

<WAFFLE.BAT// COMMENTARY: refusal costs time | STILL correct>

[Avyanna exhales.]

(Refusal costs time. Not skin.)

---

[The game continues.]

[Avyanna loses turns. Wins turns. Learns the shape of probability as something that exists whether you deserve it or not.]

[She starts to see patterns.]

["Weather fee" cards cluster after "insurance" cards.]

["Voluntary disclosure" cards show up when "assistance desk" pieces are near.]

[She starts moving her piece not toward the shortest route, but toward the route that keeps options open.]

[Bubbles watches her with the quiet intensity of someone guarding a new habit.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee optimizing for exits instead of speed | INFERENCE: safety prioritization | CONFIDENCE: 0.82>

---

[Near the end, Avyanna draws:]

> **LUCKY BREAK**
>
> *Gain two turns. No cost.*

[She stares at it.]

Avyanna: That's unrealistic.

Elia: [grinning] The universe does occasionally misplace mercy.

Avyanna: Who enforces it.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANSWER: nobody | NOTE: accept the good without paying it back>

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

(Accept the good without paying it back.)

[She takes the two turns.]

[Nothing explodes.]

[No one makes her apologize for being lucky.]

[She finishes the route one turn later, not first, but intact.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANNOUNCEMENT: trainee completed route | STATUS: not dead | EVALUATION: acceptable>

Avyanna: [laughing despite herself] Acceptable.

Elia: That's the nicest thing Waffle's ever said.

<WAFFLE.BAT// CORRECTION: i have said "delightful" before>

Elia: [to Avyanna] See. Games can be fun.

Avyanna: It was... annoying.

Elia: That's fun on this ship.

[Bubbles bumps Avyanna's shoulder again, a little more familiar this time.]

Bubbles: You played.

Avyanna: Yes.

Bubbles: And nothing bad happened.

[Avyanna swallows.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Yes.

[She files that too.]

---

[Waffle declares a "bonus round" with the smugness of an algorithm that thinks it invented generosity.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANNOUNCEMENT: BONUS ROUND | PURPOSE: prove that probability is not morality>

Avyanna: Probability isn't-

<WAFFLE.BAT// INTERRUPTION: i know | YOU need to know>

[Elia returns like she can't resist a spectacle.]

Elia: Bonus round? Who are we humiliating.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: nobody | COMMENTARY: humiliation is optional>

Elia: [disappointed] Tragic.

[Elisira appears in the doorway, sees Elia, and makes the face of someone reconsidering every life choice.]

Elisira: Do not teach her to set fires for fun.

Elia: [innocent] I would never.

Elisira: You did it yesterday.

Elia: For science.

Elisira: [flat] For boredom.

Elia: Both.

[Elisira leaves before she becomes part of the game by force.]

---

[Waffle rearranges the board.]

[Same grid. Different objective.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// RULESET UPDATE: "DRIFT & DEBT" (variance drill) | GOAL: reach endpoint with maximum optionality preserved>

Avyanna: Optionality.

Elia: Exits.

Avyanna: [quiet] Yes.

[Waffle points at Avyanna again.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// INSTRUCTION: you choose your opponent>

[Avyanna looks at the circle.]

[Bubbles: steady.]

[Cinnamon: sharp.]

[Souffle: curious.]

[Elia: grinning like a knife that learned humor.]

(Choose. Choosing is allowed.)

Avyanna: Elia.

Elia: [delighted] YES.

Elia: I will teach you despair through cardboard.

Avyanna: [dry, surprising herself] You already do that with hallway comments.

[Elia clutches her chest theatrically.]

Elia: She's learning banter. I'm so proud I could commit a felony.

---

[They take seats opposite each other.]

[Avyanna's palms sweat.]

(Games mean winners and losers. Winners get to eat. Losers get to hurt.)

[The mine tries to overlay that logic.]

[Waffle's token tray clinks, a tiny reminder: this is plastic. Not rations. Not pain.]

<BUBBLES// OBSERVATION: trainee tension spike | INFERENCE: competition trigger | CONFIDENCE: 0.86>

Bubbles: You can stop.

Avyanna: [quick] I don't want to stop.

[The sentence feels like stepping off a ledge and landing safely.]

---

[First draw.]

[Elia gets:]

> **WEATHER FEE**
>
> *Pay one token to proceed.*

Elia: [bright] No.

[She flicks the card away like it insulted her.]

Avyanna: You can't just-

<WAFFLE.BAT// COMMENTARY: she can | penalties apply>

[Waffle spins the board one notch.]

> *Penalty: lose one turn. Gain one "reputation" marker.*

Avyanna: Reputation.

Elia: [cheerful] See? Refusal costs time and buys fear. Useful.

Avyanna: That's... horrible.

Elia: Welcome to reality. But with snacks.

[Avyanna draws.]

> **VOLUNTARY DISCLOSURE**
>
> *Answer a question. If you refuse, lose one token.*

[Her throat tightens.]

(Answer a question. That's how it starts.)

Avyanna: Who enforces it.

<CINNAMON.EXE// RESPONSE: "the rules" (simulation)>

Avyanna: So I can refuse.

Elia: Yes.

Elia: But don't refuse because you're scared. Refuse because you decided.

[That's... different.]

Avyanna: I refuse.

[Waffle removes a token from her pile with dramatic ceremony.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// COMMENTARY: refusal recorded | NOTE: you are still alive>

Avyanna: [breathing] Still alive.

Elia: That's our brand.

---

[They play.]

[Elia takes wild risks, refuses anything that smells like leverage, and somehow lands on safe tiles because she treats probability like something she can bully.]

[Avyanna plays cautiously at first, then learns to take one calculated risk when the risk buys her two exits later.]

[She watches Elia's face light up when the rules bend.]

(Elia doesn't just break rules because she's bored. She breaks them because it proves they were never laws.)

[At one point, Avyanna draws "LUCKY BREAK" again.]

[She freezes.]

Avyanna: I didn't earn-

Elia: Stop that.

[No humor. Just a command that isn't a threat.]

Elia: Don't you dare turn luck into guilt.

Avyanna: [small] Why.

Elia: Because that's how they keep you small.

Elia: Take the break. Then use it to build something.

[Avyanna swallows.]

[She takes it.]

[She uses the extra turn to move into a better position and-for the first time-blocks Elia's path without apologizing.]

Elia: [eyes widening, delighted] Oh.

Elia: You read me.

Avyanna: [heart racing] I-

Elia: Again.

[Avyanna laughs. It's thin with adrenaline, but it's real.]

---

[The bonus round ends with Elia winning by doing something ridiculous that shouldn't work and does anyway.]

Elia: Chaos wins.

<CINNAMON.EXE// CORRECTION: probability won>

<WAFFLE.BAT// ADDENDUM: chaos is a probability distribution>

Elia: [pointing] See? Even the computers agree I'm art.

[Avyanna's chest aches, but not from fear.]

(I played. I lost. I'm still here. No one took food away. No one hit me.)

Bubbles: Do you want to play again later.

[Avyanna hesitates.]

[Then decides to practice wanting in a place where wanting is safe.]

Avyanna: Yes.

<WAFFLE.BAT// DELIGHT: YES>

<CINNAMON.EXE// NOTE: progress>

[Elia grins at her like she just watched a door unlock.]

Elia: Good.

Elia: We'll make you a menace yet.
