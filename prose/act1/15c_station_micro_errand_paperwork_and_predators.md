# Chapter 15c: Paperwork and Predators

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH15, FIRST STATION ERRAND]**
> *Location: Station concourse (unnamed)*
> *Present: Avyanna, Elisira*
> *Teaching: "helpful" is not always help; politeness is wrapper; forms are teeth*

---

[The concourse smells like hot oil, cheap perfume, and the particular tang of too many bodies pretending not to need each other.]

[Avyanna walks half a step behind Elisira, trying to match her pace.]

[Elisira moves like she belongs. Not because she's loud. Because she's certain.]

(How do you look like you belong when you're always waiting to be corrected.)

Elisira: Stay close enough that you can hear me.

Avyanna: [automatic] Yes.

[Avyanna catches herself.]

Avyanna: Why.

[Elisira glances at her, a tiny flicker of approval.]

Elisira: Because this is a funnel.

[She gestures without moving her hands-just a small tilt of chin toward a cluster of kiosks, a queue, and a row of "assistance desks" with people who look too eager.]

Elisira: Funnels are where predators hunt.

Avyanna: [quiet] Like the mine.

Elisira: Like anywhere people are scared and tired.

---

[They're here for a simple errand: pick up a stamped copy of a contract addendum. A paper artifact. A thing the Guild recognizes even if systems "glitch."]

[Avyanna hates that paper matters.]

(Paper matters because someone can take it away. Paper matters because it can be burned.)

[Elisira doesn't hate it. Elisira treats paper like a weapon with a handle.]

[They approach the counter.]

[A clerk smiles too widely.]

Clerk: Can I help you, miss-

[Her eyes flick to Avyanna's collar tag. Too fast to be casual.]

Clerk: -traveler.

[Avyanna's skin crawls.]

Elisira: [pleasant] We're here for a pickup. Code is on the slate.

[She slides the slate over. Not close enough for the clerk to snatch it.]

[The clerk's smile tightens.]

Clerk: We'll need to verify identity.

Avyanna: [tensing] Which-

Elisira: [cutting in, still pleasant] Under which jurisdiction.

[The clerk blinks.]

Clerk: Excuse me?

Elisira: You said "need." I'm asking: need by which rule. Enforced by whom.

[The clerk's eyes flick toward a security camera. Toward a uniform across the hall.]

[Then back.]

Clerk: It's... standard.

Elisira: Standard is not enforceable.

[The clerk's stylus taps twice against the counter.]

Clerk: Fine. Sign here.

[She prints a slip with a pen attached by chain.]

[The slip is a trap with polite edges: it asks for name, origin, and "additional contacts."]

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

(Additional contacts means: who can we threaten.)

Elisira: [to Avyanna, quiet] You don't sign anything you didn't request.

Avyanna: [whisper] I didn't request this.

Elisira: Correct.

[Elisira takes the pen-not from the clerk's hand, from the counter.]

Elisira: [pleasant] We're declining voluntary disclosure.

Clerk: It's required.

Elisira: Then cite it.

[Silence.]

[The clerk's smile becomes brittle.]

Clerk: If you don't cooperate, the pickup may be delayed.

[Avyanna's heart rate spikes.]

(Delay means loss. Loss means punishment. Loss means hunger.)

Elisira: Then delay it.

[She says it like: we have all day. Like time can't be weaponized here.]

Elisira: We'll file a complaint with the arbitration desk. We'll note the attempted coercion. We'll request the security footage.

[The clerk's eyes widen, just a fraction.]

[She reaches under the counter and produces the stamped packet like it's always been there.]

Clerk: No need to escalate.

Elisira: Good.

---

[They step away.]

[Avyanna's knees feel like they're trying to unlock.]

Avyanna: [breathing] You can just... do that.

Elisira: Yes.

Avyanna: Why didn't she-

Elisira: Because the clerk wasn't enforcing policy. She was testing a boundary.

Avyanna: Like a-

Elisira: Predator.

[Avyanna's hands tremble as she clutches the packet.]

Avyanna: What if she had called security.

Elisira: Then we'd have receipts.

Avyanna: That sounds like Vesper.

Elisira: Vesper taught me half of it. I taught her the other half. It's incestuous.

Avyanna: [a startled laugh] That's not-

Elisira: [deadpan] It's a metaphor. Do not repeat it.

---

[A man steps into their path.]

[Not uniformed. Not armed. Just... friendly.]

Man: Hey. You look lost. I know where the arbitration wing is. I can-

[Avyanna's mouth opens.]

[The mine reflex tries to offer politeness as payment.]

Elisira: No thank you.

Man: I'm just helping.

Elisira: We did not request help.

Man: Wow. Hostile.

Elisira: [pleasant] Correct.

[They walk around him.]

[Avyanna's chest tightens as if waiting for the hit that follows refusal.]

[None comes.]

[The man mutters something under his breath and moves on, scanning for softer prey.]

Avyanna: [quiet] No thank you.

Elisira: Yes.

Avyanna: That's... allowed.

Elisira: It's required.

[Avyanna looks at her.]

Elisira: For survival.

---

[Back at the docking tunnel, Avyanna exhales like she's been holding her breath since they left the ship.]

Avyanna: I kept thinking... if I'm rude, something bad happens.

Elisira: That's because something bad used to happen.

Avyanna: Does it ever stop feeling like-

Elisira: [cutting in, softer than usual] Yes.

[A pause.]

Elisira: Not fast. But yes.

[Avyanna nods.]

[She presses the stamped packet to her chest like it can keep her safe.]

(Paper is teeth. But sometimes it bites the right thing.)

---

[They don't go straight back.]

[Elisira takes the long route through the concourse on purpose.]

[Not because she likes crowds. Because she likes information.]

Avyanna: Why are we-

Elisira: Watching.

[They pause near a wall of public postings.]

[Not a bulletin board. A contract board. Smaller than the Guild's, but the same bones: jobs, fees, penalties, a thousand ways to make desperation look like choice.]

[Avyanna reads a posting aloud without thinking.]

Avyanna: "Processing surcharge applies to late submissions."

[Her stomach twists.]

(Processing surcharge. Mine word. Station word. Same shape.)

Elisira: What does that mean.

Avyanna: [automatic] It means... pay.

Elisira: That's the effect. Not the meaning.

Avyanna: [working] It means they're charging for time they didn't lose. They're charging for... being behind.

Elisira: Who benefits.

Avyanna: The office. The broker. Whoever owns the desk.

Elisira: Who enforces it.

Avyanna: The clerk. The system. The fact that if you miss a submission, you lose your claim.

Elisira: Good.

---

[A woman approaches them.]

[Mid-thirties, expensive coat, smile tuned for crowds. She carries a badge that looks real enough to be dangerous.]

Woman: You look like you're having trouble with the boards.

Elisira: No.

Woman: I can help you file. I know the system. I can get you processed faster.

[Avyanna's mouth goes dry.]

(Faster means: I own you now.)

[The woman's gaze flicks to Avyanna again. Too curious.]

Woman: You're new. You don't want to get tripped up by bureaucracy. It's such a shame when people lose claims over small mistakes.

[She smiles wider, as if pity is a gift.]

[Avyanna feels her body prepare the mine-script: apologize, agree, accept the hook.]

[Elisira doesn't raise her voice.]

Elisira: No thank you.

Woman: I'm not charging.

Elisira: Then you are lying.

[The woman's smile tightens.]

Woman: Excuse me?

Elisira: People who do labor for free in systems like this are buying something else.

Woman: I'm just-

Elisira: Helping.

Elisira: We did not request help.

[The woman's eyes harden, just for a blink. A tell.]

Avyanna: [quiet, to Elisira] Anger at refusal.

[Elisira's gaze flicks to her. Approval without softness.]

Elisira: Correct.

[The woman recovers her smile.]

Woman: You don't have to be rude.

Elisira: I do.

Woman: That's going to make you enemies.

Elisira: It's a public concourse. Everyone is already an enemy. The difference is which ones pretend.

[The woman's smile drops entirely.]

Woman: Fine. Good luck.

[She walks away quickly, scanning for someone who will trade fear for assistance.]

Avyanna: [breathing] I almost-

Elisira: Took the hook.

Avyanna: Yes.

Elisira: That's normal.

Avyanna: It doesn't feel normal.

Elisira: It feels like training data.

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: Am I... always going to be that easy.

Elisira: No.

Elisira: You just proved it.

---

[They walk back toward the docking tunnel.]

[Avyanna clutches the stamped packet less like a lifeline and more like evidence.]

(Politeness is wrapper. Evaluate the ask.)

[She repeats it silently until the words stop feeling like a borrowed tool and start feeling like hers.]
