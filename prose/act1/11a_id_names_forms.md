# Chapter 11a: ID, Names, and Forms

> **[SCENE - OCCURS BETWEEN CH11-12]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief secure conference room*
> *Present: Avyanna, Vesper, Elisira*
> *Teaching: Identity systems, paperwork as weapon, harm reduction through documentation*

---

[The conference room is smaller than Avyanna expected. One table, three chairs, a secure terminal, and walls that Cinnamon assures her are soundproofed.]

[Vesper sits with three different datapads arranged in front of her like cards in a game. Elisira stands near the door-not blocking it, just present.]

[Avyanna sits across from Vesper and tries not to look like she's calculating exits.]

Vesper: [calm] We're going to register you properly. Three systems. Three different identities. All legitimate. All protective.

Avyanna: [confused] Three?

Elisira: [from the door] Some people need five names to survive. Three is conservative.

---

[Vesper taps the first datapad.]

Vesper: System one: Rim Accord. This is your primary legal identity. The one that says you're a free citizen with full autonomy. No indenture contracts. No ownership claims. No extraction permissions.

[She pulls up a form. It's written in plain language-none of the labyrinthine jargon Avyanna remembers from the mine's paperwork.]

Vesper: Name, birthdate, origin station, current residence, occupation. Standard fields. But here's what matters: every piece of information you put here is public record. Anyone can request it. Compact, Guild, random busybody with a credit chit.

Avyanna: [tense] So they can find me.

Vesper: [precise] They can find this version of you. Which is why we're strategic about what this version says.

---

[Vesper walks through each field.]

Vesper: Name. You're using Avyanna Lagrange. That's your choice. If you wanted to change it, now's the time.

Avyanna: [quiet] I want to keep it. It's... mine.

Vesper: [nodding] Understood. Birthdate. The mine didn't track yours. So you pick one. Any date. It's yours now.

[Avyanna stares at the calendar field.]

Avyanna: I don't... how do I choose?

Elisira: [dryly] Pick a date that makes you hard to profile. Not a holiday. Not a memorable anniversary. Something ordinary.

Avyanna: [after a moment] Third month, fourteenth day. Standard year... I don't know. Seventeen years ago?

Vesper: [entering it] Close enough. We'll backdate your age to seventeen. Young enough that gaps in your record are explainable, old enough that you have legal autonomy.

---

Vesper: Origin station. This one's strategic. We're not listing Aurum Extraction. We're listing Taelios Forge-a mid-tier manufacturing station with lousy record-keeping. If anyone checks, the station will shrug and say "records incomplete due to fire in archive seven years ago."

Avyanna: [slowly] You're... lying?

Vesper: [meeting her eyes] I'm protecting you with plausible deniability. Taelios is real. The fire is real. Your presence there is unfalsifiable because the records genuinely don't exist.

Elisira: [adding] This is harm reduction. We're giving you a past that doesn't make you a target.

---

Vesper: Current residence: Lumen Thief, Guild registry 7742-KAEL. Occupation: apprentice evidence contractor.

[She pauses.]

Vesper: That last one is important. "Apprentice" means you're learning, not experienced. Keeps expectations low. "Evidence contractor" means you work with the Guild but aren't Guild property. You're crew, not asset.

Avyanna: [testing the words] Crew. Not asset.

Vesper: [firm] Crew. With full autonomy. This form makes that official.

---

[Vesper taps through the rest of the fields. Medical information: minimal disclosure. Education: "station standard, records incomplete." Emergency contacts: Lumen Thief crew roster.]

[She submits the form. A confirmation code appears.]

Vesper: That's identity one. Rim Accord now recognizes you as a free citizen. If anyone questions your status, you show them this registry number. It's your proof of autonomy.

[Avyanna stares at the number. Seventeen characters. Hers.]

---

[Vesper switches to the second datapad.]

Vesper: System two: Guild transit credentials. This one's more restricted. Only Guild-affiliated stations can access it. But it gives you freedom of movement on Guild routes without customs hassles.

[Another form. This one asks for biometrics.]

Vesper: We'll need a retinal scan, fingerprints, and a voiceprint. All stored encrypted. Only released to Guild checkpoints, and only when you authorize it.

Avyanna: [wary] What if I don't authorize it?

Vesper: Then you don't get through the checkpoint. But that's your choice. You control when your biometrics are shared.

---

[Elisira steps forward with a small scanner.]

Elisira: This won't hurt. Look here. Don't blink.

[Flash. Avyanna's retina captured in a quarter-second. Fingerprints next-each finger pressed to a cool glass surface. Then voiceprint: Avyanna speaks her name, her registry number, and a randomized passphrase.]

[All of it stored. All of it hers. All of it controlled by her consent.]

Vesper: [submitting] Guild transit credentials issued. You can now move through Guild space with minimal friction.

---

[Vesper picks up the third datapad. Pauses.]

Vesper: System three is different. This one is... insurance.

Avyanna: [sensing weight] Insurance?

Vesper: [carefully] Some people need a name that isn't connected to their primary identity. A name they can use when the first two are compromised. A name that lets them disappear if necessary.

Elisira: [from the door] We call it an egress identity. A way out when the walls close in.

---

[Avyanna's stomach tightens.]

Avyanna: You think I'll need to disappear?

Vesper: [honest] I think you might. The Compact knows you exist. Aurum Extraction knows you exist. Right now, they don't know where you are. But if they find out, you'll need options.

[She slides the third datapad across the table.]

Vesper: This identity is minimal. Different name. Different birthdate. Different origin. Same species, same approximate age, but everything else is fiction. We'll register it with a neutral station that doesn't share data automatically.

Vesper: You'll never use it unless you have to. But if you need it, it'll be there.

---

[Avyanna stares at the blank form.]

Avyanna: What name do I use?

Vesper: Your choice. Pick something that doesn't emotionally attach to you. This identity is a tool, not a person.

[Avyanna thinks. Names from the mine. Names from stations she's seen. Something ordinary. Something forgettable.]

Avyanna: [slowly] Kess Tam.

Vesper: [approving] Good. Generic. Unremarkable. Birthdate?

Avyanna: Eighth month, second day. Eighteen years ago.

Vesper: Origin?

Avyanna: [looking at Vesper] You choose. I don't know what's safe.

Vesper: [noting] Miren's Cradle. Agricultural station. Large population, high turnover, minimal surveillance. Perfect for ghosts.

---

[They fill out the rest. Minimal details. No medical history. No education records. No emergency contacts. Just a name, a face, and a birthdate that might exist somewhere in the statistical noise of the galaxy.]

[Vesper submits it. Another confirmation code. Another identity, stored and waiting.]

Vesper: [meeting Avyanna's eyes] You now have three names. Three versions of yourself, each with different utility and different exposure.

Vesper: Avyanna Lagrange, Rim Accord citizen: this is who you are publicly.

Vesper: Avyanna Lagrange, Guild contractor: this is who you are professionally.

Vesper: Kess Tam: this is who you become if everything else fails.

---

[Avyanna's hands are shaking. Three names. Three lives. Three ways to exist.]

Avyanna: [whispered] How do I remember which one to use?

Elisira: [stepping forward] Practice. We'll drill scenarios. "You're at a Rim station, customs asks for ID"-you show Rim Accord. "You're at a Guild checkpoint"-you show Guild credentials. "You're running and someone's hunting you"-you become Kess Tam and you don't look back.

---

[Vesper closes the datapads.]

Vesper: Now the hard part. Learning what information you're willing to make public, and what you protect.

[She pulls up a sample form-a station customs declaration.]

Vesper: This form asks: name, origin, destination, purpose of visit, duration of stay, and "any goods to declare."

[She taps each field.]

Vesper: Name: you give them your public identity. Avyanna Lagrange.

Vesper: Origin: you say "Lumen Thief, Guild registry 7742-KAEL." You don't elaborate.

Vesper: Destination: you tell them the station name. You don't tell them who you're visiting or why.

Vesper: Purpose: you say "transit" or "Guild contract work." You don't give specifics.

Vesper: Duration: you estimate conservatively. You don't commit to leaving by a date.

Vesper: Goods: you declare what's required by law. You don't volunteer information.

---

[Avyanna absorbs this.]

Avyanna: [slow] You're teaching me to... give the minimum?

Vesper: [precise] I'm teaching you that you're not required to tell strangers your life story. Information is leverage. Don't hand it out for free.

[She pulls up another form-this one from a Compact administrative office.]

Vesper: This one asks: "Are you now or have you ever been employed by an extraction facility?"

[Avyanna's breath catches.]

Vesper: What do you say?

Avyanna: [shaking] I... was. In the mine.

Vesper: [gently] That's the truth. But truth has nuance. You were *held* by an extraction facility. You were not employed-you were enslaved. The word "employed" implies consent, compensation, and autonomy. You had none of those.

Vesper: So the accurate answer is: "No. I was held against my will by Aurum Extraction. I was never an employee."

---

[Avyanna stares at the form.]

Avyanna: [quiet] They'll say I'm lying.

Vesper: [flat] Let them. Your answer is defensible. You're reframing their language to reflect reality. That's not lying-that's refusing to adopt their narrative.

Elisira: [adding] And if they push, you say: "That's not your business. Next question."

---

[They practice. Twenty forms. Twenty scenarios. Each one designed to teach Avyanna what information is required, what's optional, and what's none of anyone's damn business.]

**Scenario: Station Security**
- Form asks: "List all previous residences for the past five years."
- Avyanna: [using the script] "I was held at Aurum Extraction under indenture. I have no other residences to report."
- Vesper: Good. You answered truthfully without giving them ammunition.

**Scenario: Medical Intake**
- Form asks: "Describe any pre-existing conditions or injuries."
- Avyanna: [using the script] "I'll discuss that with my doctor. Not with your intake form."
- Vesper: Correct. Medical history is between you and your provider. Intake forms don't get access.

**Scenario: Banking**
- Form asks: "Source of funds?"
- Avyanna: [using the script] "Wages from Guild contract work."
- Vesper: Perfect. Accurate, minimal, unremarkable.

---

[After an hour, Avyanna is exhausted. But something in her posture has shifted. She's learning to hold boundaries in bureaucratic spaces.]

Vesper: [standing] Last lesson. The most important one.

[She pulls up a form. This one is from a Compact regulatory office.]

Vesper: This form asks you to "voluntarily disclose" information about Primordial Lattice interactions. It's phrased like a survey. It's actually a registry of people the Compact wants to track.

[She deletes the form without filling it out.]

Vesper: You never fill out "voluntary disclosure" forms. Ever. If disclosure is truly voluntary, you volunteer to decline.

---

[Avyanna nods slowly.]

Avyanna: What if they say it's required?

Vesper: [precise] Then you ask: "Required by which law? Under which jurisdiction? Enforced by whom?"

[She taps the table.]

Vesper: If they can't answer all three, it's not required. It's a request dressed up as a mandate. And you decline.

Elisira: [adding] And if they try to coerce you-if they threaten consequences for declining-you document it and you report it. Because that's not law. That's extortion.

---

[Avyanna sits back. Three identities. Boundaries in forms. Scripts for refusal.]

Avyanna: [quiet] You're teaching me to hide.

Vesper: [correcting] I'm teaching you to control what people know about you. There's a difference between hiding and exercising informational autonomy.

Vesper: You're not erasing yourself. You're deciding which version of yourself is appropriate for which context.

---

[Avyanna processes that.]

Avyanna: The mine wanted me to have no privacy. No secrets. No control over my own information.

Vesper: [nodding] Yes. Because control over information is power. They wanted all the power. We're giving it back to you.

[She hands Avyanna a small data chip.]

Vesper: These are your credentials. All three identities, encrypted. Keep it on you. Memorize the access codes. If you ever need to use Kess Tam, you'll have everything you need.

---

[Avyanna takes the chip. It's small. Lighter than it should be for the weight it carries.]

Avyanna: [looking at Vesper] Have you ever had to use... an egress identity?

Vesper: [after a pause] Yes. Twice.

Avyanna: Did it work?

Vesper: [quietly] I'm still here.
