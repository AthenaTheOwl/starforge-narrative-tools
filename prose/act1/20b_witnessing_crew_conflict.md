# Chapter 20b: Witnessing Crew Conflict (Without Violence)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH20, AFTER CONTESTED CONTRACT]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief common room*
> *Present: Avyanna (witnessing), Vesper, Rho (in conflict)*
> *Purpose: Seeing healthy disagreement, learning conflict can exist without danger*

---

[Avyanna hears the argument before she sees it.]

[Raised voices from the common room. Not screaming. Not violence-adjacent. But definitely argument-level volume. Her stomach clenches. Conflict = danger = someone gets hurt.]

[She should leave. Go to her quarters. Not witness whatever's about to happen.]

[But she's frozen in the corridor. Listening. Afraid.]

---

Rho: [frustrated, loud] -that's not the point! The point is you made the call without consulting the crew!

Vesper: [controlled but firm] I made the call because waiting would have cost us the contract. Time-sensitive decisions don't get committee approval.

Rho: [angry] This wasn't time-sensitive! You had six hours before client deadline! You could have asked!

Vesper: [cold] I could have. I chose not to. Because I'm the contractor lead and operational decisions are my jurisdiction.

Rho: [pointing] That's horseshit and you know it! We operate on consensus! That's the whole fucking point of this crew!

---

[Avyanna's breathing is shallow. This is how it starts. Argument escalates. Someone throws the first hit. Someone gets hurt. Someone-]

[She should intervene. Or run. Or something. But she's frozen.]

[Elia walks past her, entering the common room without hesitation.]

Elia: [calm, to Rho and Vesper] Are we having a loud conversation or a fight?

Vesper: [not looking away from Rho] Loud conversation.

Rho: [frustrated] Loud conversation that should have happened six hours ago before Vesper decided to act unilaterally.

Elia: [sitting on the couch, unbothered] Okay. Continue. I'm listening.

---

[Avyanna stares. Elia just... walked into an argument. Sat down. Didn't try to stop it. Didn't flee.]

[Jalen appears beside Avyanna in the corridor. Sees her frozen. Sees the argument in the common room.]

Jalen: [quiet] They're fighting. Not fighting-fighting. Just disagreeing loudly.

Avyanna: [whispered] Someone's going to get hurt.

Jalen: [shaking their head] No one's getting hurt. Watch.

[They walk past her into the common room. Sit beside Elia. Also just... listening.]

---

Vesper: [to Rho] The client offered a time-limited bonus. Complete the contract six hours early, get an extra five thousand credits. I assessed risk, determined it was manageable, accepted the modification.

Rho: [pointing] YOU assessed. YOU determined. Not WE. That's the problem.

Vesper: [measured] Would the crew consensus have been different?

Rho: [pause] ...Probably not. But that's not the point!

Vesper: [leaning forward] Then what IS the point? Because if the outcome would have been the same, this is about process, not substance.

Rho: [frustrated] YES! It's about process! It's about you not treating the crew like assets you manage! We're supposed to be equals!

---

[Nyx appears beside Avyanna. Doesn't say anything. Just gestures: want to come sit, or want to stay here?]

[Avyanna follows Nyx into the common room. Sits on the far end of the couch. Tense. Ready to run.]

[The argument continues.]

Vesper: [to Rho] I don't treat you like assets. I treat you like crew I trust to handle what I delegate. You're upset because I made an operational decision that fell within my role parameters.

Rho: [standing, pacing] I'm upset because you're doing the thing again! The thing where you decide something affects all of us and you don't tell anyone until it's done!

Vesper: [voice rising] Because telling you MID-OPERATION would have been worse! You would have questioned! Debated! We would have missed the deadline!

Rho: [shouting] THEN WE MISS THE DEADLINE! That's the cost of consensus!

---

[Silence. Heavy. Charged.]

[Avyanna's hands are clenched. This is where violence starts. This is where someone gets hurt. This is-]

Vesper: [after a long moment, quieter] You're right.

[Avyanna blinks. Wait. What?]

Vesper: [sitting back, rubbing her face] You're right. I acted unilaterally because I didn't want to risk missing the bonus. I prioritized money over process. That's not how we operate.

Rho: [stopping pacing, surprised] ...Oh.

Vesper: [looking at him] I'm sorry. I should have consulted the crew, even if it meant missing the deadline. The process matters more than five thousand credits.

---

[Rho sits down heavily.]

Rho: [quieter] I'm not trying to undermine you. I trust your judgment. I just... I need to be included. We all do.

Vesper: [nodding] I know. And I violated that. I won't do it again.

[Pause.]

Rho: [reluctant] The contract modification was probably the right call. We got the bonus. No one got hurt. I just wish you'd told us before you accepted it.

Vesper: [measured] Next time, I will. Even if it's mid-op. Even if it costs us.

Rho: [sighing] I don't want it to cost us. I just want to know what's happening.

Vesper: [meeting his eyes] Understood. I'll do better.

---

[Silence again. But different. Not charged. Just... processing.]

[Elia speaks for the first time since sitting down.]

Elia: [to both of them] Are we resolved, or do we need to keep talking?

Rho: [to Vesper] We resolved?

Vesper: [nodding] For now. If you're still angry tomorrow, we revisit.

Rho: [considering] I'm not angry. I'm just... annoyed. But I'll get over it.

Vesper: [slight smile] Fair.

---

[The tension breaks. Rho stands, goes to the galley, comes back with water. Hands one to Vesper without comment. She takes it. They drink in silence that's no longer hostile.]

[Avyanna is still frozen on the couch. Trying to process what she just witnessed.]

(They argued. Loudly. They were angry. And then... they resolved it. Without violence. Without punishment. Vesper apologized. Rho accepted. They're just... sitting here. Drinking water. Still crew.)

[Her brain is short-circuiting.]

---

Nyx: [beside her, quiet] First time witnessing crew conflict?

Avyanna: [barely audible] Yes.

Nyx: [nodding] It's disorienting. Conflict without violence. Takes time to believe it's real.

[Jalen turns to Avyanna.]

Jalen: [measured] That's what healthy disagreement looks like. They had a problem. They yelled about it. They found the root issue. They resolved it. No one got hurt. No one got fired. Just... conflict and resolution.

Avyanna: [shaking] But Rho shouted. And Vesper shouted back. In the mine, that would mean-

Elia: [interrupting] This isn't the mine. Here, people are allowed to be angry. Allowed to disagree. Allowed to raise their voices when they're frustrated.

Elia: [meeting her eyes] The difference is what happens after. In the mine, anger led to violence because violence was the tool of control. Here, anger leads to conversation because conversation is our tool of resolution.

---

[Rho turns to Avyanna.]

Rho: [gentler] Hey. Sorry you witnessed that. I know it probably scared you.

Avyanna: [uncertain] I thought... I thought you were going to hurt each other.

Rho: [shaking his head] I was angry. Vesper was defensive. But we're crew. We don't hurt each other. We just... process things loudly sometimes.

Vesper: [adding] Conflict is normal. Inevitable, even. The question isn't whether conflict happens. It's how you handle it.

Vesper: [to Avyanna] You just saw us handle it. Poorly at first-we both escalated. But then we de-escalated. Communicated. Apologized where appropriate. Resolved the actual issue.

---

[Avyanna processes. Conflict -> communication -> resolution. Without violence. Without punishment.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Can I ask a question?

Vesper: [nodding] Always.

Avyanna: [hesitant] What if... what if you couldn't resolve it? What if the disagreement was too big?

Rho: [considering] Then we'd table it. Cool off. Revisit later with mediator help. Probably Nyx or Elia-someone neutral who can facilitate.

Rho: [firm] What we wouldn't do is let it fester. And we wouldn't use violence. Ever. That's the floor.

---

[Vesper stands. Stretches.]

Vesper: [to Rho] We good?

Rho: [nodding] We're good.

Vesper: [to the room] I'm filing a note in Sparkle that I acted unilaterally and violated crew consensus protocols. For the record.

Rho: [surprised] You don't have to-

Vesper: [firm] Yes I do. Accountability isn't optional.

[She pulls out her datapad. Types. Posts to the Sparkle board.]

**COMPLAINT #849: I (Vesper) made an operational decision without crew consultation. Violated consensus protocols for time-sensitive opportunity. Rho called me on it. He was right. I'm committing to better communication going forward. -Vesper**

---

[The room is quiet. Avyanna stares at the Sparkle board entry projected on the wall.]

(Vesper complained about herself. Documented her own mistake. Publicly. For accountability.)

[Rho reads it. Nods.]

Rho: [to Vesper] Thanks. That matters.

Vesper: [clipped] It's procedure. You were right to call it out.

[She leaves. The common room exhales.]

---

[Rho sits beside Avyanna.]

Rho: [gentle] You okay?

Avyanna: [processing] I don't... I don't understand what I just saw.

Rho: [patient] What part?

Avyanna: [gesturing] All of it. You fought. Then you resolved. Then Vesper apologized. Then she documented it. No one got hurt. No one got fired. You're still crew.

Rho: [nodding] Yeah. That's how it works here.

Avyanna: [shaking] That's not how it worked in the mine.

Rho: [quietly] I know. The mine used violence to control conflict. Here, we use communication. It's messier. Louder sometimes. But no one bleeds.

---

[Avyanna sits with that. Conflict without blood. Disagreement without danger.]

[Elia stands.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] You witnessed your first crew argument. They'll happen again. Different people. Different issues. But the pattern is the same: conflict, communication, resolution, documentation.

Elia: [meeting her eyes] You're safe here. Even when people are angry. Even when voices are raised. You're safe.

[Elia leaves. Then Jalen. Then Nyx. One by one, the crew returns to their tasks. Like nothing catastrophic happened. Because nothing catastrophic happened. Just conflict. And resolution.]

[Rho stays with Avyanna.]

Rho: [after a moment] Want to talk about it? Or want space?

Avyanna: [quiet] Can you just... stay? For a bit?

Rho: [settling back into the couch] Yeah. I can do that.

---

[They sit. Avyanna processes. Rho exists nearby, not demanding anything.]

[After twenty minutes, Avyanna speaks.]

Avyanna: [tentative] You were really angry. I could hear it.

Rho: [nodding] I was. Vesper made a decision that affected everyone without asking. That feels like a violation of trust. So yeah, I was pissed.

Avyanna: [careful] But you're not angry now.

Rho: [considering] Less angry. Still a little annoyed. But she apologized. She acknowledged the problem. She committed to changing. That's enough for me to let it go.

Avyanna: [quiet] Just like that?

Rho: [meeting her eyes] Just like that. Because holding onto anger when the other person is trying to fix the problem? That's just hurting yourself. And Vesper's not perfect. None of us are. But she listens when she's wrong. That's what matters.

---

[Avyanna nods slowly.]

(They fought. They resolved. They're still crew. No one got hurt. No one got exiled. Just... human conflict. And human resolution.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(The hum at the base of her skull eases: conflict can be safe; conflict can resolve.)

[Eventually, Rho stands.]

Rho: [stretching] I'm gonna go fix that thruster issue I've been avoiding. You good?

Avyanna: [nodding] Yeah. I'm good.

Rho: [grinning] Good. Because next time Vesper and I argue, you'll probably see it again. We're both stubborn. It's genetic.

[He leaves. Avyanna sits alone in the common room.]

[She thinks about the argument. About Vesper apologizing. About Rho forgiving. About Sparkle documentation. About conflict without violence.]

[And slowly, incrementally, she's learning that anger doesn't have to end in blood. Sometimes it just ends in conversation. And apologies. And crew still being crew.]
