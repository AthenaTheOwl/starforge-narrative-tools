# Chapter 16e: Senna's Math

> **[INTERSTITIAL - OCCURS POST-CH16]**
> *Trigger: numbers as a weapon against contracts*
> *Purpose: Senna's version of "show me the math"*

---

[Rho and Cinnamon taught her compound interest like it was a knife safety lesson.]

[Not scary.]

[Necessary.]

[Avyanna walked away from the galley with her head full of percentages that felt like teeth.]

[Now she lies awake, staring at the ceiling, repeating numbers like prayers she doesn't believe in.]

Twenty-two percent annual.

Monthly compounding.

Hidden fees that aren't hidden if you know where to look.

(If they won't show you the math, they're lying.)

[The sentence is Rho's.]

[But it scrapes at something older in her memory.]

---

## Intercut - AE Site K-9 (Senna's Ledger)

[Senna doesn't have a datapad.]

[Datapads belong to supervisors.]

[Senna has a strip of packing paper stolen from the supply bay and a chunk of charcoal.] 

[Avyanna sits beside her in the shadow of a support column, knees tucked to her chest.]

Avyanna: Why are we doing this.

Senna: Because you're curious.

Avyanna: Curiosity gets people hurt.

Senna: Curiosity gets people out.

[Senna draws a line, then another, making a crude table.]

Senna: Here is what they tell you.

[She writes:]

QUOTA: 100

PAY: 0

[Avyanna snorts quietly.]

Avyanna: That's not math.

Senna: It is.

Senna: It's just a lie-shaped one.

[Senna writes below it:]

PENALTY: -5

PENALTY: -5

PENALTY: -5

[Avyanna watches the list grow.]

Avyanna: That's what they do.

Senna: Yes.

Senna: Now watch the trick.

[Senna circles the penalties.]

Senna: They call it "compliance."

Senna: They call it "training."

Senna: But it's interest.

[Avyanna frowns.]

Avyanna: We don't get paid.

Senna: You get charged.

Senna: Debt doesn't care if you ever saw money.

[Senna draws an arrow.]

Senna: If you owe five today, you owe seven tomorrow.

Avyanna: Why.

Senna: Because the number is a weapon.

Senna: The weapon works best when you don't understand it.

---

[Avyanna's throat tightens.]

Avyanna: I can't do that math.

Senna: You can.

[Senna taps the paper.]

Senna: Shortcut.

Senna: If they refuse to show you how the number changes, assume it changes against you.

Senna: If they show you a number once and never again, assume it's moving.

Senna: If they tell you "it's standard," assume "standard" means "profitable."

[Avyanna stares.]

Avyanna: That's... not math.

Senna: It's survival.

[Senna leans in.]

Senna: Real math comes later.

Senna: First you learn the rule.

Senna: If they won't show you the math, they're lying.

[Avyanna swallows the sentence like it's contraband food.]

---

## Return - Ship Ceiling

[Avyanna's eyes snap open.]

[The ceiling is clean.]

[The air doesn't taste like metal.]

[But the sentence is still there, bright and sharp as a tool.]

(If they won't show you the math, they're lying.)

[She sits up.]

[Finds the notebook from Senna's "small good thing" rule.]

[On the next page, she writes:]

If they won't show you the math, they're lying.

[Under it, smaller:]

Ask where the number comes from.

Ask who benefits when it changes.

[Avyanna closes the notebook and rests her forehead against it.]

[She doesn't feel smart.]

[She feels armed.]

---
*End Chapter 16e*
