# Chapter 13b: Coffee Ritual

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH13, EARLY MORNING]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief galley*
> *Present: Avyanna, Rho, Jalen*
> *Purpose: Domestic routine, belonging without performance*

---

[0530 ship time. The galley is dim except for the work lights over the counter.]

[Avyanna wakes early because she still doesn't know how to sleep past dawn. The mine taught her that sleeping late meant missing rations. The Lumen Thief has no such rule, but her body doesn't believe it yet.]

[She enters the galley expecting it to be empty.]

[It's not.]

---

[Rho stands at the counter with his back to the door, doing something that involves the coffee maker, cursing, and what sounds like percussive maintenance.]

[Jalen sits at the table with a datapad, not looking up, radiating the particular stillness of someone who's been awake for hours and plans to stay that way.]

[Avyanna freezes in the doorway.]

Rho: [not turning around] Come in or don't. Door's open either way.

[Avyanna steps in. The door hisses closed behind her.]

---

[Rho is making coffee. Badly. The machine hisses and spits like it's personally offended.]

Rho: [to the machine] I know you don't like me. The feeling is mutual. But we're doing this anyway.

[The machine gurgles. Something that might be coffee drips into a carafe.]

Jalen: [still not looking up] You're going to break it again.

Rho: [cheerful] It's already broken. I'm just negotiating with the corpse.

---

[Avyanna hovers near the table. Uncertain. The mine had schedules. Mealtimes. Assigned seats. She doesn't know the rules here.]

Jalen: [finally glancing up] Sit. Or stand. Or leave. All acceptable.

[The words are permission. Avyanna sits.]

[Jalen returns to their datapad. Avyanna watches Rho wage war with the coffee maker.]

Rho: [to the machine] Yes. I know. You want the good beans. You're getting station-grade swill. Cope.

[The machine makes a noise like dying hydraulics.]

---

[Eventually, Rho wins. He pours three cups of something that looks like coffee and probably contains caffeine. Maybe.]

[He sets one in front of Jalen. One in front of Avyanna. Keeps the third for himself.]

Rho: [sitting] Fair warning: it's terrible.

Jalen: [taking a sip without looking] Always is.

Avyanna: [staring at her cup] I don't... I've never had coffee.

Rho: [grinning] Then you don't know what you're missing. Which means this won't disappoint you.

---

[Avyanna picks up the cup. It's warm. The smell is bitter and sharp and foreign.]

[She takes a cautious sip.]

[It tastes like burnt dirt and regret.]

[She swallows anyway because wasting food-wasting anything-feels like a crime.]

Rho: [watching her face] You hate it.

Avyanna: [lying] It's fine.

Rho: [laughing] You're a terrible liar. It's okay to hate it. Half the crew hates my coffee. The other half is lying.

Jalen: [dryly] I don't hate it. I'm just dead inside.

---

[Avyanna sets the cup down carefully.]

Avyanna: [hesitant] Do you... have tea?

Rho: [standing immediately] Yes. Why didn't you say so?

Avyanna: [confused] I thought... I thought I had to drink what you made.

[Rho pauses. Looks at her. Something in his expression shifts-recognition of an old wound.]

Rho: [carefully] You don't. You can drink whatever you want. Or nothing. Your call.

---

[He pulls out a different container. Tea bags in six different varieties. Sets them in front of her.]

Rho: Pick one. Or don't. I'm not your supervisor.

[Avyanna stares at the options. In the mine, you got what you got. Here, she has six choices and no idea how to choose.]

Jalen: [without looking up] Green tea's mild. Black tea's strong. Herbal tea isn't actually tea but people call it that anyway.

Avyanna: [pointing] Green. Please.

Rho: [making it] Good choice. Least likely to taste like dirt.

---

[He makes the tea. Sets it in front of her. This time, when she sips, it's... better. Still strange. But better.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Thank you.

Rho: [sitting back down] Don't thank me. Just... drink what you like. That's how it works here.

[They sit in silence. Rho drinks his terrible coffee with evident satisfaction. Jalen reads their datapad. Avyanna holds her tea and tries to understand what's happening.]

---

[This isn't... anything. It's not a meeting. Not a lesson. Not a task.]

[It's just three people in a room at 0530, drinking things, existing near each other.]

[No one is performing. No one is proving anything.]

Avyanna: [carefully] Do you do this every morning?

Rho: [nodding] Most mornings. Jalen's always up early. I'm always up early. We collide in the galley and pretend we're not hiding from our own thoughts.

Jalen: [finally looking up] I'm not hiding. I'm working.

Rho: [to Avyanna] They're hiding.

Jalen: [returning to their datapad] This conversation is beneath me.

---

[Avyanna almost smiles. The motion feels strange on her face.]

[She takes another sip of tea. It's warmer now. Less foreign.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Can I... come back tomorrow?

Rho: [surprised] You don't need permission. Galley's open. Coffee's terrible. Tea's mediocre. We'll be here.

Jalen: [adding] Same time. Same silence. Same existential dread.

Rho: [throwing a napkin at him] You're a ray of sunshine.

Jalen: [catching it without looking] I know.

---

[They fall back into silence. Comfortable. Unforced.]

[Avyanna drinks her tea. Rho reads something on his datapad and mutters about supply costs. Jalen's stylus moves across their screen, plotting something that probably involves math Avyanna doesn't understand yet.]

[No one demands anything. No one expects anything.]

[She's just... here. And that's allowed.]

---

[At 0600, Jalen stands.]

Jalen: [to Rho] Shift change in thirty. Don't forget the gyro calibration.

Rho: [saluting lazily] Aye aye, navigator.

[Jalen nods to Avyanna.]

Jalen: [to her] See you at 0530 tomorrow. Or don't. Either way.

[They leave. The door hisses shut behind them.]

---

[Rho refills his coffee. Doesn't offer Avyanna more tea. She's still working on the first cup.]

Rho: [after a moment] In the mine, you didn't get to choose what you drank, did you.

Avyanna: [shaking her head] No. You got water. Or the stuff they called juice. It wasn't juice.

Rho: [grimacing] Yeah. I've tasted extraction-grade "juice." It's a war crime.

[Rho sets down his own mug. Deliberate.]

Rho: Here, you get to choose. Every time. Coffee, tea, water, that terrible synthetic fruit drink Elia likes. Whatever. It's yours to decide.

---

[Avyanna looks at her tea.]

Avyanna: [quiet] I don't know how to... have preferences.

Rho: [leaning back] Then you practice. You try green tea. Tomorrow you try black tea. Next week you try herbal. Eventually you figure out what you like.

Rho: And if you never figure it out? If you just keep drinking whatever's available? That's fine too. Preferences aren't mandatory. They're just... allowed.

---

[Avyanna absorbs this. Preferences as optional. Choice as practice, not test.]

Avyanna: [hesitant] What if I choose wrong?

Rho: [snorting] It's tea, kid. Not surgery. Worst case, you don't like it and you try something else tomorrow.

[He stands, rinses his cup, sets it in the cleaning rack.]

Rho: [turning back] The mine taught you that every choice has consequences, right? That choosing wrong means punishment?

Avyanna: [barely audible] Yes.

Rho: [nodding] Yeah. Thought so.

[He sits back down. Looks at her directly.]

Rho: Here's the thing. Choosing the wrong tea isn't a mistake. It's information. "I don't like black tea" is useful data. It narrows your options. Makes future choices easier.

Rho: The mine wanted you to be terrified of choosing. Because terrified people don't ask for things. They just take what they're given and call it mercy.

---

[Avyanna's hands tighten around her cup.]

Avyanna: [shaking] They said... they said we were lucky. To have rations at all.

Rho: [flat] They lied.

Rho: You weren't lucky. You were exploited. There's a difference.

[His voice drops. Not softer. Heavier.]

Rho: And here, you get to choose your own damn tea. Because that's what people do. They have preferences. They express them. They change their minds. And nobody punishes them for it.

---

[Avyanna sits with that. The tea is cooling in her hands. The galley is quiet except for the ship's ambient hum.]

[She finishes the tea. Sets the cup down.]

Avyanna: [standing] I should... I should go. Let you work.

Rho: [waving her off] I'm not working. I'm procrastinating. You're fine.

[But he doesn't stop her from leaving.]

[Avyanna reaches the door. Pauses.]

Avyanna: [not turning around] Same time tomorrow?

Rho: [grinning] Same time. Same terrible coffee. Same slightly-better tea.

---

[Avyanna leaves. The door hisses shut behind her.]

[In the corridor, she leans against the bulkhead and breathes.]

(I chose tea. I didn't like the coffee. I said so. And nothing bad happened.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm. Quiet. Content.]

(The warmth at the base of her skull steadies, a wordless reassurance she translates anyway: choice, yours, safe.)

[She pushes off the wall and heads back to her quarters.]

[Tomorrow, she'll try black tea. Just to see.]
