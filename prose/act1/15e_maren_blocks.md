# Chapter 15e: Maren's Blocks

> **[INTERSTITIAL - OCCURS POST-CH15]**
> *Trigger: Elisira teaches "pattern" like it's a language*
> *Purpose: show Maren Sova's foundation without confirming her fate*

---

[Elisira's lessons always look like paperwork until you realize they're knives.]

[After the ghost net drill, Avyanna finds Elisira at the galley table with a small tin of colored blocks spread before her.]

[Not children's toys.]

[Something more deliberate-hard-edged pieces, each one marked with a tiny notch.]

[Elisira sorts them into rows.]

[Blue.]

[Red.]

[Yellow.]

[Then she flips two pieces without changing her expression.]

[The whole row reads differently.]

Avyanna: [cautious] What is that.

Elisira: Encryption.

Avyanna: With blocks.

Elisira: With patterns.

[Avyanna watches her hands.]

[They're steady.]

[Not the steady of calm.]

[The steady of someone who learned to survive by making systems behave.]

Avyanna: Who taught you.

[Elisira's hands pause.]

[A beat too long.]

Elisira: Someone.

---

## Intercut - Age Eleven (Maren's Table)

[The apartment is small.]

[Station-small: air that tastes like recycled promises, walls thin enough that you can hear the neighbors' arguments through them.]

[Elisira sits at a table with her feet not quite reaching the floor.]

[Her hands are stained with marker ink.]

[Across from her, Maren Sova lays down three colored blocks like she's dealing cards.]

Maren: Rule one.

Elisira: [automatic] Don't trust a label.

[Maren's mouth twitches.]

Maren: Good.

Maren: Rule two.

Elisira: Don't trust a story.

Maren: Better.

Maren: Rule three.

[Elisira hesitates.]

[Maren waits.]

[Not impatient.]

[Just present, the way a good guardian is present-no pressure, no punishment, only expectation.]

Elisira: Trust the pattern.

Maren: Yes.

---

[Maren taps the blocks.]

Maren: This is a message.

Elisira: It's colors.

Maren: Colors are symbols.

Maren: Symbols become rules when someone enforces them.

Maren: Who enforces this.

Elisira: [frowning] You.

Maren: Here.

[Maren slides the blocks across the table, out of her own reach.]

Maren: Now who enforces it.

[Elisira stares.]

[The answer lands slow and bright.]

Elisira: Me.

[Maren nods.]

Maren: Good.

Maren: Remember that.

Maren: Most cages are built from things people agree to enforce for free.

---

[Elisira's throat tightens.]

Elisira: Why are you teaching me this.

Maren: Because you notice.

Maren: And because one day you'll need to hide in plain sight.

Elisira: That sounds like leaving.

[Maren's eyes flicker-too fast to be a confession, too slow to be nothing.]

Maren: It sounds like being prepared.

Elisira: Prepared for what.

Maren: [quiet] For a galaxy that lies.

---

[Maren reaches over and brushes Elisira's hair back from her forehead.]

[Touch offered, not taken.]

Maren: You can hate me if I disappear.

[Elisira freezes.]

Elisira: What.

Maren: You can decide I abandoned you.

Maren: If that story keeps you alive.

[Elisira's eyes sting.]

Elisira: Don't say that.

Maren: I'm saying it because I don't get to control what you need later.

Maren: I only get to give you tools now.

---

## Return - Galley (Now)

[Elisira's fingers move again.]

[She flips a block.]

[The pattern changes.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Is it easier when it's colors.

Elisira: It was.

Avyanna: Was.

[Elisira looks at her for one second.]

Elisira: It's easier when the rules are honest.

Avyanna: The galaxy isn't.

Elisira: [flat] Correct.

[Elisira turns a block over in her fingers, considering its weight.]

Elisira: That's why we make our own.

[She pushes one block toward Avyanna.]

Elisira: Your turn.

[Avyanna hesitates.]

[Then she touches it.]

[Not a toy.]

[A tool.]

[A small, heavy proof that people can teach you how to disappear without telling you to vanish.]

---
*End Chapter 15e*
