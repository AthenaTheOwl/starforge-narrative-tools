# Chapter 18b: Station Overwhelm and Recovery Protocols

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH18, DURING EXTENDED STATION LAYOVER]**
> *Location: Station public area -> quiet zone -> ship*
> *Present: Avyanna, Waffle, later Nyx*
> *Teaching: Recognizing limits, self-advocacy, recovery without shame*

---

[Station Helix-9 is crowded. More crowded than Avyanna expected. More crowded than she can handle.]

[She's been walking through the market district for twenty minutes. Waffle hovers beside her, scanning for threats, maintaining a polite bubble of space around her. But the crowd is pressing in anyway. Too many bodies. Too many voices. Too much motion and light and sound.]

[Her breathing is getting shallow. Her vision is narrowing. The edges are going gray.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ALERT: trainee exhibiting physiological stress markers | HEART RATE: elevated | RESPIRATION: shallow and rapid | RECOMMENDATION: extraction to low-stimulation environment>

[Avyanna doesn't respond. She's trying to push through it. Trying to function. If she stops now, if she asks to leave, she's failing. She's weak. She's-]

<WAFFLE.BAT// OVERRIDE: trainee nonresponsive to verbal | IMPLEMENTING EMERGENCY GROUNDING PROTOCOL>

---

[Waffle projects a bright light pattern directly in front of Avyanna's face. Geometric. Rhythmic. Impossible to ignore.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ATTENTION: you are experiencing sensory overload | THIS IS NOT FAILURE | THIS IS BIOLOGY | INSTRUCTION: follow me to quiet zone | DURATION: two minutes>

[The light pattern moves. Slowly. Waffle is guiding her.]

[Avyanna follows because following is easier than deciding. Her legs move. Waffle carves a path through the crowd with surprising authority-people step aside for the Guild drone without complaint.]

[Two minutes later, they're in a quiet zone. A designated low-stimulation area. Dimmed lights. Soundproofing. Soft seating. Empty except for one person sleeping in a corner.]

---

[Waffle hovers beside a bench.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// INSTRUCTION: sit | FOCUS ON LIGHT PATTERN | BREATHE: in for four, hold for four, out for four | I AM HERE | YOU ARE SAFE>

[Avyanna sits. Her hands are shaking. Her vision is still gray at the edges. But the noise is gone. The crowd is gone. There's just quiet and Waffle's light pattern and the instruction to breathe.]

[In for four. Hold. Out for four.]

[In for four. Hold. Out for four.]

[Her vision clears incrementally. The gray recedes. Her heart rate slows.]

---

<WAFFLE.BAT// OBSERVATION: panic attack in progress, moderate severity | RESPONSE: extracted to safe environment | STATUS: trainee stabilizing | DURATION: will monitor>

[Avyanna focuses on the light. On the counting. On Waffle's steady presence.]

[After five minutes, she can speak.]

Avyanna: [hoarse] I'm sorry.

<WAFFLE.BAT// CORRECTION: you have nothing to apologize for | SENSORY OVERLOAD IS EXPECTED POST-TRAUMA RESPONSE | YOU RECOGNIZED DISTRESS | YOU ACCEPTED ASSISTANCE | THIS IS APPROPRIATE BEHAVIOR>

Avyanna: [shaking] I should be able to handle crowds. It's just people. It's not dangerous.

<WAFFLE.BAT// CORRECTION: danger is subjective | YOUR NERVOUS SYSTEM ASSESSED THREAT BASED ON AVAILABLE DATA | CROWDS = UNPREDICTABLE = HIGH COGNITIVE LOAD = PERCEIVED DANGER | YOUR BODY RESPONDED APPROPRIATELY TO PERCEIVED THREAT>

---

[Avyanna sits with that. Her body isn't wrong. It's just responding to old data.]

Avyanna: [quiet] How long until I can handle this?

<WAFFLE.BAT// ANSWER: variable | TRAUMA RECOVERY IS NONLINEAR | SOME DAYS YOU WILL TOLERATE CROWDS | SOME DAYS YOU WILL NOT | THIS IS NORMAL | THE GOAL IS NOT PERFECTION | THE GOAL IS RECOGNIZING YOUR LIMITS AND RESPONDING APPROPRIATELY>

[Waffle's lights pulse gently.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// ADDENDUM: you could have remained in the crowd and dissociated | MANY TRAUMA SURVIVORS DO | YOU ACCEPTED EXTRACTION INSTEAD | THAT IS PROGRESS>

Avyanna: [uncertain] It doesn't feel like progress.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: progress rarely feels like progress while it is happening | PROGRESS FEELS LIKE SURVIVING | YOU ARE SURVIVING | THEREFORE YOU ARE PROGRESSING>

---

[They sit in the quiet zone for fifteen minutes. Avyanna breathes. Waffle maintains its light pattern and proximity. No judgment. No impatience. Just presence.]

[Eventually, Avyanna's hands stop shaking.]

Avyanna: [to Waffle] Can we go back to the ship?

<WAFFLE.BAT// AFFIRMATIVE: return route optimized for minimal crowd exposure | DURATION: eight minutes | QUERY: do you require additional support or are you functional for transit>

Avyanna: [standing slowly] I can walk. I just... I need to not be on the station anymore.

<WAFFLE.BAT// ACKNOWLEDGED: extracting to lumen thief | PROCEDURE: stay within two meters | IF DISTRESS RECURS, SIGNAL AND I WILL ADJUST ROUTE>

---

[They leave the quiet zone. Waffle navigates them through service corridors instead of main concourses. Fewer people. Less noise. The route is longer, but safer for Avyanna's nervous system.]

[She focuses on Waffle's back. On following. On breathing. On not thinking about how she failed to handle a simple station visit.]

[Eight minutes later, they reach the ship's airlock. The door hisses closed behind them. Station noise cuts off. Ship sounds resume-familiar hum of life support, the steady rhythm of systems that don't change unpredictably.]

[Avyanna's shoulders drop two inches.]

---

<WAFFLE.BAT// OBSERVATION: trainee relaxation response upon returning to ship environment | INFERENCE: ship is perceived as safe space | CONFIDENCE: 0.92 | THIS IS APPROPRIATE | HOME SHOULD FEEL SAFER THAN EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENTS>

Avyanna: [quiet] Thank you. For getting me out.

<WAFFLE.BAT// RESPONSE: that is crew function | YOU WOULD DO THE SAME FOR ME IF I WERE IN DISTRESS | MUTUAL AID IS STRUCTURAL, NOT TRANSACTIONAL>

[Waffle hovers beside her as she walks to her quarters. She expects Waffle to leave. It doesn't.]

Avyanna: [at her door] I'm okay now. You don't have to stay.

<WAFFLE.BAT// CORRECTION: you are stabilized, not okay | DISTINCTION MATTERS | I WILL NOTIFY NYX OF INCIDENT PER MEDICAL PROTOCOL | THEY MAY WISH TO CHECK IN>

Avyanna: [tense] You're reporting me?

<WAFFLE.BAT// CLARIFICATION: i am documenting a medical event | NOT FOR PUNISHMENT | FOR CARE | NYX WILL ASSESS WHETHER ADDITIONAL SUPPORT IS NEEDED | IF YOU WISH TO DECLINE ASSESSMENT, THAT IS YOUR RIGHT | BUT I RECOMMEND ACCEPTANCE>

---

[Avyanna sits on her bunk. Waffle hovers near the door-present but not crowding.]

[Five minutes later, Nyx arrives. Knocks. Waits for permission.]

Avyanna: [tired] Come in.

[Nyx enters. Sits in the desk chair. Doesn't loom.]

Nyx: [calm] Waffle says you had a panic attack on station. Are you injured?

Avyanna: [shaking her head] No. Just... overwhelmed.

Nyx: [nodding] Station sensory overload. Common. How are you feeling now?

Avyanna: [honest] Tired. Embarrassed. Angry at myself.

Nyx: [precise] Tired is expected. Panic attacks are exhausting. Embarrassed and angry are optional and unhelpful.

---

[Avyanna looks up. Nyx's expression is neutral. Clinical. Not judging.]

Nyx: [continuing] You encountered a stimulus your nervous system couldn't process. You recognized distress. You accepted assistance. You extracted to safety. You recovered.

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] That's not failure. That's textbook appropriate response to overwhelm.

Avyanna: [frustrated] I should be able to handle a crowded station. It's not dangerous.

Nyx: [flat] Your nervous system doesn't care about objective danger. It cares about perceived danger. And for someone who spent years in an environment where crowds meant violence and unpredictability meant punishment, a crowded station *is* dangerous. To your nervous system, that's truth.

---

[Nyx pulls out a medical scanner. Waves it near Avyanna without touching her.]

Nyx: [reading] Elevated cortisol. Adrenaline still clearing. Heart rate returning to baseline. You're recovering appropriately.

[They put the scanner away.]

Nyx: [leaning forward slightly] Here's what I'm prescribing. Not medication. Just protocol.

Avyanna: [wary] Protocol?

Nyx: [listing] One: You rest for the next two hours. No tasks. No obligations. Just recovery.

Nyx: Two: If you feel up to it, you eat something. Your body burned a lot of resources during that panic response. Food helps replenish.

Nyx: Three: You do not punish yourself for having limits. Limits are information, not failure.

---

[Avyanna processes that. Limits as information.]

Avyanna: [quiet] What if I can't do the next station visit? What if this keeps happening?

Nyx: [measured] Then we adjust. Shorter visits. Different times of day when stations are less crowded. Backup plans for extraction. Grounding tools.

Nyx: [meeting her eyes] Your nervous system is healing. Healing isn't linear. Some days you'll tolerate more. Some days you'll tolerate less. We adapt to what you can handle on a given day. That's not weakness. That's strategy.

Avyanna: [uncertain] The crew won't think I'm... useless?

Nyx: [dry] The crew thinks you're a trainee recovering from severe trauma who's learning to identify and respond to your limits. That's exactly what we expect you to be.

---

<WAFFLE.BAT// ADDENDUM: crew has experience with trauma recovery | ELIA HAD SIMILAR SENSORY OVERWHELM FIRST YEAR ABOARD | JALEN STILL AVOIDS CERTAIN STATION SECTORS DUE TO COMPACT PTSD | YOU ARE NOT UNIQUE IN HAVING LIMITS | YOU ARE JUST LEARNING TO NAME THEM>

[Avyanna looks at Waffle. Then at Nyx.]

Avyanna: [slowly] Other crew have panic attacks?

Nyx: [nodding] Yes. Different triggers. Different presentations. But everyone on this ship has had moments where their nervous system said "no more." You're not alone in this.

Nyx: [standing] I'm going to make you tea. You're going to drink it. Then you're going to rest. In two hours, I'll check on you again. If you need anything before then, you tell Waffle or use ship comms.

Avyanna: [quiet] Thank you.

Nyx: [at the door] Thank Waffle. They executed the extraction perfectly.

---

[Nyx leaves. Waffle remains.]

<WAFFLE.BAT// OBSERVATION: you are processing | THIS IS APPROPRIATE | QUERY: do you require continued presence or do you prefer solitude>

Avyanna: [considering] Stay? For a little while?

<WAFFLE.BAT// AFFIRMATIVE: i will remain | I WILL DIM MY LIGHTS TO NON-DISTRACTING LEVEL | I WILL MONITOR FOR DISTRESS | YOU MAY SLEEP IF YOU WISH | I WILL ENSURE YOU ARE NOT DISTURBED>

[Waffle dims its lights. Hovers near the ceiling. Present but unobtrusive.]

[Avyanna lies back on her bunk. Her body is heavy with exhaustion. Her mind is quieter now. The panic is gone. The shame is lessening.]

(I had a panic attack. Waffle extracted me. Nyx checked on me. No one punished me. No one called me weak.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(A warm pressure at the base of her skull: limit, named, safe.)

---

[She sleeps. Waffle keeps watch. Two hours later, Nyx returns with tea and a light meal. Avyanna wakes, eats, drinks. Feels more human.]

Nyx: [sitting] How are you feeling?

Avyanna: [honest] Better. Still tired. But better.

Nyx: [nodding] Good. Tomorrow, we talk about grounding techniques for managing sensory overload before it becomes full panic. For now, you rest.

Avyanna: [hesitant] What if there's another station visit soon?

Nyx: [calm] Then we plan for it. Shorter duration. Specific objectives. Exit strategies. We don't throw you into situations you're not ready for. We build capacity gradually.

[Nyx stands.]

Nyx: [at the door] You did well today. You recognized you were in distress. You accepted help. You recovered. That's not failure. That's exactly what we need you to do.

---

[After Nyx leaves, Avyanna sits with that. Not failure. Appropriate response. Limits as information, not inadequacy.]

[Tomorrow, she'll learn grounding techniques. She'll practice identifying overwhelm earlier. She'll build tools for managing the unpredictable galaxy outside the ship.]

[But tonight, she rests. And that's enough.]

---

<CINNAMON.EXE// MEDICAL INCIDENT LOG: trainee experienced sensory overload event on station | WAFFLE EXTRACTION PROTOCOL: executed successfully | NYX ASSESSMENT: no injury, appropriate recovery | FOLLOW-UP: grounding techniques training scheduled | STATUS: incident resolved without complication>

<WAFFLE.BAT// PERSONAL NOTE: trainee accepted extraction without extended resistance | TRAINEE ACCEPTED MEDICAL ASSESSMENT | TRAINEE RESTING | THIS IS PROGRESS | SENSORY OVERLOAD WILL RECUR BUT TRAINEE NOW HAS EXPERIENCE WITH SUCCESSFUL RECOVERY PROTOCOL | CONFIDENCE: 0.89>

<NYX PERSONAL LOG: "Panic attack. Standard post-trauma presentation. She's learning that her nervous system is allowed to have limits. That those limits don't disqualify her from crew. Tomorrow we work on prevention and early intervention. But today was about learning that recovery is possible. She learned.">

---

*End Scene*
