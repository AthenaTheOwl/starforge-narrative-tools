# Chapter 18a: Cultural Context Without Colonialism

> **[MICRO-SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH18, LIKELY DURING RETURN TRANSIT]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief galley (quiet shift)*
> *Present: Avyanna, Nyx*
> *Teaching: How to navigate cultural diversity without assuming one right answer*

---

[The galley is quiet in the way the ship gets quiet when it thinks the humans need room to breathe.]

[Avyanna sits at the table with a cup of tea that tastes like it was made by someone who's never met a plant in person.]

[There's a small pot of living herbs under the light strip by the bulkhead-green, stubborn, thriving in defiance of metal and vacuum.]

[Nyx comes in without fanfare. Two cups. They set one beside her without asking if she wants it. The gesture says: you can refuse, but you don't have to justify refusal.]

Avyanna: [quiet] The elder at the Garden. They did things differently.

Nyx: [sitting] Yes.

Avyanna: The way they talked about the Lattice. The way they... moved. It wasn't like how you teach.

Nyx: [dry] The Sutured Houses and I disagree about almost everything.

Avyanna: But you took me there.

Nyx: Disagreement doesn't mean disrespect.

[Avyanna watches Nyx's hands around the cup. Steady. Like they're holding something fragile and choosing not to crush it.]

Nyx: The mine taught you there was one correct way to live. One correct way to speak. One correct way to survive.

Nyx: That is a monoculture. Monoculture is a control mechanism.

Avyanna: [hesitant] So... the galaxy isn't like that.

Nyx: No.

Nyx: The galaxy is thousands of cultures arguing with each other while pretending they're not.

---

Avyanna: [careful] How do I know which one is right.

[Nyx's eyes lift to hers. Not sharp. Not punishing. Just precise.]

Nyx: Wrong question.

[Avyanna's shoulders tense anyway. Correction is pain, even when it isn't.]

Nyx: Not wrong as in punishable.

Nyx: Wrong as in it will make you stupid.

[Nyx nods toward the herb pot.]

Nyx: Different soil grows different things.

Nyx: If you walk into someone else's life and declare their soil wrong, you don't educate them. You invade them.

Avyanna: [slow] So what's the right question.

Nyx: What is this person trying to accomplish.

Nyx: Who benefits.

Nyx: Who gets hurt.

Nyx: And-most important-what part of your reaction is about harm, and what part is just... unfamiliar.

---

Avyanna: [quiet] I'm going to be on stations with people who do things I don't understand.

Nyx: Yes.

Avyanna: And I don't want to... do what the mine did. Decide I'm right because it's what I know.

Nyx: Then practice describing without prescribing.

Avyanna: [frowning] Explain.

Nyx: Describe what you saw. Don't prescribe what you think they should do instead.

Nyx: "They speak loudly." "They avoid eye contact." "They won't shake hands." "They won't take a drink offered by strangers."

Nyx: Those are observations.

Nyx: "That's rude." "That's wrong." "That's suspicious." Those are judgments.

[Avyanna turns the tea cup in her hands. The herb leaves shift in the ventilation.]

Avyanna: So... I'm allowed to not have an opinion.

Nyx: [dry] It is, in fact, mandatory.

Nyx: You are allowed to say, "I don't know enough to judge."

[The sentence lands like a tool. Like a lockpick that doesn't cut her fingers.]

---

Avyanna: What if it really is harm.

Nyx: Then you name the harm specifically.

Nyx: Not "this feels wrong to me"-but "this removes autonomy."

Nyx: "This is extraction."

Nyx: "This is coercion dressed up as tradition."

Nyx: And you check your assumptions before you act.

Avyanna: [quiet] Like Elisira teaches.

Nyx: Like the ship teaches.

[Avyanna's throat tightens at that-at being included as a student in a place that keeps teaching as if teaching is abundance.]

---

[Nyx stands, gathers the cups.]

Nyx: You will make mistakes.

Nyx: Everyone does.

Nyx: The goal isn't purity. The goal is curiosity with teeth.

[Avyanna watches them go. Then looks at the herb pot again.]

(Different soil. Different needs. Harm is structural. Curiosity is allowed.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm-not a warning. Just attention, like it's listening with her.]
