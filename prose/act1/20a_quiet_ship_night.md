# Chapter 20a: Quiet Ship Night

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH20, LATE NIGHT CYCLE]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief, various locations during night shift*
> *Present: Avyanna wandering, encounters with crew*
> *Purpose: Ship as lived-in space, crew rhythms, belonging through small moments*

---

[2300 ship time. Night cycle. Most of the crew is sleeping. The Lumen Thief is never truly quiet-life support hums, systems cycle, the ship breathes-but it's quieter. Softer. Like the difference between day-sounds and night-sounds in any place that's lived in.]

[Avyanna can't sleep. Again. Her body still hasn't calibrated to ship rhythms. Or maybe it has, and she's just someone who doesn't sleep well. Hard to know the difference yet.]

[She leaves her quarters wearing soft clothes that used to belong to someone else and now belong to her. Bare feet on deck plating. The ship is warm. Not mine-warm, where heat meant broken cooling systems. Ship-warm, where temperature is maintained for human comfort.]

---

[She walks without destination. The corridor lights are dimmed for night cycle-blue-shift, easier on eyes that might need to wake up fast. She passes crew quarters. Behind Elia's door: silence. Behind Rho's: faint music, something old and acoustic. Behind Vesper's: the soft glow of a still-active terminal.]

[Everyone has their own night rhythms. Everyone exists in their own way when they think no one's watching.]

[Avyanna turns toward the galley. Maybe tea. Maybe just the comfort of a space that smells like food and care.]

---

[The galley is dim. One light over the counter. And Rho, sitting at the table with a cup of something and a datapad, looking like he's been there for hours.]

Rho: [not looking up] Can't sleep, or won't sleep?

Avyanna: [hesitating in the doorway] Can't.

Rho: [gesturing at the empty chair] Join the club. Tea's hot. Help yourself.

[Avyanna makes tea. The same green tea she chose that first morning. It's become her default. Her preference. The first one she's had in seventeen years.]

[She sits across from Rho. He's reading something technical-engineering schematics, maybe. Definitely not entertainment.]

Avyanna: [quiet] What are you working on?

Rho: [sighing] Trying to figure out why the port thruster is burning fuel inefficiently. Jalen says it's pulling three percent more than it should. Three percent doesn't sound like much until you're budgeting for a long jump and suddenly you're short on margins.

---

[He taps the schematic.]

Rho: [to himself] Has to be the injector timing. Or the fuel mixture calibration. Or seventeen other things that could be wrong.

Avyanna: [tentative] Is it urgent?

Rho: [leaning back] Nah. Annoying, not dangerous. I just can't figure it out and it's bothering me. So here I am at 2300, reading schematics instead of sleeping like a functional human.

[He grins at her.]

Rho: [adding] What's your excuse?

Avyanna: [shrugging] My brain won't stop. Too many thoughts. Too much... everything.

Rho: [nodding] Yeah. I know that feeling.

---

[They sit in comfortable silence. Rho reads. Avyanna drinks tea. The galley is warm and safe and neither of them is performing for the other.]

[After a while, Rho closes the datapad.]

Rho: [stretching] Can't solve it tonight. Brain's too tired. Might as well admit defeat and try to sleep.

[He stands, rinses his cup, puts it in the cleaning rack.]

Rho: [to Avyanna] You staying up, or heading back?

Avyanna: [uncertain] Staying for a bit. If that's okay.

Rho: [waving her off] Galley's open all night. Lock it if you're the last one out. Otherwise, do whatever.

[He leaves. Avyanna is alone again. But the space still feels occupied. Rho's presence lingers-the rinsed cup, the datapad he left on the counter, the faint smell of whatever he was drinking earlier.]

---

[She finishes her tea. Walks out into the corridor. The ship is quiet. She drifts toward the bridge. Maybe Jalen's there. Maybe not. Either way, the bridge has windows. Stars. A view that isn't walls.]

[The bridge door is open. Jalen sits at the navigation console, three screens active, stylus moving. Always working. Always calculating something.]

Avyanna: [at the doorway] Can I come in?

Jalen: [not looking up] Door's open. That means yes.

[She enters. Takes the seat she sat in last time they taught her basic navigation. They don't acknowledge her beyond that initial permission. Just keep working.]

[Avyanna watches the stars through the viewport. They're in transit between stations-no nearby gravity wells, no hazards, just void and distance and the mathematics of getting from here to there.]

---

Jalen: [after five minutes] Can't sleep?

Avyanna: [quiet] No.

Jalen: [nodding] I'm not good at sleeping either. Too many calculations running. Hard to shut the brain off.

[They save their work. Turn to face her.]

Jalen: [gesturing at the viewport] You know what that is?

Avyanna: [confused] Space?

Jalen: [dry] Technically correct. But more specifically: that's about four light-years of nothing. We're in the void between inhabited systems. No stations. No ships. No people except us.

[They lean back.]

Jalen: [quieter] I find that comforting. Knowing that for a few days, we're the only thing that exists in a very large radius. No one can get to us. No one knows where we are except us. We're just... adrift in a pocket of silence.

---

[Avyanna looks at the stars again. Thinking about that. Four light-years of nothing. A bubble of safety maintained by distance and math.]

Avyanna: [careful] Do you get scared? Out here?

Jalen: [considering] Sometimes. When systems fail. When navigation is uncertain. When I remember that we're very small and space is very large and all of this is held together by math that could be wrong.

[They tap their console.]

Jalen: [adding] But mostly I feel... quiet. Like the ship is quiet. Like my brain can stop screaming for a few hours and just exist.

Avyanna: [whispered] That's what I want. For my brain to stop screaming.

Jalen: [meeting her eyes] Yeah. It'll come. Takes time. But you'll get there.

---

[They sit. Jalen returns to their calculations. Avyanna watches the stars. The bridge is quiet except for the soft sounds of stylus on screen and ship systems maintaining life in vacuum.]

[After twenty minutes, Avyanna stands.]

Avyanna: [to Jalen] Thank you. For letting me sit here.

Jalen: [not looking up] Bridge is open during night shift. You're welcome here whenever.

[She leaves. Walks back toward crew quarters. Passes the med bay. The door is cracked open. Light spills into the corridor.]

[She pauses. Looks in.]

---

[Nyx is at their desk, reviewing something on a datapad. They look up when they sense her presence.]

Nyx: [calm] Can't sleep?

Avyanna: [leaning against the doorframe] Everyone keeps asking me that.

Nyx: [slight smile] Because you keep wandering the ship at night. Pattern recognition.

[They set the datapad down.]

Nyx: [gesturing inside] Come in if you want. Or keep wandering. Both acceptable.

[Avyanna steps inside. The med bay smells like antiseptic and something herbal. Clean and cared-for.]

Nyx: [observing her] Trouble sleeping, or avoiding sleep?

Avyanna: [sitting in the patient chair] Both? I'm tired but my brain won't stop. And when I do sleep, I dream about the mine.

Nyx: [nodding] Trauma dreams. Normal. Unpleasant, but normal.

---

[They pull out a small box. Inside: various items. Smooth stones. Fidget toys. A small weighted pad. Grounding tools.]

Nyx: [setting the box on the desk] These are for people who can't shut their brain off. You can borrow one. See if tactile grounding helps.

[Avyanna looks at the items. Picks up a smooth stone-similar to the worry stone she bought on her first station visit, but heavier.]

Nyx: [explaining] Some people need something to hold. Something physical to focus on when thoughts get too loud. You already have the worry stone. This is a weighted version. Different sensation. Might help more.

Avyanna: [holding it] Can I try it?

Nyx: [nodding] Yes. Keep it as long as you need. Return it when you're done, or don't. I have others.

---

[Avyanna closes her hand around the weighted stone. The pressure is grounding. Real. Pulling her attention out of her head and into her body.]

Avyanna: [quiet] Thank you.

Nyx: [returning to their datapad] You're welcome. Now go try to sleep. Or keep wandering. Whichever feels right.

[Avyanna leaves the med bay. The weighted stone is in her hand. Solid. Present. An anchor.]

[She walks back to her quarters. The corridor is empty now. Just her and the ship and the quiet sounds of systems maintaining existence.]

---

[In her quarters, she lies down with the weighted stone in her hand. Her thumb rubs across the smooth surface. The pressure is comforting in a way she doesn't have words for yet.]

[Her mind is still loud. But quieter. The stone gives her something to focus on other than the panic and the memories and the constant calculation of threats.]

[She doesn't sleep. Not yet. But she rests. And for now, that's enough.]
