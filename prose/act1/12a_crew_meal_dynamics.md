# Chapter 12a: Crew Meal Dynamics

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH12, EVENING ROUTINE]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief galley, dinner time*
> *Present: Full crew (Vesper, Rho, Jalen, Elia, Nyx, Avyanna)*
> *Teaching: Collective care, meal sharing as non-performance, crew as chosen family*

---

[Dinner on the Lumen Thief is chaotic in the way that families are chaotic-not dangerous, just loud and overlapping and full of people who've stopped performing for each other.]

[The galley smells like something Rho is calling "stew" that probably started life as three different meals before he combined them into one pot. It's not beautiful. But it's hot and it's food and no one has to eat alone.]

[Avyanna sits at the table between Nyx and Elia. Across from her: Vesper, Rho, Jalen. Six people. Six plates. One meal.]

[She's still not used to this.]

---

Rho: [ladling stew into bowls] Fair warning: I added spice. Jalen said the last batch was bland.

Jalen: [not looking up from their datapad] I said it tasted like warm disappointment.

Rho: [cheerful] Same thing.

Vesper: [accepting a bowl] If this kills me, promote Elia to engineer.

Elia: [dry] I don't want the job.

Rho: [mock-wounded] No one appreciates my culinary artistry.

Nyx: [tasting the stew] It's... functional.

Rho: [grinning] I'll take it.

---

[Avyanna accepts her bowl. The stew is thick and brown and smells like a dozen spices she can't name. She takes a cautious bite.]

[It's good. Better than good. Better than anything she ate in the mine, where food was fuel and nothing else.]

Rho: [watching her face] You like it?

Avyanna: [surprised] Yes. It's... it's really good.

Rho: [pleased] See? Some people have taste.

Jalen: [finally looking up] Some people have low standards.

Rho: [throwing a piece of bread at them] Heretic.

[Jalen catches it without looking. Takes a bite. Shrugs.]

Jalen: [admitting] It's acceptable.

Rho: [triumphant] Victory.

---

[They eat. Conversation flows around Avyanna like water around a stone-not excluding her, just not requiring her to participate. She can listen. Observe. Exist in the space without needing to justify her presence.]

Vesper: [to Elia] Status on the new client's recon?

Elia: [between bites] Two potential entry points. Both viable. I'll have a full report by tomorrow.

Vesper: [nodding] Priority level?

Elia: Medium. No immediate danger, but timeline is tight. We'll need to move within the week.

Vesper: [making a note] Understood. I'll coordinate with the client.

---

[This is business. But it's also dinner. The two things coexist without tension.]

Rho: [to Nyx] How's the med bay inventory?

Nyx: [precise] Adequate. We're low on broad-spectrum antibiotics. I've submitted a requisition to the next Guild station.

Rho: [frowning] Low as in "we should worry" or low as in "we have enough for now"?

Nyx: [considering] Low as in "if someone gets infected in the next week, we're fine. If two people get infected, we're rationing."

Rho: [nodding] I'll expedite the req. Bump it to priority two.

Nyx: [approving] Appreciated.

---

[Avyanna watches the exchange. Casual logistics. Everyone tracking different systems. Everyone contributing information. No hierarchy. Just coordination.]

Jalen: [to the table, still scrolling their datapad] Station Kael-7 is reporting increased Compact patrol activity. Might affect our transit route.

Vesper: [looking up] Increased how much?

Jalen: [checking] Thirty percent more ships than usual. No official reason given.

Vesper: [thoughtful] Flag it. If it goes above fifty percent, we reroute. I'm not interested in Compact attention.

Jalen: [noting it] Flagged.

---

[Elia finishes her stew, sets the bowl aside.]

Elia: [to Rho] Your turn for dishes.

Rho: [groaning] I cooked.

Elia: [flat] You made stew. That's not cooking. That's assembly.

Rho: [pointing at the pot] That is a *masterwork* of assembly.

Elia: [unmoved] Still your turn for dishes.

Rho: [to the table] Someone back me up here.

Vesper: [dry] You're on dish rotation. Elia's correct.

Rho: [sighing dramatically] I am unappreciated and overworked.

Jalen: [without looking up] You're appreciated exactly the correct amount.

---

[Avyanna almost smiles. Watching Rho get roasted is becoming one of her favorite ship activities.]

Nyx: [to Avyanna] You're quiet tonight. More than usual.

[Avyanna tenses. Observation = evaluation = potential correction.]

Nyx: [gentle] That wasn't criticism. Just observation. You're allowed to be quiet.

Avyanna: [careful] I'm just... listening. Learning how this works.

Nyx: [nodding] Good strategy. You'll figure out when you want to talk. No pressure.

---

[Rho stands, starts collecting bowls. Avyanna moves to hand him hers.]

Rho: [waving her off] You're still new. You get a grace period. I'll grab it.

Avyanna: [confused] Grace period?

Vesper: [explaining] First month on crew, you're exempt from chores. Lets you acclimate without added pressure.

Avyanna: [frowning] But I can help-

Rho: [interrupting kindly] And you will. Next month. Right now, your job is learning to exist here without constant survival math.

[He takes her bowl, stacks it with the others.]

Rho: [grinning] Besides, dishes are meditative. I do my best thinking while scrubbing.

Jalen: [dry] That explains so much.

---

[Rho carries the dishes to the cleaning station. The rest of the crew lingers at the table-no rush to leave, no schedule demanding they disperse. Just people existing in proximity because it's pleasant.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] How's the training with me going? The physical stuff.

Avyanna: [honest] Hard. But... not bad hard. Learning hard.

Elia: [approving] Good distinction. If it ever tips into bad hard, tell me. We adjust.

Avyanna: [quiet] You keep saying that. "Tell me." "We adjust." Like it's simple.

Elia: [meeting her eyes] It is simple. Not easy, but simple. You speak, I listen, we change course. That's the system.

---

[Vesper stands, stretches.]

Vesper: [to the table] I'm reviewing contracts in the conference room if anyone needs me. Otherwise, I'll see you at 0800 for the briefing.

Jalen: [standing] I've got bridge night shift. Jalen out.

Nyx: [collecting their medical datapad] Med bay inventory to finish. Then sleep.

[One by one, the crew disperses. Not in formation. Not on command. Just people ending their meal and moving to their next tasks.]

[Avyanna remains at the table. Elia stays too, finishing her water.]

---

Avyanna: [hesitant] Can I ask something?

Elia: [nodding] Always.

Avyanna: [slow] This. Dinner. Everyone sitting together. Is this... mandatory?

Elia: [considering] Mandatory? No. Expected? Yes. But expected and mandatory are different.

Elia: [explaining] We eat together because it's useful. Crew coordination happens here. Information sharing. Also, eating alone is depressing. But if you needed to eat alone some night, you could. No one would punish you.

Avyanna: [absorbing] Expected, not mandatory.

Elia: [nodding] Like most things here. We have rhythms. Routines. But they're flexible. Built for humans, not machines.

---

[Rho returns from the cleaning station, wiping his hands on a towel.]

Rho: [to Avyanna] You still sitting here?

Avyanna: [defensive] I can leave-

Rho: [interrupting, gentle] Not kicking you out. Just checking if you need company or solitude.

[Avyanna pauses. Company or solitude. Her choice.]

Avyanna: [slow] Solitude. I think. Tonight.

Rho: [nodding] Fair. Galley's yours. Lock up when you're done if you're the last one out.

[He leaves. Elia stands, pushes her chair in.]

Elia: [to Avyanna] Good dinner. See you tomorrow for training.

Avyanna: [nodding] Tomorrow.

---

[And then she's alone in the galley. Not isolated. Not abandoned. Just... alone. By choice.]

[The remnants of dinner are cleared. The pot is soaking in the cleaner. The table is wiped down. The space is tidy in the way that means people care about communal areas.]

[Avyanna sits at the table and breathes. Absorbs the quiet. Thinks about six people eating together, arguing about stew, coordinating work, and then dispersing without drama.]

(This is what crew looks like. Not perfect. Not formal. Just people taking care of each other through small actions. Cooking. Cleaning. Sharing information. Eating together.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm. Witnessing.]

(Warmth hums at the base of her skull, not words-just a pattern she reads as: crew, chosen, home.)

---

[After ten minutes, Avyanna stands. Locks the galley door per Rho's instruction. Walks back to her quarters.]

[In her bunk, she thinks about dinner. About Rho's stew. About Elia's directness. About Nyx's observation without judgment. About Vesper coordinating contracts over food. About Jalen catching bread without looking.]

[Small things. Ordinary things. Things that mean: you belong here. You're part of this. You're crew.]

[She falls asleep easier than she has in weeks.]
