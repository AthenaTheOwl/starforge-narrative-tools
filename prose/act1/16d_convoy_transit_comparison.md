# Chapter 16d: Convoy Transit Comparison

> **LUMEN THIEF - TRANSIT NOTE (INTERNAL):**
> *Topic: myth is procedure other people can't afford*
> *Reminder: calm is a capability*

---

[They fall in with a convoy because the corridor is shifting and sometimes "normal" is safer than "alone."]

[Two ships ahead. One behind.]

[A fourth off their port side, pacing them like it wants to be seen as equal.]

[Its lights are brighter than necessary.]

[Its comms are louder than necessary.]

[Its transponder says insured. The kind of insured that makes people assume the universe will apologize if it hurts you.]

---

[The Lumen Thief bridge is in working-quiet.]

[Jalen in the pilot cradle. Elisira standing behind them with a slate. Rho in the doorway with coffee that smells like stubbornness. Vesper at the rail with a datapad full of jurisdiction traps. Elia pretending to be bored near weapons control.]

[Avyanna at the last open station, strapped in, pretending her ribs aren't humming with the corridor's wrongness.]

(Convoy means witnesses. Witnesses mean lists.)

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: convoy formation recognized | NOTE: increased traffic reduces ambiguity but increases attention | INFERENCE: risk trade | CONFIDENCE: 0.78>

<BUBBLES// REMINDER: do not interpret convoy proximity as safety>

Elia: [cheerful] Thank you, Bubbles. Very comforting.

Bubbles: [over speaker, earnest] You are welcome.

---

[The insured ship pings them.]

[A hail request slides onto the console with the casual entitlement of something used to being answered.]

Jalen: [flat] Decline.

Vesper: [without looking up] Let them talk at us for a second. It will cost less than them inventing a story about why we refused.

[Jalen makes a sound that is not agreement and taps accept anyway.]

---

Other Captain: [voice smooth, broadcast-quality] Independent vessel Lumen Thief, this is convoy lead for insured hauler group. We see you're running the same lane. Confirm you can maintain pace and formation.

[There's a pause where the captain expects deference.]

[Avyanna knows the shape of that pause. The mine was built out of it.]

Vesper: [mild] Define "confirm."

Other Captain: [a beat] Acknowledge that you won't become a hazard.

Vesper: We are not a hazard.

Other Captain: [tiny laugh] Everyone says that.

Elisira: Everyone lies.

---

Other Captain: We've got ten onboard. Standard redundancy. We can provide assistance if your systems lag.

[Ten onboard.]

[Avyanna pictures it: a medic that isn't also a negotiator; a pilot that isn't also a lawyer; a tech that isn't also a fighter.]

[Ten people to do what this ship does with fewer bodies and more voices.]

(Unfair.)

Rho: [to no one in particular] We don't need help.

Other Captain: [polite] It's not charity. It's protocol.

Vesper: Protocol is enforcement with manners.

Other Captain: ...Excuse me?

Vesper: Nothing. Continue your checklists.

---

[The corridor shifts.]

[Not dramatic. Not a scream.]

[Just a pressure change that makes Avyanna's teeth ache and her hands want to clench.]

(Mine collapse.)

Elia: [bright] Ooh. That's a seam.

Jalen: [quiet] Drift left two degrees. Hold. Hold.

[The Lumen Thief slides like it enjoys obeying someone who knows why they're asking.]

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: shear gradient increasing | ACTION: reduce nonessential power draw 9% | INFERENCE: lower thermal noise signature | CONFIDENCE: 0.82>

<WAFFLE.BAT// COMMENTARY: corridor thinks it's funny>

[Avyanna swallows.]

[She tracks Vesper's hands. The way they stay on the rail. The way her posture stays open even when everything in the universe is trying to make her shrink.]

(This is what control looks like. Not yelling. Not threats. Just choices held steady.)

---

Other Captain: [voice tightening] Convoy, confirm stability. Readouts just spiked.

[Over comm, the other ship's bridge goes from smooth to crowded.]

Other Crew Voice: (pilot) Lane's drifting, Captain.

Other Crew Voice: (tech) Guidance is lagging.

Other Crew Voice: (medic) Strap in, strap in, strap in-

Other Crew Voice: (admin) Insurance guidance says reduce speed twelve percent.

Other Crew Voice: (someone else) That's going to break formation.

Other Captain: Hold formation. Maintain pace. We're insured for this corridor rating.

[Avyanna feels her stomach twist.]

(Insured doesn't mean safe. Insured means someone decided what your death is worth.)

---

[The convoy ship beside them shudders.]

[Its running lights flicker once.]

[Avyanna hears a thin, ugly sound through the hull: metal complaining.]

Jalen: [calm] They're going to overcorrect.

Elisira: [already moving on her slate] Their captain is forcing pride into physics.

Elia: [still cheerful, but the cheer has teeth] Do we rescue them or let them learn.

Vesper: We do not let ten people die to teach a lesson.

Rho: [grim] We also don't let them drag us into their panic.

---

[Avyanna watches Vesper's profile.]

[The way she holds both truths without flinching.]

(Floors, not thrones. Floors still have edges.)

Vesper: [into comm, precise] Convoy lead. Your readouts are lying to you.

Other Captain: [sharp] Say again?

Vesper: Your insurer's guidance is a generic template. This corridor is shifting faster than their update cycle.

Other Captain: And you know that how?

[The question is bait.]

[Prove yourself for free. Show your hand. Earn their belief.]

[Avyanna feels Elia watching her. Waiting to see what she does with a pause shaped like hierarchy.]

Avyanna: [quiet, into her mic] Because you just said "reduce speed twelve percent" like a prayer.

[Silence.]

Vesper's head turns slightly. Not surprise. Acknowledgment.

Avyanna: That's not a measurement. It's a slogan.

Other Captain: [too controlled] Watch your tone, kid.

Elisira: [flat] Define "kid."

---

Vesper: [still calm] If you want to stay in formation, you need to drift left by two degrees and stop fighting the seam.

Other Captain: That's not in our- our procedures.

Vesper: Your procedures were written for paperwork, not weather.

Other Captain: [breathing audible now] We are insured-

Rho: [cuts in, voice like a clamp] Insurance doesn't fly your ship.

---

[The convoy lead hesitates.]

[Avyanna can hear ten people's fear stacking behind one person's pride.]

Other Crew Voice: (pilot, urgent) Captain, if we don't adjust we're going to shear.

Other Captain: ...Drift left two degrees. Hold.

[The other ship slides.]

[Not as clean as the Lumen Thief.]

[But it slides.]

[The metal-complaint sound eases by a fraction.]

---

Other Captain: [after a long breath] Acknowledged.

Other Captain: ...Thank you.

Vesper: [immediate] You're welcome.

[Avyanna feels something in her chest loosen.]

(No punishment. No invoice. Just a fix, and then you move on.)

---

[The convoy keeps moving.]

[The insured ship doesn't try to pace them anymore.]

[It drops half a length back, like it suddenly remembers distance is etiquette.]

[Before they cut comms, the other captain speaks again.]

Other Captain: Lumen Thief-

[Not bait this time.]

[Not posturing.]

Other Captain: Are you always like this.

Elia: [bright] Like what.

Other Captain: Like you already know what the corridor is going to do.

Jalen: [flat] No.

Jalen: We just listen faster.

Elisira: And we don't worship templates.

Vesper: And we ask "who enforces it" before we trust it.

Rho: And we don't confuse insured with immortal.

[A pause.]

Other Captain: ...Copy.

[The channel closes.]

---

[For a while, nobody on the bridge speaks.]

[Avyanna watches the stars smear and un-smear.]

[Watches the convoy hold itself together on a borrowed lesson.]

(They'll tell people.)

(Not "we survived." They'll tell people about the ship that corrected an insured captain mid-seam without raising its voice.)

<CINNAMON.EXE// OBSERVATION: external comms silence persists | INFERENCE: rumor retention likelihood high | CONFIDENCE: 0.77>

Elia: [soft, satisfied] See.

Avyanna: What.

Elia: Myths are just other people's fear, organized.

Elia: The trick is making them afraid of the right thing.

[Vesper doesn't look away from her screen.]

Vesper: The right thing is the system.

[Avyanna nods.]

[Her hands unclench.]

[Outside, the corridor keeps pretending it's weather.]
