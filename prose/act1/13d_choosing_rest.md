# Chapter 13d: Choosing Rest (Not Productivity)

> **[SCENE - OCCURS POST-CH13d, RECOVERY DAY]**
> *Location: Lumen Thief, Avyanna's quarters and common areas*
> *Present: Avyanna, various crew interactions*
> *Purpose: Learning that rest is valid, productivity is not mandatory, recovery is work*

---

[Avyanna wakes up exhausted. Not the usual exhaustion from nightmares or insomnia. A deeper exhaustion. The kind that settles into bones and says: you cannot function today.]

[She lies in her bunk and calculates. She has training with Elia scheduled. She should help Rho with maintenance tasks. She could practice navigation exercises with Jalen. She should be useful. Should be productive. Should be earning her place.]

[Her body says: no.]

[The conflict is paralyzing. Need to work versus cannot work. Obligation versus capacity. The mine's voice in her head saying weakness is unacceptable versus her body shutting down.]

[She doesn't move for twenty minutes. Just lies there, trapped between should and can't.]

---

[A knock at her door. Soft. Not demanding.]

Nyx: [from the corridor] Avyanna? You missed breakfast. Are you okay?

[Avyanna's throat tightens. Missed breakfast = noticed = evaluation = judgment = consequences.]

Avyanna: [hoarse] I'm fine.

Nyx: [pause] That didn't sound convincing. I'm coming in.

[The door opens. Nyx enters with a medical scanner and the particular calm that means they're in diagnostic mode.]

Nyx: [sitting in the desk chair] What's wrong?

Avyanna: [not looking at them] Nothing. I'm just tired.

Nyx: [running the scanner] Tired as in "didn't sleep well" or tired as in "complete exhaustion"?

Avyanna: [quiet] The second one.

---

[Nyx reads the scanner results.]

Nyx: [measured] Your cortisol levels are elevated. You're showing signs of systemic fatigue. Not sick. Just... depleted.

[They put the scanner away.]

Nyx: [to Avyanna] Have you been pushing yourself hard this week?

Avyanna: [defensive] I'm training. That's my job.

Nyx: [correcting] Your job is learning. Learning requires rest. You can't pour from an empty cup.

Avyanna: [frustrated] I have training scheduled. I should get up. I should-

Nyx: [interrupting, firm] You should rest. That's what your body is telling you. And your body is correct.

---

[Avyanna stares at the ceiling.]

Avyanna: [shaking] If I don't train today, I'll fall behind. If I fall behind, I'm useless. If I'm useless-

Nyx: [interrupting again, gentler] If you push through exhaustion, you'll injure yourself or make mistakes. Then you'll be unable to train for weeks, not just one day.

[They lean forward.]

Nyx: [precise] Rest is not laziness. Rest is maintenance. Your body is a system that requires downtime to repair and consolidate learning. Training while exhausted is like running a ship with failing engines-you might move, but you'll cause damage that costs more than you gain.

---

[Avyanna processes that. Rest as maintenance. Not failure.]

Avyanna: [quiet] What do I tell Elia? She's expecting me.

Nyx: [standing] I'll tell Elia. You don't have to justify exhaustion.

[They pull out their datapad. Type a message. Send it.]

Nyx: [showing her the screen] "Avyanna is taking a recovery day. Systemic fatigue. Training postponed to when she's functional. -Nyx."

Nyx: [to Avyanna] Done. Elia will understand. Everyone on this crew has taken recovery days. It's expected, not exceptional.

---

[Elia's response comes back immediately: "Understood. Tell her to actually rest. -Elia"]

Nyx: [reading it to Avyanna] Elia says actually rest. Not pretend-rest while worrying about being productive.

Avyanna: [uncertain] What does actually resting look like?

Nyx: [considering] No obligations. No tasks. No forcing yourself to be useful. You sleep, eat, do low-effort activities that bring comfort. You give your system permission to recover.

Avyanna: [frustrated] I don't know how to do that. I don't know how to just... be useless for a day.

Nyx: [flat] You're not useless. You're recovering. There's a difference.

---

[Nyx pulls out a small bottle from their medical kit.]

Nyx: [handing it to her] Magnesium supplement. Helps with sleep quality and muscle recovery. Take one now. Drink water. Try to sleep more.

[They stand at the door.]

Nyx: [before leaving] If you need anything-food, company, medical check-in-use ship comms. Someone will respond. That's what crew does.

[They leave. Avyanna is alone with the supplement bottle and permission to rest and no idea how to actually do it.]

---

[She takes the supplement. Drinks water. Lies back down.]

[Her brain screams at her to get up. To be productive. To earn her place. To prove she's not weak.]

[Her body says: no. Sleep. Recover.]

[The conflict is exhausting. She's exhausted from being exhausted.]

[Eventually, she sleeps. Not peaceful sleep. But sleep. Her body taking what it needs despite her brain's protests.]

---

[She wakes three hours later. Still tired. But less hollowed-out. The edges of the exhaustion have softened.]

[She sits up. Her quarters feel small and quiet. Not isolating. Just... still.]

[She could stay here. Stay in bed all day. But the walls are closing in. She needs space. People. But not obligations.]

[She pulls on comfortable clothes. Leaves her quarters.]

---

[The common room is empty except for Rho, who's stretched out on the couch reading something on a datapad. He looks up when she enters.]

Rho: [calm] Hey. Nyx said you're on recovery day.

Avyanna: [hovering in the doorway] Yeah. I don't really know what to do with myself.

Rho: [patting the other end of the couch] Sit. Exist. No pressure.

[She sits. Rho returns to his reading. Doesn't demand conversation. Doesn't expect entertainment. Just... shares space.]

[After ten minutes of silence, Rho speaks.]

Rho: [not looking up] You know what I do on recovery days?

Avyanna: [quiet] What?

Rho: [gesturing at his datapad] I read trash fiction. The kind with terrible plots and improbable physics and characters who make deeply stupid decisions. It requires zero brain power. It's comforting in its predictability.

[He grins at her.]

Rho: [adding] Currently reading a space romance where the engineer falls in love with a sentient asteroid. It's magnificently bad.

---

[Avyanna almost smiles.]

Avyanna: [tentative] Do you have... other trash fiction? That I could read?

Rho: [immediately pulling up a list] I have an entire collection. Genre preferences?

Avyanna: [uncertain] I don't know. Something that won't make me think too hard.

Rho: [sending her three titles] Try these. If you hate them, try others. The point isn't literary merit. The point is pleasant distraction.

[Avyanna downloads one. Opens it. Starts reading.]

[The prose is clunky. The plot is ridiculous. The characters are one-dimensional. It's perfect. It requires nothing from her except basic reading comprehension and a tolerance for absurdity.]

[They sit on opposite ends of the couch. Reading. Existing in proximity. Not performing. Not producing. Just... resting in company.]

---

[An hour later, Jalen enters. Sees them. Raises an eyebrow.]

Jalen: [to Avyanna] Recovery day?

Avyanna: [nodding] Yeah.

Jalen: [getting water from the galley] Good. Pushing through exhaustion is how people make catastrophic mistakes.

[They sit in the chair across from them.]

Jalen: [to no one in particular] I once tried to navigate a jump while running on two hours of sleep and four cups of coffee. Miscalculated the trajectory by point-three degrees. Doesn't sound like much, but at jump speeds, that's a forty-thousand-kilometer deviation.

Jalen: [dry] Vesper was not pleased. Made me take a mandatory rest day. I was furious. Until I slept for fourteen hours and woke up realizing how dangerously impaired I'd been.

---

[Avyanna looks at them.]

Avyanna: [careful] You made a mistake and Vesper didn't punish you?

Jalen: [correcting] Vesper mandated rest. That's not punishment. That's operational safety. I was a liability. Rest fixed the liability.

Jalen: [meeting her eyes] Exhaustion isn't a moral failing. It's a state. States can be changed. That's what recovery days are for.

[They return to their datapad. The three of them sit in the common room. Reading, thinking, existing without obligation.]

[Avyanna's brain is quieter now. The guilt is still there-shouldn't be resting, should be working-but it's muffled. Manageable.]

---

[Around midday, Nyx returns with food. Hands Avyanna a plate without asking if she's hungry.]

Nyx: [matter-of-fact] Eat. Your body needs fuel to recover.

[The food is simple. Protein, carbs, vegetables. Not fancy. Just functional nutrition.]

[Avyanna eats. Realizes she's actually hungry. That her body has needs beyond work. That meeting those needs is allowed.]

Nyx: [sitting beside her] How are you feeling?

Avyanna: [honest] Still tired. Less exhausted. Guilty.

Nyx: [nodding] The guilt is the mine's logic. It'll fade as you practice resting without punishment.

Avyanna: [quiet] How long does that take?

Nyx: [considering] Depends. Months, usually. You're retraining your nervous system to accept that rest is safe. That you're allowed to have limits. That you won't be discarded for being human.

---

[Avyanna finishes eating. Nyx takes the plate.]

Nyx: [before leaving] You're doing well. Taking a recovery day when you need it is maturity, not weakness. Remember that.

[They leave. Avyanna sits with Rho and Jalen. Returns to her trash fiction. Lets her brain float in the ridiculous plot about sentient asteroids and improbable romance.]

[Evening comes. She's still tired. But the exhaustion is less sharp. More like normal tired, less like existential depletion.]

[She returns to her quarters. Her plant is there. Alive. Thriving despite her taking a day off from everything.]

(I rested. I did nothing productive. The crew didn't abandon me. The ship didn't fall apart. I'm still here. Still crew.)

[The shard at the base of her skull is warm.]

(The hum at the base of her skull softens, as if agreeing with the concept: rest is valid; rest is safe.)

---

[She sleeps that night. Real sleep. Deep sleep. Recovery sleep.]

[Tomorrow, she'll train with Elia again. She'll be functional. Rested. Better.]

[But today, she rested. And that was enough.]
