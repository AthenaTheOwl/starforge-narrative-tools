# Chapter 14d: Bearing (The "Why")

> **[INTERSTITIAL - OCCURS POST-CH14]**
> *Trigger: Jalen refuses to teach "instinct" without ethics*
> *Purpose: Orin Vey's framework, carried forward*

---

[Jalen teaches like every mistake will kill someone.]

[Which is not paranoia.]

[It's experience, translated into a voice that never softens.]

[Avyanna sits in the nav alcove with her hands folded in her lap so she can't fidget them into a confession.]

Jalen: Names are politics.

Avyanna: So we don't use them.

Jalen: We use them when we need leverage.

Jalen: We use coordinates when we need truth.

[Avyanna nods.]

[Then she asks the question she's been holding like contraband.]

Avyanna: How do you know where to go.

Jalen: Bearing.

Avyanna: That's just a word.

Jalen: That's most of your problems.

[Avyanna's mouth twitches.]

Avyanna: Teach me, then.

[Jalen's gaze flicks to her, sharp.]

Jalen: You want the mechanics or the ethics.

Avyanna: [honest] Both.

[Jalen goes still.]

[Not suspicious.]

[...Relieved, maybe.]

---

## Intercut - Orin Vey (A Cockpit Rule)

[The cockpit is cramped.]

[The ship is cheap and too loud and alive in the way a thing is alive when it doesn't have enough redundancy.]

[Jalen is eight and small and furious about being alive at all.]

[Orin sits in the pilot seat like they were born there-hands steady, eyes bright with the kind of attention that makes routes behave.]

[Orin doesn't ask Jalen's name first.]

[Orin asks something else.]

Orin: Why do you want to learn this.

[Jalen's throat tightens.]

Jalen: So I don't die.

Orin: That's a reason.

Orin: Not a why.

Jalen: [spits it] So no one else dies because of me.

[Orin's mouth twitches.]

Orin: That's closer.

Orin: Bearing isn't about the route you take.

Orin: It's about knowing why you're taking it.

[Jalen glares.]

Jalen: That sounds like philosophy.

Orin: It's survival.

[Orin taps the nav display.]

[A route line flickers, then three alternatives bloom around it like options in a cruel game.]

Orin: Look.

[Jalen looks.]

[The alternatives make their stomach turn.]

Jalen: They're all bad.

Orin: Yes.

Orin: Bearing is choosing which bad you can live with.

Jalen: What if I choose wrong.

Orin: Then you witness it.

Jalen: That's not helpful.

Orin: It's the only thing that stops you from becoming a liar.

---

[Orin reaches over and adjusts the throttle with gentle precision.]

[They don't touch Jalen.]

[They don't force.]

[They speak like consent is part of piloting.]

Orin: You can refuse to fly.

Jalen: [instant] No.

Orin: You can refuse a route.

Jalen: [hesitates] That's not allowed.

Orin: That's a story.

Orin: Who enforces it.

[Jalen's mouth opens.]

[Closes.]

[Orin waits.]

Jalen: The client.

Orin: And who enforces the client.

[Jalen stares at the screen like it might answer.]

Orin: If no one can reach you, their enforcement is just fear.

Orin: Fear is real.

Orin: But it is not a law.

---

[Orin leans back.]

Orin: Here's the rule, then.

Orin: When you choose a route, say the why out loud.

Orin: If you can't say it, you don't understand it.

Orin: If you don't understand it, you don't get to pretend the consequences were weather.

[Jalen swallows.]

Jalen: Okay.

Orin: Good.

Orin: Now fly.

---

## Return - Nav Alcove

[Avyanna blinks back into the present with the strange certainty that Jalen didn't intend to give her that story.]

[But something in their eyes, when they said "Bearing," sounded like a memory with teeth.]

Avyanna: [quiet] It's about knowing why.

[Jalen's jaw flexes.]

Jalen: Don't quote things at me.

Avyanna: You just did.

[Jalen's mouth tightens.]

Jalen: [rough] Yeah.

Jalen: So.

Jalen: Why are you asking.

Avyanna: Because I don't want to be a liar.

[Jalen exhales like that answer made the room lighter by a millimeter.]

Jalen: Good.

Jalen: Then you're teachable.

---
*End Chapter 14d*
